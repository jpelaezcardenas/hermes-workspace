# Cover Letter

> **Target length:** 1 page
> **When to use:** First page of every RFP/RFI response. Always include unless the RFP explicitly says not to.
> **Tone:** Professional, confident, concise. Not salesy. Signed by a C-level executive.

Dear [Mr./Ms./Dr. Surname],

### Opening: Acknowledgment & Intent

[1–2 sentences. State that SettleMint is pleased to submit its response to the referenced RFP/RFI. Reference the specific document title and reference number. Express genuine interest in the opportunity.]

> **Example:** "SettleMint is pleased to submit this proposal in response to [Organization Name]'s Request for Proposal [Reference Number] for [brief description of what's being procured]. We welcome the opportunity to demonstrate how our Digital Asset Lifecycle Platform (DALP) addresses your requirements."

### Body: Why SettleMint

[2–3 short paragraphs covering:]

**Paragraph 1. Understanding of the requirement:**
[Show that you understand what the client needs and why. Reference specific challenges or objectives mentioned in the RFP. Frame the core challenge around the complexity of doing tokenization right at production scale, not the technology itself, but the regulatory, governance, lifecycle, and infrastructure requirements. This is NOT a product pitch, it's proof that you read and understood their document.]

**Paragraph 2. Key differentiators (3–4 bullet points):**
[Highlight the most compelling reasons to choose SettleMint for THIS engagement. Select from:]

- [Solves the complexity of doing digital assets right. DALP addresses the complexity of institutional-grade tokenization, embedding compliance and governance directly into the platform]
- [Platform maturity, production-proven with institutional clients across X jurisdictions]
- [Time to market, typical 12–16 week implementation vs. 12–18 month custom build]
- [Regulatory alignment, built for regulated financial services, [specific regulation] compliant]
- [Technology leadership, [specific relevant capability, e.g., multi-chain support, compliance engine]]
- [Relevant experience, reference most relevant case study without going into detail]
- [Total cost of ownership, platform licensing vs. ongoing custom development costs]

> **Guidance:** Choose 3–4 bullets most relevant to THIS client. Don't use all of them. Quality over quantity.

**Paragraph 3. Commitment:**
[Brief statement of SettleMint's commitment to this client's success. Mention willingness to present, demonstrate, or discuss further. Keep it genuine, no empty promises.]

### Closing: Contact & Call to Action

[Name the primary contact for questions about this submission. Provide direct contact details.]

We look forward to discussing this proposal further and demonstrating how DALP can [achieve client's stated objective].

> **Final checklist:**
> - [ ] Correct addressee name, title, and organization, triple-check spelling
> - [ ] Correct RFP/RFI reference number
> - [ ] Signatory approved and available to sign (physical or electronic)
> - [ ] Keep to ONE PAGE, this is a cover letter, not an executive summary
> - [ ] Differentiators are tailored to THIS client, not generic
> - [ ] Enclosures list matches what's actually in the submission package
> - [ ] Date is the submission date, not the draft date
> - [ ] Professional tone, confident but not arrogant
