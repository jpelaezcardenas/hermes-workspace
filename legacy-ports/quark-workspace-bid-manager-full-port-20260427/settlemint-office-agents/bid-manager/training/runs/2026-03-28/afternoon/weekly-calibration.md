## Key Findings

### 1. L2 average (44.9) exceeds the target band ceiling

The calibration rule states: "If average > 42/50 for 5 consecutive reviews: tighten scoring standards." Week 2 produced nine consecutive L2 scores above 42, with five above 44. This triggers the tightening threshold.

**However**, the L1 average (39.0) is barely inside the target band. The improvement is concentrated in the rewrite, not the first draft. Tightening scoring across the board risks making L1 scores discouraging without addressing the actual gap. The better calibration response is targeted: raise expectations specifically in the three persistent weak categories (Client Centricity, Visual, Competitive Edge) while keeping other category standards stable.

### 2. L1 improvement target (38+) nearly met

Seven of nine L1 scores met or exceeded 38. The two exceptions are both content refreshes (CR 1-2 at 33, CR 5-6 and CR 7-8 at 38). The 33 for CR 1-2 on Monday is a likely warm-up effect after the weekend. Overall, the Week 1 action item to raise L1 above 38 is largely working.

### 3. MR-6 set a new high-water mark (47/50)

The PE fund tokenization response scored the highest of any exercise across both weeks. The combination of a well-specified scenario, honest handling of waterfall calculation limitations, and strong client framing produced a response that may be reference-quality for fund tokenization RFPs.

### 4. The rewrite loop reliably improves scores, but the improvement ceiling may be approaching

Average delta held steady at 5.9 (vs 5.8 in Week 1), but L2 scores are clustering around 44-46. Pushing L2 beyond 46 consistently will require improvements that the single-reviewer loop may not catch, such as competitive positioning depth, evaluator persona stress-testing, and format innovation.

### 5. Client Centricity is the hardest habit to change

Despite being identified as a priority in Week 1 calibration and targeted with "open every section with a client-value sentence," L1 Client Centricity dropped from 3.0 to 2.8. The rewrite consistently fixes it (L2: 4.0, up from 3.7), but the drafting default has not shifted. This suggests a structural intervention is needed, not just a reminder.

## Calibration Decisions for Week 3

### Scoring adjustment
Raise the floor expectation for three categories in both L1 and L2 scoring:
- **Client Centricity:** L1 must show at least one client-outcome sentence per section to score 3. L2 must show consistent client framing throughout to score 4.
- **Visual / Screenshots:** L1 must include at least 2 screenshot references to score 3. L2 must include 3+ with descriptive captions to score 4.
- **Competitive Edge:** L1 must include at least one differentiation argument per major section to score 3. L2 must connect DALP's architecture to competitive advantages specifically.

Other categories: keep current standards. Overall target band remains 38-44 for now. If L2 average exceeds 46 in Week 3, apply a full recalibration.

### Process improvements for Week 3
1. **Mandatory client-framing pre-step.** Before writing any section, complete one sentence: "The client needs this because ___." Write that sentence first, then build the section around it. This is no longer optional guidance; treat it as a required drafting step.
2. **Screenshot pre-selection checklist.** Before drafting, read the screenshot registry and select 3-5 screenshots mapped to specific sections. List them in a comment block at the top of the draft. The reviewer should check whether the listed screenshots were actually embedded.
3. **Competitive angle pre-check.** Before writing each section, identify one thing that a point-solution or custom-build approach would struggle with. Weave that contrast into the section. Do not name competitors; frame as category weakness vs. platform advantage.
4. **Rubric lock.** All exercises must use the 10-category, 5-point rubric. No exceptions. If a reviewer spawns with a different scale, the exercise must be re-scored before the score enters the scorecard.

### Content improvements to carry forward
- MR-6 PE fund tokenization response (47/50) is the strongest exercise output to date. Extract its honesty framing patterns as templates for future fund-related RFPs.
- MR-3 CSD digital twin response (46/50) demonstrates strong handling of hybrid on-chain/off-chain architecture questions. Its reconciliation framing is reusable.
- AC-6 structured products deep-dive (46/50) covers an asset class with no existing content section. Queue for `content/` directory creation pending Gyan approval.
- AC-3 real estate deep-dive (45/50) updates the real estate content with UAE/ADGM regulatory context that is not in the current content sections.
