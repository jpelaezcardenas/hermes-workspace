# Request for Information

## Digital Platform for Sharia-Compliant Sukuk Issuance and Lifecycle Management

**Issuing Authority:** Gulf Development Bank (GDB)
**Document Reference:** GDB-RFI-2026-041
**Date of Issue:** 1 April 2026
**Response Deadline:** 30 April 2026
**Classification:** Confidential

---

## Background

Gulf Development Bank is a multilateral development institution headquartered in Manama, Bahrain, with a mandate to finance economic diversification and infrastructure projects across the six GCC member states. GDB holds an investment-grade credit rating (A/Stable) and has issued over USD 18 billion in conventional bonds through Euroclear and the Central Bank of Bahrain's Scripless Securities Settlement System.

GDB's Board of Directors has approved a strategic initiative to issue its first Sharia-compliant sukuk instruments on a digital platform. The programme targets an initial issuance size of USD 500 million, with the capacity to scale to USD 2 billion over three years. The instruments will be distributed to qualified institutional investors across the GCC, Southeast Asia, and select European jurisdictions (Luxembourg, United Kingdom, Germany).

GDB's Sharia Supervisory Board operates under AAOIFI (Accounting and Auditing Organization for Islamic Financial Institutions) standards and requires that all instruments, distribution mechanisms, and servicing operations conform to Sharia principles as interpreted by the Board.

## Objectives

GDB seeks a technology platform capable of supporting the following objectives:

1. Digital issuance of multiple sukuk structures on a single, unified platform
2. Lifecycle management from structuring through periodic distributions to maturity or dissolution
3. Regulatory compliance across multiple GCC and international jurisdictions simultaneously
4. Integration with GDB's existing treasury management system and banking infrastructure
5. Secondary market transfer capability with Sharia-compliant transfer validation
6. Full audit trail satisfying both AAOIFI and national regulatory requirements

## Scope of Information Requested

### Section A: Sukuk Structure Support

A.1. Describe how the platform supports the issuance of ijara (lease-based) sukuk, including the representation of the underlying leased asset, periodic rental payment distribution, and asset return at maturity.

A.2. Describe how the platform supports murabaha (cost-plus financing) sukuk, including deferred payment scheduling, profit margin calculation, and commodity reference mechanics.

A.3. Describe how the platform supports wakala (agency-based) sukuk, including the appointment and monitoring of a wakeel (agent), expected profit rate distribution, and incentive fee calculation.

A.4. Explain how the platform differentiates between profit-sharing distributions and interest payments at the smart contract level, ensuring that no interest-bearing mechanism is embedded in any transaction or data structure.

A.5. Describe how the platform tracks and verifies the underlying real assets backing each sukuk issuance, ensuring tangibility and Sharia-compliant asset-backing at all times during the instrument's lifecycle.

### Section B: Sharia Governance and Compliance

B.1. Describe the platform's mechanism for incorporating Sharia board approval workflows into the issuance and servicing process, including how approvals are recorded and made auditable.

B.2. Explain how the platform ensures compliance with AAOIFI Financial Accounting Standards (FAS 33, FAS 34) and Sharia Standards (SS 17 for Investment Sukuk, SS 9 for Ijara, SS 8 for Murabaha).

B.3. Describe how the platform enforces Sharia-compliant transfer rules during secondary market transactions, including the prohibition of trading debt-based sukuk below par (murabaha restrictions).

B.4. Describe the platform's approach to ensuring that underlying assets remain identified, Sharia-compliant, and unencumbered throughout the sukuk lifecycle, including periodic asset verification mechanisms.

### Section C: Distribution and Servicing

C.1. Describe how the platform calculates and distributes periodic profit payments to sukuk holders, including support for fixed-rate, floating-rate, and hybrid distribution schedules.

C.2. Explain the platform's mechanism for maturity redemption, including face value repayment, early dissolution triggers, and partial dissolution (amortizing structures).

C.3. Describe how distribution amounts are determined and executed when the platform is not performing the underlying profit/loss calculation itself.

C.4. Describe the settlement mechanism for primary issuance, including delivery-versus-payment or equivalent atomic settlement capabilities.

### Section D: Regulatory and Multi-Jurisdictional Compliance

D.1. Describe the platform's ability to enforce investor eligibility rules simultaneously across GCC jurisdictions (CBB, CMA Saudi Arabia, DFSA, ADGM, CMA Kuwait, CMA Oman) and international jurisdictions (FCA UK, CSSF Luxembourg, BaFin Germany).

D.2. Explain the platform's approach to identity verification and KYC for qualified institutional investors across these jurisdictions.

D.3. Describe how the platform generates or supports regulatory reporting aligned with the requirements of each relevant jurisdiction.

D.4. Describe the platform's data residency capabilities and how investor data can be segregated by jurisdiction if required by local regulation.

### Section E: Integration and Technical Architecture

E.1. Describe the platform's API architecture and how it integrates with external treasury management systems, core banking platforms, and Islamic banking core systems.

E.2. Describe the deployment options available (on-premises, cloud, hybrid) and the blockchain networks supported.

E.3. Describe the platform's approach to high availability, disaster recovery, and business continuity, including target uptime, RPO, and RTO.

E.4. Describe the security architecture, including authentication, authorization, encryption, and key management.

### Section F: Audit Trail and Reporting

F.1. Describe the audit trail capabilities of the platform, including what events are recorded, how they are stored, and how they can be accessed by internal audit, external auditors, and regulators.

F.2. Describe the reporting capabilities for sukuk holders, including periodic statements, distribution history, and current holdings.

F.3. Describe how the platform supports Sharia audit requirements, including the ability to demonstrate that all operations conformed to the approved Sharia structure throughout the lifecycle.

## Evaluation Criteria

Responses will be evaluated against the following criteria:

| Criterion | Weight |
| --- | --- |
| Functional coverage for sukuk lifecycle | 30% |
| Sharia governance and compliance capability | 25% |
| Technical architecture and integration readiness | 20% |
| Regulatory compliance across target jurisdictions | 15% |
| Implementation timeline and deployment flexibility | 10% |

## Response Format

Respondents should structure their response to follow the section order above (A through F). Supplementary materials, architecture diagrams, and platform screenshots demonstrating relevant capabilities are encouraged.
