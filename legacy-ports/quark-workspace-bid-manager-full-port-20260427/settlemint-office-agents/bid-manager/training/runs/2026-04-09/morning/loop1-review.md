# Loop 1 Review — Content Refresh Sections 7–8

**Date:** 2026-04-09 | **Exercise:** Content refresh: sections 7–8 (Week 4, Thursday)

## Scoring Rubric (10 categories × 5 points each = /50)

### 1. Executive Readability: 4/5
The commercial section opens clearly and the pricing philosophy is easy for mixed evaluators to follow. The weakness is density. Several subsections still read like an internal capability inventory rather than evaluator-guided commercial prose.

### 2. Technical Credibility: 4/5
The content is grounded in real DALP capabilities and avoids obvious technical overreach. However, some answers expose more implementation texture than an RFI bank needs, while other parts use broad labels without enough immediate proof.

### 3. Requirement Coverage: 4/5
Both target sections cover the expected topics. The main gap is uneven emphasis: some commercial differentiators are repeated, while a few evaluator-relevant points, especially integration effort reduction and settlement boundary clarity, are present but not consistently foregrounded.

### 4. Honesty / Boundaries: 5/5
Capability boundaries are generally sound. The content does not claim DALP replaces bank rails or guarantees legal compliance. Existing [TO VERIFY] placeholders remain properly marked.

### 5. Flow / Narrative Arc: 4/5
The sections are coherent, but not yet fully disciplined. Repetition around platform breadth and indexing ownership weakens the reading experience and makes the refresh feel additive rather than editorially tightened.

### 6. Writing Quality: 4/5
The prose is mostly strong, active, and specific. The main issue is that several paragraphs lead with mechanism before evaluator value, which makes them feel heavier than they need to.

### 7. Client Centricity: 3/5
This is the biggest scoring drag. Too many passages explain what DALP contains instead of what the institution gains. The evaluator should feel operational control, lower vendor dependency, lower integration burden, and clearer accountability earlier in each answer.

### 8. Visual / Screenshots: 4/5
No visual work was required for this specific content refresh, but the sections are at least structured in a way that would support diagrams and screenshots cleanly. Still, no new visual prompts were embedded into the content itself.

### 9. IP Protection: 4/5
The content is mostly safe, but some wording leans closer to internal implementation language than necessary. The next loop should keep the proof while stepping back from overly specific interface detail where it does not improve evaluator confidence.

### 10. Competitive Edge: 4/5
The non-metered commercial model and owned read layer are real differentiators. The content would score higher if those points were framed more directly as risk, cost, and governance advantages for the buyer.

## Total Score: 40/50

## Key Issues for Loop 2

1. Lead more often with evaluator value, not platform structure.
2. Reduce repetition across commercial and architecture answers.
3. Tighten IP-safe phrasing where implementation detail is unnecessary.
4. Make the settlement integration boundary more explicit and cleaner.
5. Reframe operational tooling as implementation-risk reduction rather than feature volume.
