# SettleMint Application for Participation in the PVARA Framework

## Executive Summary

SettleMint respectfully submits this application to participate in Pakistan's emerging virtual asset regulatory framework under PVARA.

SettleMint is a digital asset infrastructure company focused on regulated institutions, market infrastructures, and sovereign programmes. Its core platform, DALP (Digital Asset Lifecycle Platform), enables regulated organizations to design, issue, govern, service, and monitor digital assets across their lifecycle with strong compliance controls, identity-linked operations, auditable workflows, and integration with existing custody, payment, and enterprise systems.

SettleMint's objective in Pakistan is not to operate an unregulated retail exchange or speculative crypto business. The objective is to support compliant digital asset market development by providing regulated-grade infrastructure for tokenized instruments, investor controls, asset servicing, auditability, and operational governance. This includes support for regulated token issuance, controlled distribution, transfer restrictions, identity-linked eligibility, and lifecycle servicing for digital financial instruments and real-world assets.

Based on the public PVARA information available at the time of drafting, SettleMint understands that the current process begins with a No Objection Certificate application, followed by AML registration with the Financial Monitoring Unit, local incorporation, and then the applicable VASP licensing process. SettleMint is prepared to engage with PVARA to confirm the correct licensing perimeter for its intended role in Pakistan.

This application is therefore submitted on the assumption that PVARA is open to infrastructure-led participants whose operating model supports regulated issuance, investment platform functionality, and compliant virtual asset operations, subject to final classification by the Authority.

SettleMint seeks to contribute to Pakistan's regulated virtual asset ecosystem in one or more of the following roles, subject to PVARA's final classification and approval:

1. Technology and infrastructure provider for regulated digital asset programmes.
2. Platform operator for permissioned token issuance and controlled investor participation models.
3. Enabler for compliant tokenized financial instruments and real-world asset structures.
4. Infrastructure partner to licensed financial institutions, exchanges, custodians, and issuers entering the Pakistan market.

SettleMint would not seek to bypass Pakistan's regulatory framework. The operating principle is the opposite: work within the framework, confirm the correct regulatory perimeter early, and ensure that any launch is aligned to licensing, AML, governance, and consumer protection requirements.

## Why SettleMint Fits PVARA's Objectives

### 1. Regulatory-first operating model

PVARA's public materials emphasize supervision, AML and CFT alignment, market integrity, and consumer protection. SettleMint's platform design aligns with that direction because DALP is built for environments where policy enforcement, investor eligibility, transfer restrictions, and structured auditability are mandatory.

### 2. Strong compliance architecture

DALP supports configurable compliance modules covering identity verification, jurisdiction restrictions, allow and block lists, supply controls, holding periods, transfer approvals, and collateral constraints. These controls are designed to support regulated issuance and controlled distribution, rather than open, unmanaged token circulation.

### 3. Identity-linked operations

PVARA highlights KYC, recordkeeping, suspicious activity reporting, and accountable operations. DALP is built around verified identity and role-based governance. This supports investor eligibility checks, operational segregation of duties, and auditable lifecycle events.

### 4. Consumer protection and governance

PVARA's framework stresses security standards, safeguarding of customer interests, transparent practices, and accountability. DALP supports maker-checker approvals, role-based permissions, operational logs, policy templates, and integration with institutional custody and compliance processes.

### 5. Market development potential

Pakistan's framework is at an early but important stage. SettleMint can support the development of compliant digital asset use cases beyond simple exchange activity, including tokenized deposits, funds, bonds, sukuk, real estate structures, and other regulated digital instruments where lifecycle control matters.

## Proposed Pakistan Market Approach

SettleMint proposes a phased and compliant market entry approach.

### Phase 1, Regulatory alignment and application

- Confirm the correct licensing or participation category with PVARA.
- Submit the NOC application with corporate materials, business plan, and operating model description.
- Align the proposed Pakistan model to the Authority's definitions for issuer, investment platform, exchange, custody, or infrastructure participant.

### Phase 2, Local operating readiness

- Complete required AML and FMU registration steps through the approved local structure.
- Establish the local corporate vehicle if required.
- Define governance, customer protection, onboarding, and reporting processes for the Pakistan market.

### Phase 3, Controlled launch model

- Start with a permissioned, tightly governed use case.
- Focus on regulated issuance and controlled participant onboarding rather than broad retail exposure.
- Operate with clear policy restrictions, audit logs, and regulator-visible governance.

### Phase 4, Ecosystem expansion

- Extend to additional use cases once the initial regulated operating model is proven.
- Support banks, issuers, investment platforms, and tokenized asset programmes using the same governance and control stack.
