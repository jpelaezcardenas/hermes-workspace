# Investment Rationale

## Cost of Current Approach

Building tokenized payment rail infrastructure from scratch requires assembling deposit issuance, stablecoin management, AML integration, ISO 20022 payment connectivity, and operational monitoring from separate components. Multi-vendor assembly introduces SLA gaps and reconciliation overhead. The coordination cost compounds with each cross-border corridor added.

## Why DALP Changes Economics

DALP consolidates deposit management, stablecoin operations, payment rail connectivity (ISO 20022), compliance enforcement, and operational monitoring into one platform. The bridge functionality for cross-network settlement and the XvP atomic settlement model eliminate the per-corridor integration overhead of alternative approaches. Reuse of the same compliance engine across all payment types reduces ongoing compliance maintenance cost.

## ROI Framework

| ROI Driver | Without DALP | With DALP |
|---|---|---|
| Payment settlement | T+1 or longer | T+0, atomic XvP |
| Compliance operations | Manual screening | Automated ex-ante |
| Cross-border corridors | Per-corridor integration | Bridge + multi-chain |
| Reconciliation | Daily multi-system | Automated, atomic |
| Audit preparation | Manual assembly | Structured 534 error codes |
