FirstRand requires a platform and delivery partner capable of bringing a digital bond platform for wholesale and treasury issuance to production with the discipline expected of a core regulated system operating under SARB and FSCA oversight. This commercial proposal presents SettleMint's licensing, implementation, and support model for DALP, structured to provide predictable costs and clear total cost of ownership.

**Commercial recommendation:**

# Investment Rationale

## Cost of Current Approach

Internal development of wholesale bond infrastructure requires 18 to 24 months of custom development with a dedicated engineering team of 5 to 8 specialists. Multi-vendor assembly reduces development time but introduces integration overhead, SLA gaps, and permanent reconciliation costs. Both approaches carry significant hidden costs in vendor coordination, compliance maintenance, and audit preparation.

## Why DALP Changes Economics

DALP eliminates the integration tax by consolidating issuance, compliance, custody orchestration, settlement, and servicing in one platform. Included lifecycle capabilities remove the need for separate procurement of coupon calculation, maturity processing, and compliance enforcement. Reuse across asset classes means expanding from bonds to equities or deposits requires configuration, not new procurement. The predictable subscription model enables budget planning without volume-driven surprises.

## ROI Framework

| ROI Driver | Without DALP | With DALP |
|---|---|---|
| Time-to-market | 18 to 24 months | 19 weeks |
| Settlement | T+2 with reconciliation | T+0, deterministic |
| Compliance ops | Manual review | Automated ex-ante |
| Operational headcount | 5 to 8 specialists | 2 to 3 operators |
| Multi-vendor coordination | 3+ vendors | Single partner |
