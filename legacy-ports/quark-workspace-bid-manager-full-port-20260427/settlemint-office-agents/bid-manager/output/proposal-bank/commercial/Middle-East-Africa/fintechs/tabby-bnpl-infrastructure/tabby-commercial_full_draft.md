## 1. Executive Summary

## 2. Investment Rationale

### 2.1 Cost of Current Approach

The current approach to receivables funding involves manual processes, fragmented systems, and limited investor transparency. These constraints create hidden costs across multiple dimensions:

**Operational overhead**: Manual reconciliation across merchant systems, funding partners, and internal systems requires dedicated operations staff. Data inconsistencies between systems create exception-handling workarounds that consume additional resources.

**Investor limitation**: Limited transparency on pool composition and performance restricts investor appetite, resulting in higher funding costs or constrained capacity. The inability to demonstrate granular audit trails limits participation from institutional investors with compliance requirements.

**Regulatory compliance cost**: Manual evidence production for regulatory reviews consumes compliance team time. Ad hoc reporting approaches require repeated effort for each regulatory request rather than automated generation from authoritative data.

**Scale constraints**: Current processes constrain scaling as volume increases. Each new merchant onboarding requires manual integration effort. Funding pool management relies on spreadsheet-based calculations that become error-prone at volume.

The platform addresses these constraints through automation, integration, and audit capabilities that collapse the hidden cost structure while enabling scale.

### 2.2 DALP Economic Impact

DALP changes the economics by providing a unified platform for asset lifecycle management, investor reporting, and regulatory compliance.

| Cost Driver | Current State | DALP Impact | Mechanism |
|-------------|---------------|-------------|-----------|
| Operations staff | 4-6 FTE for reconciliation | 1-2 FTE reduction | Automated reconciliation |
| Investor onboarding | 2-3 weeks per investor | 2-3 days | Self-service portal |
| Regulatory reporting | 40+ hours per report | 2-3 hours | Automated generation |
| Exception handling | 15-20% of volume | <5% | Real-time validation |
| Merchant onboarding | 4-6 weeks | 1-2 weeks | API-first integration |

The platform enables approximately 60-70% reduction in operational overhead for receivables management, with the percentage varying based on current process maturity and volume profile.

### 2.3 ROI Framework

Return on investment combines multiple value drivers:

**Cost avoidance**: The platform avoids costs that would be incurred through alternative approaches, including build costs, integration costs, and compliance costs associated with assembling point solutions.

**Operational efficiency**: Direct labor savings from automation and process improvement translate to cost reduction that compounds annually.

**Revenue enablement**: Improved investor transparency enables access to larger investor pools, potentially reducing funding costs by 50-100 basis points on applicable volumes.

**Risk reduction**: Improved audit trails and compliance evidence reduce regulatory risk and remediation costs.

The ROI calculation depends on assumptions about current cost structure, transaction volumes, and funding scale. Detailed ROI modeling is available through commercial validation workshops.

## 3. Commercial Model

### 3.1 Licensing Structure

The platform operates on an annual subscription model with tiered options:

| Tier | Description | Included Elements |
|------|-------------|-------------------|
| Foundation | Core platform capabilities | Asset lifecycle, compliance modules, API access |
| Professional | Enhanced capabilities | Advanced reporting, analytics, additional integrations |
| Enterprise | Full capabilities | Dedicated environment, custom development, premium support |

For Tabby's requirements, the Professional tier is recommended, providing the capability scope required for BNPL tokenization with appropriate integration and reporting capabilities.

### 3.2 Pricing Components

**Platform Subscription** (Annual)

- Environment: Production plus DR
- Users: Unlimited business users
- API calls: 100,000/month included
- Support: Standard business hours

**Additional Components**

- Additional API calls: Tiered pricing per 10,000 calls
- Additional environments: Dev, test, UAT available
- Extended support: 24/7 coverage available
- Premium support: Dedicated support resources available

### 3.3 Volume Economics

Transaction processing pricing provides economies of scale:

| Volume Tier | Price Per Transaction |
|-------------|----------------------|
| First 50,000 | $0.15 |
| 50,001 - 200,000 | $0.12 |
| 200,001 - 500,000 | $0.10 |
| Above 500,000 | $0.08 |

Volume pricing applies to asset lifecycle events including creation, state changes, and transfers. The volume tier structure enables cost reduction as platform adoption increases.

### 4.1 Implementation Scope

The implementation investment covers the five workstreams defined in Tabby's RFP:

| Workstream | Deliverables | Investment |
|------------|---------------|-------------|
| WS-01 Mobilisation | Programme setup, governance, RAID | Included in implementation |

### 4.2 Investment Structure

Implementation investment is structured as fixed-price with milestone payments:

| Milestone | Payment Schedule | Amount |
|-----------|-----------------|--------|
| Contract signature | 20% | Upon contract signature |
| Design approval | 20% | Upon design sign-off |
| Development complete | 30% | Upon code completion |
| UAT go-live | 20% | Upon UAT entry |
| Production go-live | 10% | Upon production go-live |

The milestone structure provides risk mitigation through stage-gated payments that align payment with demonstrated delivery.

### 4.3 Investment Assumptions

The implementation investment assumes:

- Standard scope as defined in RFP
- Tabby provides dedicated project team (4-6 resources)
- Tabby provides timely access to systems and stakeholders
- No significant scope changes
- Remote delivery model with quarterly on-site workshops

Assumptions that prove incorrect may result in change requests with associated cost impact.

## 5. Ongoing Investment

### 5.1 Annual Operating Cost

Platform operation involves recurring costs:

**Platform Subscription**: Annual license fee as defined in Section 3.2

**Support Services**: Annual support retainer providing:

- Access to support team during business hours
- Incident response and escalation
- Access to knowledge base and documentation
- Quarterly platform updates

**Infrastructure Costs**: Direct costs for cloud infrastructure, paid to cloud provider, estimated based on deployment configuration

### 5.2 Cost Progression

| Year | Platform | Support | Infrastructure | Total |
|------|----------|---------|---------------|-------|
| Year 1 (partial) | Prorated | Included | Estimated | Launch + ongoing |
| Year 2 | Full | Full | Full | Full operating |
| Year 3 | Full | Full | Full | Full operating |

Year 1 reflects partial-year operation based on implementation timeline.

### 5.3 Cost Predictability

The commercial model emphasizes predictability:

- Platform subscription is fixed regardless of transaction volume within tier
- Support is fixed regardless of incident volume within normal ranges
- Infrastructure scales predictably based on environment configuration

Hidden cost escalation is avoided through defined cost boundaries and scope management.

## 6. Value Analysis

### 6.1 Buy vs. Build Comparison

Building equivalent capability internally would require:

| Component | Build Cost Estimate | DALP Comparison |
|-----------|---------------------|-----------------|
| Platform development | 18-24 months | Available immediately |
| Integration development | 6-9 months | Pre-built adapters |
| Compliance modules | 3-6 months | Pre-certified |
| Operational capability | 12-18 months | Production-ready |
| Total estimated build | 39-57 months | Significant acceleration |

The platform approach provides time-to-value measured in weeks rather than years, with reduced execution risk and proven capability.

### 6.2 Single-Platform Economics

Using DALP for tokenized BNPL creates optionality for adjacent capabilities:

**Future extension potential**: The platform supports additional asset types, markets, and use cases without additional platform investment. This creates incremental value as Tabby's digital asset strategy evolves.

**Operational leverage**: The operational model, support infrastructure, and team capability transfer across use cases, reducing incremental operating cost for additional programmes.

**Vendor consolidation**: Single platform for digital asset lifecycle reduces vendor management overhead, integration complexity, and support coordination.

### 6.3 Risk-Adjusted Value

The proposal value analysis incorporates risk adjustment:

| Factor | Impact | Mitigation |
|--------|--------|------------|
| Implementation overrun | Potential 10-20% | Milestone structure |
| Integration complexity | Potential delays | Early integration scoping |
| Regulatory change | Unknown | Configuration flexibility |
| Volume variance | Cost impact | Tiered pricing |

The risk-adjusted value considers probability-weighted outcomes across scenarios.
