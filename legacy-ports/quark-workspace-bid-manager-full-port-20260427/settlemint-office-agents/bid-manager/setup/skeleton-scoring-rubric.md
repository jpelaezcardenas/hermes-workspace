# Skeleton Scoring Rubric

## Purpose
This rubric evaluates proposal skeletons as structural blueprints. It does **not** score writing quality of finished proposal prose. It scores whether the skeleton gives a writer enough structure, instructions, evidence direction, and hierarchy to produce a high-quality proposal.

## Total score: 100 points

| Dimension | Points | What It Measures |
|---|---:|---|
| Structural completeness | 20 | Does every section and subsection exist? Are there no major gaps? |
| Word count guidance | 15 | Does every section specify target word count? Are they realistic? |
| Visual element specification | 15 | Are diagrams and tables specified with type, content, and placement? |
| Content source references | 15 | Does every section point to specific source files? |
| Hierarchy clarity | 10 | Is the heading/subheading structure clear and consistent? |
| Instruction quality | 10 | Are the write-about instructions specific enough to follow? |
| Flow and ordering | 10 | Do sections follow logical progression? |
| Reusability | 5 | Can this skeleton be adapted for different clients without restructuring? |

## Performance bands

### 0-20% of available points for a dimension
Very weak. The dimension is largely missing or unusable.
- Sections absent or severely incomplete
- Word counts mostly missing
- Visuals vague or omitted
- References non-specific or absent
- Headings inconsistent
- Instructions too vague to execute
- Order feels arbitrary
- Highly one-off and not reusable

### 20-40%
Weak. Some structure exists, but writers would still guess heavily.
- Major sections exist but important subsections missing
- Length guidance partial or unrealistic
- Some visuals named, but under-specified
- References point to broad folders rather than exact sources
- Hierarchy partly consistent
- Instructions generic
- Flow acceptable but clumsy
- Reuse possible only with significant restructuring

### 40-60%
Adequate baseline. Usable with reviewer intervention.
- Most sections present, but some structural gaps remain
- Word counts available for many but not all sections
- Visuals exist for some major sections only
- References present but uneven in specificity
- Hierarchy mostly clear
- Instructions moderately useful, but still open to interpretation
- Flow mostly logical
- Reusable for similar bids with moderate work

### 60-80%
Strong. Writers can work from it with limited clarification.
- Nearly all required sections and subsections present
- Word counts consistent, with minor gaps only
- Visuals clearly defined in major sections
- References specific and generally actionable
- Hierarchy consistent and easy to review
- Instructions concrete and tailored
- Flow strong, with only minor sequencing issues
- Reusable across multiple bid contexts with small edits

### 80-100%
Excellent. Review-ready skeleton that materially reduces drafting ambiguity.
- Complete, coherent section architecture with no obvious gaps
- Word counts realistic and comprehensive at section and subsection level
- Visual specs explicit, useful, and well placed
- References specific enough to route fact-finding directly
- Clean hierarchy with consistent numbering and nesting
- Instructions precise, outcome-oriented, and easy to assign
- Flow mirrors evaluator logic and proposal reading rhythm
- Highly reusable across client variations without structural change

## Dimension-specific scoring guidance

### 1. Structural completeness: 20 points
**0-4:** Many missing sections; not proposal-ready.
**5-8:** Basic sections present, but major proof or delivery blocks missing.
**9-12:** Core structure exists; some appendices or supporting sections missing.
**13-16:** Almost complete; only minor structural gaps.
**17-20:** Fully complete for its document type with clear mandatory/optional sections.

### 2. Word count guidance: 15 points
**0-3:** Little or no length guidance.
**4-6:** Length guidance only at top level.
**7-9:** Most major sections have ranges; subsections inconsistent.
**10-12:** Nearly all sections and subsections have realistic ranges.
**13-15:** Comprehensive, realistic, and useful page/word guidance throughout.

### 3. Visual element specification: 15 points
**0-3:** Visuals largely absent or noted vaguely.
**4-6:** Some sections mention diagrams/tables but with poor definition.
**7-9:** Major visuals defined, though some lack row/column/content guidance.
**10-12:** Most visuals clearly specified with type and purpose.
**13-15:** Visual plan is explicit, section-specific, and directly supports evaluation.

### 4. Content source references: 15 points
**0-3:** No meaningful references.
**4-6:** References broad folders or generic docs only.
**7-9:** Major sections have references but some are vague.
**10-12:** Most sections cite specific templates, docs, or files.
**13-15:** Every major section and most subsections point to specific, actionable sources.

### 5. Hierarchy clarity: 10 points
**0-2:** Heading structure confusing or inconsistent.
**3-4:** Understandable but uneven numbering or nesting.
**5-6:** Generally clear, with occasional inconsistencies.
**7-8:** Consistent section logic and numbering.
**9-10:** Clean, rigorous, review-friendly hierarchy throughout.

### 6. Instruction quality: 10 points
**0-2:** Vague prompts like `write about X`.
**3-4:** Some useful direction, but too generic.
**5-6:** Adequate specificity for many sections.
**7-8:** Strong instructions with clear scope, required coverage, and tone.
**9-10:** Highly actionable instructions that minimize interpretation risk.

### 7. Flow and ordering: 10 points
**0-2:** Sequence feels random or repetitive.
**3-4:** Basic order exists, but evaluator logic is weak.
**5-6:** Generally logical, with some awkward transitions.
**7-8:** Strong ordering that supports narrative and scoring flow.
**9-10:** Excellent evaluator-first flow from context to proof to delivery to commercials.

### 8. Reusability: 5 points
**0-1:** One-off structure; hard to reuse.
**2:** Reusable only within a very narrow scenario.
**3:** Reusable across similar bids with moderate editing.
**4:** Reusable across multiple client variants with minor tuning.
**5:** Highly reusable across the target proposal family without structural redesign.

## Score interpretation
| Total Score | Meaning |
|---|---|
| 95-100 | Review-ready skeleton; can be issued to writers immediately |
| 90-94 | Strong skeleton; minor refinements recommended |
| 80-89 | Usable but still missing important structural detail |
| 70-79 | Needs revision before drafting begins |
| Below 70 | Not acceptable as a production skeleton |

## Review method
For each skeleton:
1. Score each dimension separately.
2. Write 3-5 bullet observations on gaps.
3. Revise the skeleton.
4. Re-score.
5. Do not finalize below 95.
