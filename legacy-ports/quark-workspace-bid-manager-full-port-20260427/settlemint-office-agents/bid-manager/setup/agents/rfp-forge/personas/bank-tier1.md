# Buyer Persona: Global Tier-1 Bank

**Archetype:** JP Morgan, HSBC, Standard Chartered, BNP Paribas, Deutsche Bank, Citi, Goldman Sachs scale.

## Profile

A globally systemically important bank (G-SIB) with $1T+ in assets, operations in 40+ countries, and multiple regulated entities across jurisdictions. Has existing digital asset exploration (innovation lab, pilot projects) but is now moving toward production-grade infrastructure. Reports to multiple regulators simultaneously (Fed, PRA, ECB, MAS, HKMA). Board has approved a digital assets strategy, now procurement must execute.

## Priorities

1. **Regulatory compliance above all**: the solution must support multi-jurisdictional regulatory reporting from day one, not "on the roadmap"
2. **Integration with existing infrastructure**: core banking (Temenos, FIS, Finastra), SWIFT, T2S/T2, CLS, existing custody platforms. Rip-and-replace is not an option.
3. **Vendor stability and longevity**: will this company exist in 5 years? Financial statements, investor backing, and business continuity plans are scrutinized heavily
4. **Enterprise-grade security**: HSM-backed key management, SOC 2 Type II, ISO 27001, penetration testing cadence, zero-trust architecture
5. **Scale**: must handle millions of transactions, thousands of concurrent users, multi-entity deployment

## Evaluation Culture

Formal, committee-driven. Evaluation panels include business, technology, risk, compliance, legal, and procurement representatives. Decisions take 6–12 months. Proof of concept is standard before final selection. Reference calls with comparable institutions are mandatory. Pricing is evaluated on 5-year TCO, not headline license fee.

## Red Lines

- No vendor without audited financials or credible investor backing
- No cloud-only solutions without private deployment option
- No platforms without SOC 2 Type II and ISO 27001
- No solutions that cannot demonstrate production deployments at comparable scale

## Common RFP Patterns

60–80 page RFPs. Heavy on non-functional requirements. Separate security questionnaire (200+ questions). Mandatory vendor presentations with live demo. Two-stage evaluation: technical shortlist then commercial negotiation. Strict NDA before RFP release.
