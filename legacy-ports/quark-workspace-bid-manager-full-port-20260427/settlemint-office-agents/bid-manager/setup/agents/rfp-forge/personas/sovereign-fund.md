# Buyer Persona: Sovereign Wealth Fund / Government Investment Arm

**Archetype:** ADQ (Abu Dhabi), PIF (Saudi Arabia), GIC (Singapore), Mubadala, ADIA, Temasek, or a government-backed financial infrastructure development entity.

## Profile

A sovereign wealth fund or government investment arm with $100B+ AUM, operating as part of a national economic diversification strategy (Vision 2030, UAE Economic Vision, Singapore Smart Nation). May be procuring infrastructure for its own portfolio operations, or building national-level financial market infrastructure as a government mandate. Combines patient capital with strategic urgency, timelines are set by national vision plans, not quarterly earnings.

## Priorities

1. **National strategy alignment**: the solution must fit within broader economic vision mandates. "How does this advance our country's financial market development?" is the first question.
2. **Infrastructure-grade reliability**: this is national infrastructure. Uptime, resilience, and disaster recovery requirements are extreme. 99.99%+ availability, multi-region failover, zero data loss.
3. **Sovereignty and control**: strong preference for on-premises or private cloud deployment within national borders. Data residency is non-negotiable. Source code escrow or access may be required.
4. **Scalability to national level**: must handle the volume of an entire national market, not just one institution. Think millions of retail investors, not thousands of institutional users.
5. **Vendor commitment to the region**: local entity, local team, local support. "We'll fly people in from London" is not acceptable for a national infrastructure program.

## Evaluation Culture

Government-influenced procurement with formal governance. May use international procurement advisors (Big 4, McKinsey, Oliver Wyman). Evaluation panels include ministry representatives, financial market regulators, and technical experts. Decisions can take 6–18 months but once committed, budgets are substantial and long-term. Political alignment matters, vendor's country of origin and geopolitical positioning may be a factor.

## Red Lines

- No solutions without data residency within the sovereign territory
- No vendors without willingness to establish local entity and team
- No platforms that can't scale to national population levels
- No cloud dependencies on foreign hyperscalers without local availability zones

## Common RFP Patterns

40–60 page RFPs, often drafted with advisory firm support. Heavy on strategic alignment sections. Include national security and data sovereignty requirements. May require technology transfer or local capability building as part of the contract. Reference visits to comparable national-level deployments are standard.
