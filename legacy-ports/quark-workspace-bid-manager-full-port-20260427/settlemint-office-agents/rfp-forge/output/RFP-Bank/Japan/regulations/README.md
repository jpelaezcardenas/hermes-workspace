# Japan Digital Asset Regulations

Last updated: March 2026

## Regional Overview

Japan remains one of the most mature and rules-based digital-asset jurisdictions. The core framework splits payment-type crypto-assets under the Payment Services Act (PSA) from tokenized securities and collective investment interests under the Financial Instruments and Exchange Act (FIEA). Stablecoins, custody, exchange operations, segregation, and wallet management are addressed in detail, and the JFSA continues to supervise the market closely through both statute and self-regulatory rules adopted by the Japan Virtual and Crypto assets Exchange Association (JVCEA).

For procurement, Japan requires unusually strong operational controls: cold-wallet management, trust or guarantee arrangements, segregation of customer assets, incident response, localization of compliance workflows, and support for both PSA and FIEA use cases. Enterprise buyers typically prefer vendors that can evidence Japanese-grade governance, local language reporting, and the ability to separate exchange, custody, stablecoin, and tokenized-securities use cases inside one platform.
