# Bahrain

Last updated: March 2026

**Country:** Bahrain
**Regulatory Authority:** Central Bank of Bahrain (CBB)

## Key Regulations

### CBB Rulebook Volume 6 - Crypto-Asset Module
- **Date enacted / effective:** Introduced 2019 and updated over time
- **Summary:** Bahrain created a dedicated licensing module for crypto-asset services covering trading platforms, custody, brokerage, and related activities. The framework is explicit and bank-grade, giving enterprises more certainty than in many larger markets.
- **Scope:** Crypto-asset services, exchanges, custody, brokerage, governance.

### AML/CFT and high-risk customer controls
- **Date enacted / effective:** Ongoing
- **Summary:** The CBB pairs licensing with strong AML/CFT expectations, including customer due diligence, sanctions screening, transaction monitoring, and governance over higher-risk customers and jurisdictions.
- **Scope:** AML/KYC, monitoring, sanctions, risk management.

### Open banking / fintech integration context
- **Date enacted / effective:** Ongoing
- **Summary:** Bahrain's broader fintech agenda means digital-asset services are often assessed in the context of existing regulated financial infrastructure. For enterprise projects, integration discipline matters as much as legal licensing.
- **Scope:** Financial-infrastructure integration, fintech supervision, institutional adoption.

## Licensing Requirements
A CBB licence is required for covered crypto-asset services. Depending on structure, additional financial-services permissions or partnership models with licensed institutions may be needed.

## Taxonomy
Bahrain uses a practical crypto-asset service taxonomy tied to licensing activity, while traditional securities and payment products remain under their respective regulatory perimeters.

## Key Compliance Requirements
AML/KYC, sanctions, client-asset safeguarding, governance, cybersecurity, incident reporting, and prudential controls are central. Bahrain buyers often expect bank-grade operational risk documentation.

## Recent Developments
2024-2025 has been characterized by continued supervisory maturity rather than wholesale legislative change. Bahrain remains a credible institutional location for regulated crypto and tokenization businesses.

## Impact on RFP/Procurement
Bahrain procurement rewards vendors with strong institutional documentation, clear control ownership, and integration readiness with licensed financial institutions. A flashy product without regulator-facing substance will not travel well here.
