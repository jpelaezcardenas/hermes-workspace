# Switzerland

Last updated: March 2026

**Country:** Switzerland
**Regulatory Authority:** FINMA

## Key Regulations

### FINMA ICO / token classification guidance
- **Date enacted / effective:** Initial guidance 2018; updated over time
- **Summary:** Switzerland pioneered a practical token taxonomy that remains influential: payment tokens, utility tokens, and asset tokens. The classification drives AML treatment, prospectus questions, and whether banking, securities, or collective-investment rules apply.
- **Scope:** Token issuance, classification, AML and securities-law analysis.

### DLT Act / amendments to FMIA and related laws
- **Date enacted / effective:** Main reforms effective 2021
- **Summary:** The DLT Act modernized Swiss private, insolvency, and financial-market law to recognize ledger-based securities and DLT trading facilities. It is one of the clearest legal frameworks for enterprise tokenization of regulated financial instruments.
- **Scope:** Ledger-based securities, trading venues, bankruptcy segregation, tokenized capital markets.

### AMLA / financial institutions rules
- **Date enacted / effective:** Ongoing
- **Summary:** Even where a token is not a security, Swiss AML rules apply to payment-token style activity and financial intermediation. FINMA expects clear AML/KYC, monitoring, custody, and organizational controls.
- **Scope:** AML, custody, exchange, intermediation.

## Licensing Requirements
Licensing depends on activity: fintech/banking, securities firm, DLT trading facility, or financial intermediary registration through an SRO/FINMA pathway. There is no single omnibus crypto licence; firms must map activities to the applicable Swiss financial-services perimeter.

## Taxonomy
Switzerland classifies tokens as payment, utility, or asset tokens, with hybrids possible. Asset tokens typically resemble securities or claims, while payment tokens trigger AML focus and utility tokens may fall out of securities law if genuinely consumptive at issuance.

## Key Compliance Requirements
AML/KYC, beneficial-owner checks, custody controls, segregation of client assets, incident management, outsourcing governance, and legal analysis for token rights are central. Swiss enterprise buyers also care about private-key governance and bankruptcy remoteness.

## Recent Developments
Switzerland continued refining tokenized-securities and DLT trading-facility practice during 2024-2025. It remains one of the most enterprise-friendly environments for tokenized bonds, funds, and structured products rather than just spot crypto.

## Impact on RFP/Procurement
For procurement, Switzerland favours vendors that support ledger-based securities, formal custody/segregation controls, and legal configurability around token rights. Buyers often expect institutional-grade registry and lifecycle servicing features, not just wallet and exchange functions.
