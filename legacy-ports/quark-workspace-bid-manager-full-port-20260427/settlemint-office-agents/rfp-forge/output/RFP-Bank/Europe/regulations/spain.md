# Spain

Last updated: March 2026

**Country:** Spain  
**Regulatory Authority:** CNMV, Bank of Spain, and SEPBLAC

## Key Regulations

### CNMV Circular 1/2022 on crypto-asset advertising
- **Date enacted / effective:** Effective 2022
- **Summary:** Spain introduced one of Europe's most visible pre-MiCA crypto advertising regimes. The Circular requires risk warnings and gives CNMV oversight over mass campaigns, making marketing governance a serious operational issue.
- **Scope:** Advertising of crypto-assets to the public, risk warnings, and campaign controls.

### Markets in Crypto-Assets Regulation (MiCA) - Regulation (EU) 2023/1114
- **Date enacted / effective:** Entered into force 29 June 2023; fully applicable 30 December 2024
- **Summary:** MiCA is now the main rulebook for crypto-asset issuance and services in Spain. CNMV's MiCA portal explains that CASPs must be authorized by CNMV or another competent EU authority and that MiCA introduces investor-protection, disclosure, and conduct requirements across the EU market.
- **Scope:** CASP licensing, white papers, ARTs, EMTs, public offerings, trading admission, and conduct obligations.

### Spanish transitional regime linked to Bank of Spain registration and pre-existing providers
- **Date enacted / effective:** Transition running until 1 July 2026
- **Summary:** CNMV states that, until 1 July 2026, some providers may continue operating without a MiCA license under the transitional regime. Providers offering purchase, sale, and custody services must have been registered with the Bank of Spain, while other providers already active before 30 December 2024 may continue under transition subject to the applicable conditions.
- **Scope:** Transitional continuation, registration legacy, and market-entry timing.

## Licensing Requirements
CASPs serving Spain need authorization from CNMV or another competent EU authority with passporting rights. Spain's transition period is unusually important in procurement because providers can still be active during the changeover, but after 1 July 2026 only properly authorized providers should remain in scope. Separate analysis is still required for securities, e-money, and payment products.

## Taxonomy
Spain follows MiCA's categories for ARTs, EMTs, and other crypto-assets, while carving out MiFID II financial instruments, e-money structures, certain NFTs, limited-network tokens, and public-sector issues. Consumer-facing framing does not displace classification.

## Key Compliance Requirements
The compliance burden in Spain is not just AML. Buyers will expect controls for AML/KYC, sanctions, travel rule, white-paper governance, investor disclosures, complaints handling, marketing approvals, and warning-language management. That combination of conduct and communications controls is a Spanish differentiator.

## Recent Developments
- **19 December 2024:** CNMV published an investor communication on the start of MiCA application and linked it to ESMA's crypto-asset risk warning.
- **30 December 2024:** Full MiCA application began across the EU, including Spain.
- **23 December 2025:** CNMV introduced a new notification procedure for Title II crypto-asset white papers under MiCA.
- **2025-2026:** CNMV has continued investor-facing warnings that, despite MiCA, crypto-assets remain risky and that investors should verify whether providers are authorized, transitional, or outside the EU.
- **Until 1 July 2026:** Spain is operating a transitional window for certain previously active providers, after which MiCA authorization becomes the live gate.

## Impact on RFP/Procurement
Spanish buyers will probe investor communication, disclosure, and authorization status harder than many peers. A vendor that can support warning banners, white-paper notifications, approval workflows, and auditable localization will score better than one that only offers technical token functionality.
