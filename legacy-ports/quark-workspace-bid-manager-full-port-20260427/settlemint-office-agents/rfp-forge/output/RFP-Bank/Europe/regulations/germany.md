# Germany

Last updated: March 2026

**Country:** Germany
**Regulatory Authority:** BaFin

## Key Regulations

### MiCA and German implementation/supervision
- **Date enacted / effective:** MiCA applicable from 2024/2025
- **Summary:** Germany moved from a prominent national crypto-custody regime into the EU-wide MiCA model, but BaFin remains one of the most demanding supervisors in practice. Firms should expect detailed review of governance, outsourcing, asset segregation, safeguarding, and management suitability.
- **Scope:** CASP activities, issuance, custody, exchange and related crypto services.

### German Banking Act (KWG) crypto custody perimeter
- **Date enacted / effective:** Crypto custody added in 2020 and remains relevant for legacy positioning
- **Summary:** Before MiCA, Germany explicitly regulated crypto custody as a financial service under the KWG. That history matters because BaFin expects mature operational controls and still applies securities, banking, and custody analysis where tokenized instruments fall outside MiCA.
- **Scope:** Crypto custody, financial services, overlap with tokenized securities and banking activities.

### Electronic Securities Act (eWpG)
- **Date enacted / effective:** Effective June 2021; expanded in subsequent reforms
- **Summary:** The eWpG enables electronic securities and certain crypto-securities without paper certificates. It is particularly relevant for enterprise tokenization projects because it gives a domestic legal route for issuance and registry models beyond pure payment-token use cases.
- **Scope:** Tokenized bonds/fund units, electronic securities registries, DLT-based issuance.

## Licensing Requirements
MiCA authorization is required for CASP activities. Separate permissions may still be needed where activities trigger securities custody, investment brokerage, banking, or registry functions under German law; tokenized-securities operators should assess eWpG and KWG overlap early.

## Taxonomy
Germany now follows the MiCA categories for most crypto-assets, but BaFin still distinguishes financial instruments, units in investment funds, e-money, and crypto-assets outside securities law. Crypto-securities under the eWpG are not treated the same as MiCA-native utility/payment tokens.

## Key Compliance Requirements
Expect strong AML/KYC controls under the German AML Act, travel-rule compliance, outsourcing governance, safeguarding of private keys, client-asset segregation, incident reporting, and evidencing senior-management accountability. BaFin places heavy emphasis on operational resilience and documentation quality.

## Recent Developments
In 2024-2025, firms that relied on legacy German registrations began migrating into MiCA authorization planning. BaFin has also remained active on AML expectations and outsourcing scrutiny for regulated digital-asset firms.

## Impact on RFP/Procurement
Germany is a procurement-heavy jurisdiction: buyers typically ask for formal control mapping, segregation design, eWpG/tokenized-securities support, German-language governance packs, and evidence that the platform can survive BaFin-level model validation. A vendor that only supports exchange-style crypto flows will often be insufficient for German enterprise tokenization programs.
