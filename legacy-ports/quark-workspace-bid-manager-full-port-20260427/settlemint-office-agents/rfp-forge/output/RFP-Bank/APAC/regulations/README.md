# APAC Digital Asset Regulations

Last updated: March 2026

## Regional Overview

APAC combines some of the world's clearest digital-asset rulebooks with some of its most restrictive markets. Singapore and Hong Kong are explicit licensing hubs, Australia continues to rely on financial-services, payments, and AML overlays while designing a broader regime, and South Korea maintains exacting exchange and token-listing expectations. Southeast Asian jurisdictions such as Thailand, the Philippines, Indonesia, and Malaysia have active frameworks, while India remains cautious and tax-heavy rather than licence-forward.

Procurement across APAC demands configurable policy controls rather than a one-size-fits-all stack. Enterprise buyers need support for exchange/custody permissions, travel-rule compliance, token classification differences, and restrictions on retail access to certain products. Vendors with modular issuance, custody, compliance, and reporting layers are better positioned to support cross-border operations without forcing a redesign for each jurisdiction.

## Country Index

| Country | Primary Regulator | Key Framework | Classification Approach | Status |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Singapore | MAS | Payment Services Act; Financial Services and Markets Act | Digital payment tokens, capital markets products, e-money | Mature hub; retail restrictions tightened |
| Hong Kong | SFC; HKMA | AMLO VASP regime; tokenized product guidance | Virtual assets; securities tokens where applicable | Full licensing regime operating |
| Australia | ASIC; AUSTRAC; Treasury | Corporations Act, AML/CTF Act, proposed digital asset platform regime | Financial products vs non-financial crypto assets | Interim regime with reforms pending |
| South Korea | FSC/FIU | Virtual Asset User Protection Act; Specified Financial Information Act | Virtual assets; security tokens treated separately | Strong exchange/custody compliance regime |
| Thailand | SEC Thailand; Bank of Thailand | Digital Asset Business Emergency Decree (amended April 2025) | Cryptocurrencies and digital tokens; approved token list (35 tokens) | Extraterritorial licensing enforced; offshore platforms blocked; Travel Rule proposed Mar 2026 |
| Philippines | BSP; SEC Philippines | BSP VASP rules (Circulars 944, 1108); SEC CASP framework (MC 4 & 5, July 2025) | Virtual assets and investment-contract style tokens | VASP licensing moratorium extended indefinitely (Sep 2025); FATF Partially Compliant rating |
| India | FIU-IND; RBI; SEBI (primary regulator framework under discussion) | PMLA VDA obligations; 2026 tax/CARF rules; Asset Tokenisation Bill 2026 | Virtual digital assets for tax/AML; CARF/CRS reporting from Jan 2026 | AML regime active; SEBI-primary regulator framework under development; comprehensive market law pending |
| Indonesia | OJK (full authority as of Jan 10, 2025) | POJK 27/2024 + POJK 23/2025; P2SK Law | Digital financial assets (including crypto); derivatives now included | OJK supervision fully operational; Bappebti handover complete |
| Malaysia | SC Malaysia; BNM | Capital Markets and Services (Prescription of Securities) Order; DAX guidelines | Digital assets, digital tokens, and securities tokens | Licensing framework active |
