# India

Last updated: March 2026

**Country:** India
**Regulatory Authority:** FIU-IND (Financial Intelligence Unit), RBI, Ministry of Finance, SEBI where securities are implicated

## Key Regulations

### PMLA extension to Virtual Digital Asset Service Providers
- **Date enacted / effective:** Applied from March 2023; registration circular updated September 15, 2025
- **Summary:** India brought virtual digital asset businesses (exchanges, wallets, mining pools) into the Prevention of Money Laundering Act (PMLA) framework, requiring registration with FIU-IND and implementation of customer due diligence, recordkeeping, and suspicious transaction reporting. This is the primary formal compliance perimeter for crypto businesses. FIU-IND issued updated "AML & CFT Guidelines for Reporting Entities Providing Services Related to Virtual Digital Assets" on January 8, 2026, replacing the prior 2023 guidelines. The 2026 Guidelines consolidate the end-to-end VDA service provider registration process, multiple circulars, and updated risk classification requirements into a single document.
- **Scope:** AML/KYC, exchange/custody intermediaries, reporting obligations, VDA SP registration.

### Tax regime for virtual digital assets (30% tax + 1% TDS)
- **Date enacted / effective:** Introduced 2022 (Finance Act); Finance Bill 2026 (Section 509) effective April 1, 2026
- **Summary:** India maintains a 30% flat tax on VDA gains and 1% TDS on transfers. Finance Bill 2026 introduced Section 509, mandating transaction-level reporting by VDA service providers — including exchanges — backed by daily penalties for non-compliance. The system moves from voluntary self-reporting to automated, data-driven enforcement through mandatory data aggregation beginning in 2026, with OECD Crypto-Asset Reporting Framework (CARF) cross-border data exchange with 40+ jurisdictions launching April 1, 2027.
- **Scope:** Taxation, withholding, reporting, transaction economics, international data exchange.

### Income-Tax (Amendment) Rules, 2026 — CARF and CRS alignment
- **Date enacted / effective:** Notified March 5, 2026; retrospective from January 1, 2026
- **Summary:** CBDT issued Gazette Notification G.S.R. 158(E) amending Income-tax Rules 114F, 114G, and 114H to bring crypto assets, Central Bank Digital Currency (CBDC), and Specified Electronic Money Products (SEMPs) formally within India's financial account reporting framework under the OECD CRS and CARF. Defined "Relevant Crypto-Asset," "CBDC," and "SEMP" for reporting purposes. Crypto-to-fiat and crypto-to-crypto exchange transactions incorporated into the reporting framework. Financial institutions holding CBDC or SEMPs on behalf of customers have reporting obligations.
- **Scope:** CARF, CRS, FATCA compliance, crypto-asset reporting, CBDC/stablecoin classification.

### Asset Tokenisation (Regulation) Bill, 2026 — Private Member's Bill
- **Date enacted / effective:** Introduced in Rajya Sabha March 14, 2026 (Private Member's Bill)
- **Summary:** India's first dedicated legislative proposal for regulating tokenized real-world assets. Proposes legal recognition of asset tokenisation, a licensing framework for issuance/trading/custody/settlement of tokenised assets, and market integrity mechanisms. Uses a multi-regulator model: SEBI for securities tokens, RBI for payment-related tokens and stable value arrangements, and sector regulators (insurance, pension) for sector-specific tokens. Tokens covering real estate, infrastructure, carbon credits, and intellectual property are within scope alongside traditional securities. Provides a compliance runway for existing tokenisation sandbox projects. Status: Private Member's Bill; legislative path uncertain but signals policy intent.
- **Scope:** Tokenized real-world assets, multi-regulator model, issuance, trading, custody, settlement, lifecycle regulation.

### SEBI-as-primary-regulator discussions
- **Date enacted / effective:** Discussions ongoing as of early 2026 ahead of Union Budget 2026-27
- **Summary:** The Ministry of Finance, SEBI, and RBI were in active discussions in early 2026 to finalize a regulatory framework under which SEBI would serve as the primary regulator for crypto exchanges, with RBI overseeing foreign investment and cross-border transactions. The framework would require all Indian crypto exchanges to register with SEBI. No comprehensive legislation enacted as of March 2026, but inter-agency coordination is advancing. The absence of a single regulator has been identified as a key governance gap.
- **Scope:** Market structure, exchange registration, investor protection, cross-border oversight.

## Licensing Requirements
There is no single comprehensive crypto-asset licence. Firms must register with FIU-IND for AML purposes under the 2026 Guidelines. Securities, payments, or collective-investment features may trigger additional SEBI or RBI scrutiny. When enacted, the proposed SEBI-primary-regulator framework would require exchange registration with SEBI. The Asset Tokenisation Bill, if passed, would add a formal licensing framework for tokenized asset platforms.

## Taxonomy
India uses the term "virtual digital assets" primarily for tax and AML purposes. CBDCs and SEMPs are now formally defined in the income-tax framework (March 2026). The SEBI-RBI coordination framework distinguishes crypto-asset trading from payment flows and cross-border transactions.

## Key Compliance Requirements
AML/KYC, FIU-IND registration under 2026 Guidelines, suspicious transaction reporting, 30% tax withholding/reporting, Section 509 transaction-level reporting (from April 1, 2026), CARF-aligned international reporting (from April 1, 2027), income-tax CRS/CARF reporting obligations (retrospective from January 1, 2026), and conservative banking-interface management.

## Recent Developments

**January 8, 2026:** FIU-IND issued comprehensive updated AML & CFT Guidelines for VDA service providers, replacing the 2023 guidelines and consolidating multiple circulars including the September 2025 Registration Circular.

**January 2026:** Ministry of Finance, SEBI, and RBI engaged in active discussions to establish SEBI as primary crypto exchange regulator. No legislation introduced by the time of the February 1, 2026 Union Budget, but discussions ongoing.

**March 5, 2026 (retroactive from January 1, 2026):** CBDT issued Income-Tax (Amendment) Rules 2026, bringing crypto assets, CBDCs, and SEMPs formally into India's CRS/CARF financial account reporting framework.

**March 14, 2026:** Asset Tokenisation (Regulation) Bill, 2026 introduced in Rajya Sabha as India's first dedicated tokenization legislation. Multi-regulator model covering SEBI, RBI, and sectoral regulators.

**Finance Bill 2026 (Section 509), effective April 1, 2026:** Mandatory transaction-level reporting obligations for VDA service providers with daily penalties for non-compliance. CARF cross-border data exchange with 40+ jurisdictions from April 1, 2027.

## Impact on RFP/Procurement
Indian buyers need platforms with rigorous AML/KYC controls, FIU-IND compliant reporting, and robust transaction-level data infrastructure to meet the new Section 509 enforcement regime. The pending SEBI-as-primary-regulator framework will likely require exchange re-registration once enacted. For tokenized asset use cases, the Asset Tokenisation Bill signals legislative intent; procurement teams should track its progress but avoid designing systems around unpassaged legislation. The overarching compliance posture is compliance-first, surveillance-capable, and built for CARF-aligned international reporting.
