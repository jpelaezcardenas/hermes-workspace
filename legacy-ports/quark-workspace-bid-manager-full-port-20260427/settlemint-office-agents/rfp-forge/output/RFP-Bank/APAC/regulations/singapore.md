# Singapore

Last updated: March 2026

**Country:** Singapore
**Regulatory Authority:** Monetary Authority of Singapore (MAS)

## Key Regulations

### Payment Services Act 2019 (PSA)
- **Date enacted / effective:** In force 2020; digital payment token scope expanded over time
- **Summary:** Singapore regulates many crypto businesses under the PSA as providers of digital payment token services. The law covers exchange, transfer, custody, brokerage, and other activity with a strong emphasis on AML/CFT, technology risk, and customer protection.
- **Scope:** Digital payment token services, transfers, custody, exchange, brokerage.

### Financial Services and Markets Act 2022 (FSMA)
- **Date enacted / effective:** In force from 2023 onward
- **Summary:** FSMA extends licensing and AML reach to certain digital-token service providers based in Singapore but serving offshore business. This closes regulatory gaps and reinforces Singapore's preference for entity-wide supervision rather than perimeter arbitrage.
- **Scope:** Offshore digital token services, AML/CFT, supervisory reach.

### MAS consumer and stablecoin measures
- **Date enacted / effective:** 2023-2025
- **Summary:** MAS tightened retail access, transfer, and custody expectations for digital payment token providers and separately finalized a framework for single-currency stablecoins. Singapore therefore remains open but highly disciplined on who may offer what, and how.
- **Scope:** Retail restrictions, custody, stablecoins, consumer protection.

## Licensing Requirements
DPT service providers generally need a major payment institution or standard payment institution licence under the PSA, or a related exemption during transition. Stablecoin issuers and firms offering capital-markets token products may need additional approvals under payments or securities law.

## Taxonomy
Singapore distinguishes digital payment tokens, e-money, capital markets products, and MAS-regulated stablecoins. A tokenized bond or fund unit will fall into securities law, not just the DPT regime.

## Key Compliance Requirements
AML/KYC, travel rule, technology risk management, segregation of customer assets, restrictions on retail incentives/credit, cybersecurity, outsourcing governance, and detailed reporting are core. MAS expects mature board and senior-management oversight.

## Recent Developments
2024-2025 has been about implementing retail-access restrictions and the stablecoin regime while keeping Singapore attractive for institutional tokenization. The country is still a hub, but no longer a lightly governed one.

## Impact on RFP/Procurement
Singapore procurement is governance-heavy. Buyers want modular support for DPT, stablecoin, and tokenized-capital-markets use cases, plus strong outsourcing and incident controls. A vendor that cannot speak MAS language around risk and governance will struggle.
