## 2. Executive Summary

Mashreq Bank is one of the UAE’s longest-standing and most innovation-oriented financial institutions, with a history dating back to 1967 and a regional and international footprint spanning the UAE, Egypt, Qatar, Kuwait, Bahrain, Saudi Arabia, the United Kingdom, and the United States. Over the last several years, Mashreq has pursued a deliberate strategy to modernize its financial infrastructure, digitize client services, and expand into next-generation operating models for capital markets, treasury, transaction banking, and wealth products.

Mashreq’s digital innovation trajectory includes several relevant milestones:

- Early leadership in distributed ledger-enabled KYC infrastructure, including participation in one of the region’s first blockchain-based KYC initiatives in collaboration with DIFC in 2020.
- Expansion of API-first banking capabilities, including the launch of an API Marketplace for corporate clients in 2025.
- Continued innovation through Mashreq Capital in digital and alternative investment products, including the launch of the BITMAC fund in December 2025 as a DIFC-domiciled, DFSA-regulated investment product.
- Development of internal capabilities and proofs of concept related to tokenized funds and digital asset structures, including exposure to international initiatives such as MAS Project Guardian.
- Expansion of the NEO CORP and NeoBiz ecosystem, including broader digital business banking capabilities in Egypt and digital payment innovations for B2B segments.
- A February 2026 memorandum of understanding with DIFC focused on digital transformation and wealth solutions, reinforcing Mashreq’s intent to move beyond experimentation toward enterprise-scale execution.

Mashreq now seeks to transition from fragmented pilots, product-specific experiments, and business-line initiatives to a unified production-grade digital asset platform architecture that can support multiple business models, legal entities, jurisdictions, and asset classes. The Bank’s objective is not a narrow sandbox solution, but a strategic platform that can provide governed, extensible, and regulator-ready capabilities across the group.

This RFP is therefore intended to identify a technology partner and platform capable of enabling Mashreq to:

- Launch and administer tokenized fund products at institutional quality and production scale.
- Structure and service digital bonds and sukuk for targeted client and treasury use cases.
- Explore and operationalize tokenized deposit models aligned with evolving central bank and domestic payment modernization initiatives, including the CBUAE digital currency direction.
- Deliver digitally native wealth management and private banking products with strong suitability, investor classification, and transfer control mechanisms.
- Extend tokenized payment and programmable settlement capabilities for business clients, including NeoBiz and adjacent B2B channels.
- Provide a common control framework spanning onboarding, compliance, entitlementing, custody, workflow, lifecycle servicing, data integration, and reporting.

Mashreq expects respondents to understand that this initiative sits at the intersection of regulated banking, regulated asset management, digital payments, and capital markets infrastructure. As such, the desired platform must combine functional breadth with operational realism. Mashreq is not seeking a conceptual innovation partner. It is seeking a platform vendor with proven technology, a credible delivery model, and the capacity to operate within the governance, security, and regulatory expectations of a leading regional bank.

The selected solution should support phased deployment, beginning with a controlled pilot led by Mashreq Capital and expanding into broader group-level use cases over time. Mashreq expects the platform to be open, API-centric, interoperable, modular, and capable of supporting both permissioned and public blockchain-connected models where appropriate and approved.

## 3. Background and Strategic Context

### 3.1 Institutional Context

Mashreq has achieved strong financial and operational momentum, including record growth in 2025. The Bank’s strategic direction increasingly emphasizes platform modernization, digitized operating models, ecosystem connectivity, and selective innovation in digital assets and programmable finance.

The Bank’s digital asset interest is not speculative in nature. It is linked to concrete business priorities:

- Product innovation for wealth and institutional clients.
- Balance sheet and treasury modernization.
- Cross-border settlement efficiency.
- Improved transparency and lifecycle automation for investment products.
- Stronger API-based product distribution and partner enablement.
- Enhanced competitiveness in a regional market where regulators are progressively defining digital asset and tokenization frameworks.

### 3.2 Strategic Need for a Unified Platform

Mashreq’s recent initiatives have demonstrated business appetite and internal learning, but current capabilities are not yet consolidated into a common operating layer. Different use cases may presently rely on separate tooling, manual controls, external service providers, or pilot-specific workflows. This creates limitations in scale, governance, and repeatability.

Mashreq intends to establish a common digital asset platform to provide:

- Standardized issuance and servicing workflows.
- Shared compliance and control capabilities.
- Reusable integration patterns across legal entities and channels.
- Consistent security, identity, and operational controls.
- Lower marginal effort to launch new digital asset use cases.
- Improved readiness for regulatory engagement and audit.

### 3.3 In-Scope Group Entities and Business Lines

The platform is expected to support, over time, one or more of the following entities and business contexts:

- **Mashreq Bank** – core banking, transaction banking, treasury, and selected wealth use cases.
- **Mashreq Capital** – regulated investment products, fund structures, wealth and asset management offerings, and potential digital securities issuance.
- **NeoBiz / NEO CORP ecosystem** – programmable B2B instruments, digital payment workflows, and API-enabled corporate services.

### 3.4 Procurement Objective

Mashreq seeks proposals from vendors able to provide either:

1. A configurable platform product with the required functional modules and enterprise controls; or  
2. A platform-led solution with implementation services, integration support, and a clearly delineated product roadmap and support model.

Mashreq prefers productized capabilities over bespoke engineering-heavy responses. Vendors must clearly distinguish between:

- Features available today in production,
- Features available through standard configuration,
- Features available on the near-term roadmap, and
- Features that would require custom development.

## 5. Scope of Work

### 5.1 Overview of Scope

The selected vendor shall provide a digital asset platform and associated implementation services capable of supporting the end-to-end lifecycle of regulated digital financial products and instruments. The platform should support the issuance, administration, distribution, transfer control, settlement, servicing, reporting, and integration needs associated with the following target use cases.

### 5.2 Primary Use Cases in Scope

#### 5.2.1 Tokenized Fund Management

Mashreq seeks to evolve current fund innovation initiatives into a platform-enabled operating model for natively tokenized fund units or digitally represented fund interests, beginning with a Mashreq Capital-led use case. The solution should support the full fund lifecycle, including onboarding, subscriptions, redemptions, transfers, valuations, distributions, reporting, restrictions, and integration with fund accounting and registrar processes.

Representative scenarios include:

- Institutional feeder or private fund tokenization.
- Qualified investor fund structures.
- Fractional ownership models subject to suitability and investor classification rules.
- Secondary transfer controls within approved investor cohorts.

#### 5.2.2 Digital Bonds and Sukuk Issuance

Mashreq is evaluating digital issuance and servicing capabilities for debt products, including conventional bonds and Shariah-compliant sukuk structures. The platform should support issuance setup, allocation, ownership records, coupon/profit distribution events, maturity/redemption events, transfer restrictions, and lifecycle event management.

Representative scenarios include:

- Private placement instruments.
- Structured certificates or notes.
- Internal treasury issuance pilots.
- Sukuk structures requiring role-based compliance, asset linkage records, and Shariah governance workflow support.

#### 5.2.3 Tokenized Deposits

Mashreq is interested in tokenized deposit capabilities aligned with anticipated domestic payment modernization and digital currency developments, including models compatible with CBUAE-led direction where permitted. The solution should support programmable deposit-linked instruments, controlled mint/burn logic, wallet/account linkage, and settlement workflows governed by banking controls.

Representative scenarios include:

- Corporate treasury settlement tokens.
- Internal liquidity movement pilots.
- Deposit token rails for approved enterprise clients.
- Restricted-purpose programmable cash instruments.

#### 5.2.4 Wealth Management Products

Mashreq seeks to support digitally enabled wealth products that can offer improved accessibility, transparency, and automation while preserving strong investor protection controls. The platform should enable product packaging and servicing for wealth and private banking contexts, including fractional access and embedded compliance checks.

Representative scenarios include:

- Digitally distributed alternative products for qualified investors.
- Structured offerings with eligibility rules.
- Fractional access to approved investment opportunities.
- Automated compliance, suitability, and holding restrictions.

#### 5.2.5 B2B Tokenized Payment Instruments

Mashreq intends to assess extensions of NeoBiz and related B2B capabilities into programmable, tokenized, or digitally represented payment instruments. These may include receivable-linked instruments, restricted-value transfer instruments, or settlement tokens for approved business workflows.

Representative scenarios include:

- B2B settlement tokens in closed-loop environments.
- Corporate disbursement and collection workflows.
- Trade or supply chain-adjacent payment instruments.
- API-enabled programmable payments for ecosystem partners.

### 5.3 Services in Scope

Vendors should include the following services within scope:

- Solution design and target architecture definition.
- Product configuration and environment setup.
- Integration design and implementation support.
- Security and deployment planning.
- Regulatory mapping support and control framework documentation.
- Test planning, execution support, and defect resolution.
- Training for business, operations, technology, compliance, and support teams.
- Production cutover planning.
- Hypercare and post-go-live support.

### 5.4 Out of Scope

Unless explicitly proposed as optional modules, the following are generally outside the core scope of this RFP:

- Fully bespoke blockchain protocol engineering.
- Consumer-facing retail crypto trading use cases.
- Unregulated virtual asset exchange functionality.
- Custodial brokerage operations not aligned to Mashreq’s intended business model.
- Open market speculation-oriented trading platforms.

## 6. Business and Operating Principles

Mashreq expects proposals to be grounded in the following operating principles:

1. **Regulated-first design** – controls, auditability, and policy enforcement are non-negotiable.
2. **API-first integration** – the platform must fit Mashreq’s existing digital architecture and API Marketplace model.
3. **Modularity** – the Bank should be able to activate use cases progressively rather than all at once.
4. **Entity-aware configuration** – separate brands, legal entities, and jurisdictions must be supported without duplicative platforms.
5. **Operational realism** – the proposed model must work under real banking controls, not only in sandbox environments.
6. **Interoperability** – blockchain, custody, data, and enterprise system integration must be practical and extensible.
7. **Security by design** – strong identity, key management, encryption, monitoring, and resilience controls are mandatory.
8. **Regional readiness** – the solution must be suitable for UAE deployment and expandable to Egypt and other relevant jurisdictions.

### 13.2 Evaluation Criteria Detail

- Fit to Mashreq’s target use cases.
- API-first architecture maturity.
- Integration readiness with Mashreq systems.
- Security architecture and key management approach.

Evaluation will consider:

- Strength of built-in control framework.
- Understanding of UAE, DIFC, and banking requirements.
- Ability to support asset management and banking use cases under separate legal entities.
- Quality of auditability, evidencing, and restriction controls.

Evaluation will consider:

- Realism of implementation plan.

- Quality of proposed team.

Evaluation will consider:

- Cost transparency.

- Commercial flexibility.

#### E. References and Vendor Credentials (10%)

Evaluation will consider:

- Relevance of prior deployments.
- Strength of banking or institutional references.
- Regional experience.
- Financial stability and organizational maturity.
