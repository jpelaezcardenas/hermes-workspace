# Indonesia

Last updated: March 2026

**Country:** Indonesia
**Regulatory Authority:** OJK (Otoritas Jasa Keuangan) — Financial Services Authority; Bank Indonesia (BI) for payment/monetary aspects

## Key Regulations

### Law No. 4 of 2023 on Financial Sector Development and Strengthening (P2SK Law)
- **Date enacted / effective:** Enacted 2023; supervisory transfer effective January 10, 2025
- **Summary:** The P2SK Law mandated transferring regulatory and supervisory authority over digital financial assets — including crypto assets and financial derivatives — from the Commodity Futures Trading Regulatory Agency (Bappebti) to OJK and Bank Indonesia. This marked a fundamental philosophical shift: crypto assets moved from commodity-style regulation to the financial services framework, treated as "digital financial assets" alongside securities and other financial instruments.
- **Scope:** Supervisory framework, jurisdiction, classification of digital financial assets.

### OJK Regulation No. 27 of 2024 (POJK 27/2024) — Implementation of Digital Financial Asset Trading
- **Date enacted / effective:** Issued December 10, 2024; effective January 10, 2025
- **Summary:** The operational framework that replaced Bappebti's commodity-based rules. Classifies crypto assets as digital financial assets rather than commodities. All crypto trading platforms must hold OJK licenses (not merely Bappebti registrations). Requires risk management systems, investor disclosures, AML/KYC compliance, and audits by OJK-approved firms. A whitelist of tradable digital financial assets is maintained by the Digital Financial Asset Bourse. Minimum capital for Crypto Asset Traders set at IDR 100 billion issued capital and IDR 50 billion maintained equity.
- **Scope:** Exchange licensing, custody, clearing, approved-asset lists, consumer protection, AML/KYC.

### OJK Regulation No. 23 of 2025 (POJK 23/2025) — Amendment and Expansion
- **Date enacted / effective:** December 2025
- **Summary:** A major consolidation one year after the Bappebti-to-OJK handover. Formally legalizes and integrates digital financial asset derivatives into OJK's unified framework — previously these were the domain of Bappebti-supervised futures brokers. Expands the regulatory scope beyond crypto assets to include derivatives referencing digital financial assets. Removes the prior requirement for assets to use distributed ledger technology. Bourses intending to facilitate derivatives trading must seek OJK approval. Derivative transactions require margin management in dedicated accounts. Consumers must pass a knowledge test before engaging in derivative trading. OJK gains authority to conduct regulatory sandbox trials for digital asset innovation. Reduces daily reporting burden to annual reporting for certain operators.
- **Scope:** Crypto derivatives, expanded digital financial asset taxonomy, sandbox authority, investor suitability.

### Government Regulation No. 49 of 2024 (GR 49/2024) — Transition Framework
- **Date enacted / effective:** December 31, 2024; transition effective January 10, 2025
- **Summary:** Formally mandated the transfer of regulatory authority from Bappebti to OJK and BI. Specified that all Bappebti-issued licenses, approvals, and product registrations remain valid pending compliance with new OJK rules. Transfer expected to be fully complete by January 10, 2027.
- **Scope:** Regulatory transition, license continuity, inter-agency coordination.

## Licensing Requirements
OJK now licenses Digital Financial Asset Trading Providers, including exchanges (bourses), traders, clearing houses, and custody providers. Bappebti-registered entities were given a 6-month grace period to comply with OJK governance, data protection, and consumer protection provisions after January 10, 2025. Derivatives trading on exchange requires additional OJK approval. Existing Bappebti licenses remain valid unless conflicting with POJK 27/2024.

## Taxonomy
Indonesia has reclassified crypto assets from commodities to digital financial assets under the financial services framework. POJK 23/2025 further expanded the taxonomy to include derivatives referencing digital financial assets. Over 14 million active crypto users as of 2025; IDR 650 trillion in trading volume in 2024.

## Key Compliance Requirements
OJK licensing and re-registration, AML/KYC, approved-asset whitelist compliance, custody segregation, audit by OJK-approved firms, investor disclosures, capital adequacy, derivative approval and margin management, consumer knowledge requirements for derivatives, local Electronic System Provider (PSE) registration. OJK Circular Letter No. 20 of 2024 (SEOJK AKD AK) specifies detailed reporting obligations.

## Recent Developments

**January 10, 2025:** Formal completion of the Bappebti-to-OJK regulatory handover — a historic milestone. Crypto assets officially reclassified as digital financial assets within the financial services system. OJK assumed full supervisory authority including for pending license applications.

**December 2025:** OJK issued POJK 23/2025, expressly legalizing crypto derivatives under OJK supervision and expanding the digital financial asset framework. Previously bifurcated regulatory landscape unified. OJK granted sandbox authority for digital asset innovation.

**January 2026:** Legal analysis from major Indonesian law firms (ABNR, SSEK, ADCO) confirmed POJK 23/2025 represents full OJK consolidation, one year after the handover. Derivatives trading now formally integrated into the OJK digital asset framework.

**Ongoing 2026:** OJK has begun drafting rules for DeFi, NFTs, stablecoins, and institutional crypto trading under the new framework. Transition period fully concluded by January 10, 2027.

## Impact on RFP/Procurement
Indonesian procurement rewards vendors that fully support the OJK digital financial asset framework rather than the legacy Bappebti commodity model. Configurability for derivatives support, OJK licensing requirements, and AML/KYC controls is essential. Platforms should demonstrate readiness for both spot digital asset trading and derivatives under the unified OJK framework. Sandbox-eligibility and adaptability for upcoming DeFi/stablecoin rules are differentiators.
