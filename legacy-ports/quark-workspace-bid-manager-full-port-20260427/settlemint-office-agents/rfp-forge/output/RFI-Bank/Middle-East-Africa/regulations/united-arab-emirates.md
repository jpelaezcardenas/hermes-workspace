# United Arab Emirates

Last updated: March 2026

**Country:** United Arab Emirates
**Regulatory Authority:** VARA, SCA, CBUAE, ADGM FSRA, DIFC DFSA

## Key Regulations

### Dubai VARA framework and rulebooks
- **Date enacted / effective:** VARA law 2022; rulebooks updated through 2024-2025
- **Summary:** Dubai created a dedicated virtual-asset regulator with activity-specific licensing for advisory, broker-dealer, custody, exchange, lending, and transfer services. VARA has become one of the clearest licensing systems for crypto-native business within a major commercial hub.
- **Scope:** Virtual asset services including custody, exchange, brokerage, lending, and transfer.

### ADGM FSRA virtual asset framework
- **Date enacted / effective:** Initial framework 2018; updated over time
- **Summary:** ADGM remains a leading common-law financial free zone with a mature framework for virtual asset exchanges, custody, and related activities. It is often preferred for institutional and international-market structures.
- **Scope:** Virtual asset trading, custody, prudential supervision, institutional market infrastructure.

### Federal UAE/SCA and stablecoin/payment developments
- **Date enacted / effective:** Ongoing through 2024-2025
- **Summary:** The federal perimeter continues to evolve, particularly where virtual assets intersect with securities, payments, and dirham-backed stablecoin ambitions. Firms must map whether they are operating in mainland UAE, Dubai, ADGM, or DIFC because the perimeter differs by zone and activity.
- **Scope:** Securities-style tokens, payment tokens, stablecoins, zone-specific authorization.

## Licensing Requirements
Licensing is entity- and zone-specific. Firms may need VARA authorization in Dubai, FSRA approval in ADGM, DFSA permission in DIFC for investment tokens, or mainland approvals under SCA/CBUAE rules depending on the business model.

## Taxonomy
The UAE uses broad virtual-asset concepts with activity-based licensing, while securities/investment tokens and payment instruments can fall into different perimeters. Zone analysis is as important as token classification.

## Key Compliance Requirements
AML/KYC, sanctions, travel-rule style controls, custody segregation, local governance, outsourcing oversight, cybersecurity, and wallet/key-management controls are central. Buyers often ask for jurisdiction-specific policy templates for each UAE zone.

## Recent Developments
2024-2025 brought continued tightening of licensing detail, stronger marketing and onboarding expectations, and growing focus on stablecoins and institutional tokenization. The UAE is open, but not light-touch.

## Impact on RFP/Procurement
UAE procurement requires zone-aware architecture. Enterprise buyers prefer vendors that can support separate licensed entities, configurable rulebooks, Arabic/English documentation workflows, and institutional custody controls across multiple regulatory perimeters.
