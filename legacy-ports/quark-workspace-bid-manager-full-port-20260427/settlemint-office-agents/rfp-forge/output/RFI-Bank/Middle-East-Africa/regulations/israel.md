# Israel

Last updated: March 2026

**Country:** Israel
**Regulatory Authority:** Israel Securities Authority (ISA), Capital Markets Authority, Bank of Israel, Tax Authority

## Key Regulations

### Financial Services Supervision framework for certain virtual-asset activity
- **Date enacted / effective:** Developed through recent years
- **Summary:** Israel regulates aspects of virtual-asset activity through existing financial-services licensing law, particularly where services resemble financial asset services or custody/intermediation. The framework is more evolutionary than a single bespoke crypto statute.
- **Scope:** Financial asset services, custody/intermediation, licensing.

### ISA positions on token offerings and securities
- **Date enacted / effective:** Ongoing
- **Summary:** The ISA has taken the view that tokens can constitute securities depending on their rights and economic reality. This is highly relevant for tokenized fundraising, investment products, and enterprise issuance projects.
- **Scope:** Token offerings, security tokens, investment products.

### Tax and AML treatment
- **Date enacted / effective:** Ongoing
- **Summary:** Israel generally treats crypto-assets as property/financial assets for tax purposes and applies AML/CFT controls where relevant service-provider activity is present. These practical overlays strongly influence operating design.
- **Scope:** Tax reporting, AML/KYC, recordkeeping, monitoring.

## Licensing Requirements
Licensing needs depend on the activity: financial asset service permissions, securities-law compliance, and broader financial regulation may all apply. There is no single simple licence for all digital-asset business models.

## Taxonomy
Israel uses functional classification: some tokens are securities, some are virtual/financial assets, and tax law generally treats holdings as property. Token rights and the surrounding service model drive the answer.

## Key Compliance Requirements
AML/KYC, tax reporting, customer disclosures, custody governance, and legal classification evidence are central. Institutional buyers also care about cybersecurity and incident-response maturity.

## Recent Developments
2024-2025 has seen ongoing policy development around market structure and tokenized finance, but not yet a single unified crypto code. Enterprises should expect continued reliance on perimeter analysis and case-by-case treatment.

## Impact on RFP/Procurement
Israeli procurement favors vendors that can support both crypto-asset operations and tokenized-securities style workflows with strong legal configurability. A platform that cannot document classification logic will struggle in approval processes.
