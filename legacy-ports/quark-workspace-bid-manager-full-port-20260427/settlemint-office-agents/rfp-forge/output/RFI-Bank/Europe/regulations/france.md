# France

Last updated: March 2026

**Country:** France
**Regulatory Authority:** Autorité des marchés financiers (AMF) and ACPR

## Key Regulations

### PACTE Act PSAN regime
- **Date enacted / effective:** Introduced 2019; enhanced registration requirements expanded later
- **Summary:** France created one of Europe's earliest dedicated digital-asset regimes through the PSAN framework, covering custody, buying/selling digital assets for legal tender, trading, and related services. The regime combined AML registration with optional licensing before MiCA.
- **Scope:** Digital-asset services including custody, brokerage, trading, and exchange.

### MiCA
- **Date enacted / effective:** Applies from 2024/2025
- **Summary:** MiCA is replacing the standalone French-first approach with a harmonized EU licensing and disclosure regime. France remains important because AMF supervisory expectations are high and many firms are transitioning from PSAN status to full CASP authorization.
- **Scope:** CASP activities, public offerings, stablecoins, market conduct.

### French AML/CFT and advertising controls
- **Date enacted / effective:** Ongoing
- **Summary:** French authorities have paired licensing with close attention to financial promotions and AML compliance. This matters operationally because firms must align onboarding, customer due diligence, sanctions screening, and communications approval workflows.
- **Scope:** AML/KYC, marketing, onboarding, sanctions and consumer protection.

## Licensing Requirements
Legacy PSAN registration may still matter during transition, but long-term market access depends on MiCA CASP authorization. EMT issuance can require e-money permissions, and tokenized securities may trigger French/ EU securities law outside MiCA.

## Taxonomy
France uses the MiCA categories for crypto-assets while preserving securities-law treatment for instruments that qualify as financial instruments. The historic French notion of “digital assets” is narrowing as MiCA becomes the main framework.

## Key Compliance Requirements
Core obligations include AML/KYC, travel rule readiness, safeguarding of client assets, governance, complaints handling, disclosure/white paper controls, and approval workflows for marketing materials. France is especially sensitive to consumer-facing promotions and senior-management oversight.

## Recent Developments
2024-2025 has been dominated by the PSAN-to-MiCA transition, with market participants preparing for stricter prudential and conduct requirements. France also remains active in the EU debate around DeFi, stablecoins, and institutional tokenization.

## Impact on RFP/Procurement
French buyers want a platform that can satisfy AMF documentation standards, support MiCA white papers and policy evidence, and cleanly separate crypto-asset use cases from tokenized-securities workflows. Procurement often turns on governance depth rather than feature breadth alone.
