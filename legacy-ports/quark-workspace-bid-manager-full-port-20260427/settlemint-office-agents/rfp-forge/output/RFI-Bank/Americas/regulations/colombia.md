# Colombia

Last updated: March 2026

**Country:** Colombia
**Regulatory Authority:** Superintendencia Financiera de Colombia (SFC), UIAF, DIAN

## Key Regulations

### Decree 1297 / PSAV Registry Framework
- **Date enacted / effective:** Decree 1297 enacted 2023; PSAV registry operational 2024 onwards
- **Summary:** Colombia's landmark step toward a formal digital-asset framework. Decree 1297 created the official Registro de Proveedores de Servicios de Activos Virtuales (PSAV Registry), establishing registration obligations for all entities providing virtual asset services — including exchanges, custodians, and digital payment platforms — on a habitual, professional basis to clients in Colombia. The registry is administered by the SFC and serves as the cornerstone of Colombia's supervisory framework.
- **Scope:** VASP registration, market oversight, mandatory reporting.

### Bill 510/2025 — Virtual Asset Law (Legislative Draft)
- **Date enacted / effective:** Filed in Congress February 2025; not yet enacted as of March 2026
- **Summary:** A comprehensive draft law to regulate virtual asset service providers (PSAVs/VASPs) in Colombia. Key provisions include: mandatory PSAV registration supervised by the Superintendencia de Sociedades; conduct, transparency, and user protection obligations; AML/CFT strengthening; and enabling financial entities to offer banking products and payment access to registered PSAVs. The bill does not grant digital assets legal tender status or classify them as securities. Legislative progress was ongoing through 2025-2026.
- **Scope:** VASP registration, AML/CFT, user protection, financial access for crypto platforms.

### DIAN Resolution 000240 — OECD CARF Reporting Framework
- **Date enacted / effective:** Published 24 December 2025; 2026 designated as first full observation year
- **Summary:** Colombia's tax authority (DIAN) introduced mandatory transaction reporting requirements for crypto exchanges and service providers, aligned with the OECD's Cryptoasset Reporting Framework (CARF). Under the resolution, Cryptoasset Service Providers (PSCAs) must disclose detailed user transaction data to the DIAN for all crypto transactions exceeding COP 50,000 (approximately USD equivalent). The framework is designed to prevent crypto-based tax evasion and expands Colombia's automatic exchange of information commitments under the Multilateral Agreement.
- **Scope:** Tax reporting, transaction data disclosure, CARF alignment.

### UIAF AML Obligations for Virtual Assets
- **Date enacted / effective:** Resolución 314 de 2021; updated via Resolución 84 de 2022
- **Summary:** The Financial Intelligence Unit (UIAF) imposed mandatory reporting obligations on all entities providing virtual asset services in Colombia. VASPs must report suspicious operations, maintain transaction records, and submit to the UIAF's SIREL online reporting system. A 2026 reporting calendar was published for ongoing compliance.
- **Scope:** AML/KYC, suspicious transaction reporting, SIREL system compliance.

### La Arenera — SFC Regulatory Sandbox (Historical)
- **Date enacted / effective:** Pilot launched 2021; concluded December 2023
- **Summary:** Colombia's supervisory sandbox allowed selected banks and exchanges to operate cash-in/cash-out operations in a controlled environment under SFC oversight. The sandbox concluded in December 2023 without producing a standalone comprehensive VASP law, but generated significant supervisory experience that informed subsequent policy developments, including Decree 1297 and Bill 510.
- **Scope:** Bank-exchange connectivity, pilot compliance, supervised experimentation.

### Securities Market Law — Decree 2555/2010
- **Date enacted / effective:** 2010 (baseline); applied to digital assets on case-by-case basis
- **Summary:** The SFC considers that tokens representing securities must comply with this framework. Issuance, marketing, and trading of security-like tokens require prior SFC authorisation and compliance with transparency and registration rules. Classification remains fact-specific and functional.
- **Scope:** Security token classification, issuance, disclosure.

## Licensing Requirements
Colombia's PSAV registry (created by Decree 1297) is the primary registration requirement for virtual asset service providers. As of March 2026, Bill 510/2025 proposing a more comprehensive statutory licence was before Congress. Depending on the activity profile, securities law, payments law, and banking-interface constraints may impose additional obligations. Banks cannot directly offer crypto products under current SFC rules.

## Taxonomy
Virtual assets are not legal tender and are generally treated as digital/intangible assets for tax purposes (DIAN treats them as intangibles). Securities analysis applies where tokens convey investment rights. Classification is functional and case-specific.

## Key Compliance Requirements
AML/KYC (UIAF), PSAV registration (SFC/Decree 1297), tax reporting under DIAN Resolution 000240 (effective 2026), suspicious transaction reporting via SIREL, banking-interface controls, and legal classification analysis. Platforms must also maintain KYC thresholds and reconcile user identity with DIAN's Single Taxpayer Registry.

## Recent Developments

**2025–2026 saw Colombia's regulatory posture shift materially:**

- **Decree 1297 PSAV Registry fully operational (2024–2026):** The registry became the baseline compliance requirement for crypto platforms serving Colombian users. All exchanges, custodians, and transfer services must maintain active registration.
- **Bill 510/2025 filed (February 2025):** A comprehensive VASP law was introduced in Congress by Holland & Knight-advised legislators, proposing to transfer PSAV supervision to the Superintendencia de Sociedades and provide crypto platforms with formal access to banking and payment infrastructure.
- **DIAN CARF implementation (December 2025):** Colombia became one of the first Latin American jurisdictions to formally implement OECD CARF-aligned mandatory crypto transaction reporting. 2026 is the first active enforcement year.
- **Colombia ranks 29th globally in crypto adoption:** Over 5 million Colombians are estimated to hold digital assets, driving regulatory urgency.
- **SFC public consultation on crypto standards (ongoing):** The SFC continued stakeholder engagement on future conduct standards for exchanges and custodians operating under the PSAV framework.

## Impact on RFP/Procurement
Colombian buyers need platforms that combine strong AML and reporting infrastructure with adaptability to an evolving legislative framework. The DIAN Resolution 000240 compliance requirement makes automated transaction reporting a near-mandatory platform capability for 2026. Vendors should demonstrate audit-grade traceability, configurable KYC thresholds, and readiness for future SFC conduct standards under Bill 510 if enacted.
