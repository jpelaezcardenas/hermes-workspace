# Americas Digital Asset Regulations

Last updated: 2026-03-22

## Regional Overview

The Americas remain highly fragmented. The United States still relies on overlapping federal and state regimes, Canada uses securities-law and AML supervision with tighter stablecoin and custody expectations, and Latin American markets generally combine fintech/payment rules, AML obligations, and selective virtual-asset licensing. Offshore centres such as Cayman and Bermuda are more explicit, with dedicated virtual-asset statutes and licensing frameworks.

For procurement, regional buyers need flexible rule engines, jurisdiction-aware customer onboarding, and strong legal-entity segregation. The same token flow may trigger money-transmission, securities, derivatives, or payments obligations depending on the country. Vendors that can separate spot crypto, tokenized securities, custody, sanctions screening, and travel-rule evidence at the configuration layer are materially easier to deploy across the Americas.
