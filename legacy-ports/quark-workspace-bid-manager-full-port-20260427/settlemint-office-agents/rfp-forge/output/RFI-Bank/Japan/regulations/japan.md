# Japan

Last updated: March 2026

**Country:** Japan
**Regulatory Authority:** Japan Financial Services Agency (JFSA) and JVCEA self-regulatory framework

## Key Regulations

### Payment Services Act (PSA)
- **Date enacted / effective:** Core crypto framework since 2017; materially amended 2020, 2022, and Act No. 66 of 2025 (enacted June 2025)
- **Summary:** The PSA regulates crypto-asset exchange service providers and, after successive amendments, electronic payment instruments including certain stablecoins. Japan uses the PSA to impose registration, segregation of customer assets, custody controls, and detailed operational rules on exchanges and custodians. Act No. 66 of 2025 introduced cross-border collection agency rules, strengthened domestic custody order powers, and formalized the intermediary business category for electronic payment instruments and crypto-assets. Reserve-asset management rules for trust-type stablecoins were also made more flexible.
- **Scope:** Spot crypto-asset exchange, custody, brokerage, stablecoin-related services, customer asset protection, intermediary business.

### Financial Instruments and Exchange Act (FIEA)
- **Date enacted / effective:** Applied to tokenized securities and derivatives, including 2020 updates; further expansion expected 2026
- **Summary:** The FIEA captures electronically recorded transferable rights and other tokenized securities. The Financial System Council Working Group, in its December 10, 2025 report, recommended shifting broad crypto-asset regulation (currently under the PSA) to the FIEA, treating crypto-assets as investment instruments rather than payment instruments. If enacted in 2026, this would bring insider-trading bans, stricter custody audits, and enhanced disclosure obligations, bringing crypto rules closer to traditional securities law.
- **Scope:** Security tokens, collective investment interests, derivatives, investment intermediation; prospective reclassification of general crypto-assets.

### Stablecoin and electronic payment instrument framework
- **Date enacted / effective:** Stablecoin rules established via 2022 PSA amendment (enforced June 2023); reserve rules further developed through 2025
- **Summary:** Japan introduced one of the clearest legal stablecoin frameworks by treating many fiat-linked tokens as "electronic payment instruments" issuable by banks, trust companies, or money transfer businesses. In January 2026, the FSA opened a public consultation on draft stablecoin reserve rules under Act No. 66 of 2025, specifying that reserve assets backing yen-pegged tokens held in trust structures must consist of high-rated foreign bonds (credit category 1-2 or higher) from issuers with at least ¥100 trillion in outstanding issuance. Consultation closed February 27, 2026. JPYC launched Japan's first legally recognized yen-backed stablecoin in October 2025 under the revised framework.
- **Scope:** Stablecoins, reserve management, issuance, intermediary services, settlement use cases.

## Licensing Requirements
Crypto-asset exchange service providers must register under the PSA. Security-token platforms or intermediaries may need FIEA registration. Stablecoin issuance and distribution may require bank, trust, or fund-transfer permissions plus electronic payment instrument service registration. A new intermediary business type for crypto-asset and electronic payment instrument transactions was formally created by the 2025 PSA amendment.

## Taxonomy
Japan distinguishes crypto-assets under the PSA, electronic payment instruments (notably certain stablecoins), and tokenized securities/collective interests under the FIEA. The pending legislative shift to the FIEA for general crypto-assets would further refine this taxonomy. This remains one of the clearest statutory frameworks globally.

## Key Compliance Requirements
Customer-asset segregation, cold-wallet management, trust/guarantee arrangements, AML/KYC, travel-rule implementation, cybersecurity, incident reporting, token screening, and local-language disclosures are essential. JVCEA self-regulatory rules add practical detail beyond statute. New in 2025-2026: stablecoin reserve quality standards, domestic custody order compliance, intermediary registration, and enhanced risk disclosures for bank and insurance subsidiaries offering crypto services.

## Recent Developments

**December 10, 2025:** FSA Financial System Council Working Group released a landmark report recommending the reclassification of crypto-assets from the PSA to the FIEA, treating them as investment instruments. The proposed reforms include mandatory liability reserves for exchanges (sized by trading volume and incident history), insider-trading bans, and enhanced disclosure. Legislation expected to be submitted to parliament in the 2026 ordinary session. Crypto ETF launch targeted for 2028; tax reform proposals include reducing crypto gains tax from the current maximum of 55% to a flat 20%.

**December 16, 2025:** FSA published draft amendments to the Funds Transfer Ordinance and related ordinances implementing the 2025 PSA amendment, covering cross-border collection agency exemptions, domestic custody order scope, and reserve asset flexibility for trust-type stablecoins. Comment period closed early 2026.

**January 27, 2026:** FSA opened public consultation on stablecoin reserve rules — only foreign bonds meeting strict credit (category 1-2 or higher) and issuance size (≥¥100 trillion) criteria qualify as collateral. Updated supervisory guidelines for banks, insurance companies, and their subsidiaries require explicit risk disclosures when offering crypto intermediation services. Foreign stablecoin issuers seeking Japanese market access must confirm they will not directly target retail users in Japan.

**October 2025:** JPYC launched Japan's first legally recognized yen-backed stablecoin under the revised PSA framework. Japan's three megabanks (MUFG, SMBC, Mizuho) are also piloting stablecoin and tokenized deposit projects.

## Impact on RFP/Procurement
Japanese procurement strongly rewards platforms with rigorous custody segregation, lifecycle controls, and support for both PSA and FIEA use cases. Enterprise buyers will expect support for the evolving FIEA reclassification — procurement decisions made in 2026 must anticipate that crypto-asset regulation may shift to a securities law model. Vendors should also demonstrate readiness for stablecoin reserve compliance, domestic custody order capabilities, and the new intermediary business category.
