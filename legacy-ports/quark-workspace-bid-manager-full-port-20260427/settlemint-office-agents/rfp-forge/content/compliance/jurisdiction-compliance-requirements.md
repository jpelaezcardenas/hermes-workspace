# Jurisdiction-Specific Regulatory Compliance Requirement Patterns

Use these blocks when the buyer is based in or subject to the regulatory framework of the specified jurisdiction. Select the relevant jurisdiction block and adapt to the institution's specific supervisory regime.

---

## EU — Markets in Crypto-Assets (MiCA) Compliance

**Applicable to:** Institutions operating in EU/EEA, or serving EU clients, or marketing crypto-assets to EU investors.

- **CR-EU-01:** The proposed solution shall support compliance with MiCA token issuance requirements, including the requirement to publish a crypto-asset white paper for public token offerings, and maintain accurate and transparent disclosure of token economics, governance, and reserve/redeemability mechanisms.
- **CR-EU-02:** The Respondent shall describe how the platform supports the MiCA market abuse prevention regime, including requirements for insider dealing, unlawful disclosure of inside information, and market manipulation prohibitions applicable to crypto-assets.
- **CR-EU-03:** The solution shall support the MiCA mandatory wallet provider licensing regime, including user verification, wallet segregation, and transaction monitoring capabilities required under Articles 12–14 of Regulation (EU) 2023/1114.
- **CR-EU-04:** The Respondent shall demonstrate how the platform implements the MiCA sustainable disclosure requirements, including environmental and climate-related impact reporting for token issuers where applicable.
- **CR-EU-05:** The proposed solution shall support the MiCA orderly wind-down provisions, including the ability to transfer client assets to another authorized provider or return them to clients within the regulatory timelines.

## United Kingdom — FCA Regulatory Regime

**Applicable to:** Institutions regulated by the Financial Conduct Authority, or marketing cryptoassets to UK consumers.

- **CR-UK-01:** The proposed solution shall support compliance with the UK cryptoasset financial promotions regime, including the requirement that all marketing communications are fair, clear, and not misleading, and that risk warnings are displayed in accordance with FCA rules.
- **CR-EU-01:** The solution shall support the FCA's registration requirement for cryptoasset businesses under the Money Laundering Regulations, including user due diligence, ongoing monitoring, and suspicious activity reporting capabilities.
- **CR-UK-02:** The Respondent shall describe how the platform addresses the FCA's consumer duty expectations, including ensuring cryptoasset products provide fair value, target appropriate customer segments, and include appropriate risk disclosures.
- **CR-UK-03:** The proposed solution shall support the FCA's market abuse regime as extended to cryptoassets, including capabilities for detecting and reporting suspected market manipulation and insider dealing.
- **CR-UK-04:** The Respondent shall demonstrate how the platform supports the UK regulatory sandbox and innovation path, including controlled testing environments, investor exposure limits, and exit planning as required by FCA guidance.

## United States — SEC and CFTC Regulatory Framework

**Applicable to:** Institutions operating in US markets, or offering cryptoasset products to US persons, or subject to US federal securities or commodities law.

- **CR-US-01:** The proposed solution shall support the determination and documentation of whether a digital asset constitutes a security under the Howey test, and provide capabilities for securities law compliance including registration exemption analysis, investor accreditation verification, and restriction enforcement.
- **CR-US-02:** The solution shall support CFTC-regulated derivatives and commodities activities, including the designation of a compliant digital commodity exchange, compliance with the Commodity Exchange Act, and swap dealer/SEF registration considerations.
- **CR-US-03:** The Respondent shall describe how the platform addresses the SEC's broker-dealer registration requirements as applicable to cryptoasset trading, custody, or market-making activities, including regulatory capital, books and records, and reporting obligations.
- **CR-US-04:** The proposed solution shall support compliance with the Bank Secrecy Act and FinCEN money laundering regulations, including Currency Transaction Reports, Suspicious Activity Reports, and Travel Rule (FATF Recommendation 16) compliance for cryptoasset transfers.
- **CR-US-05:** The solution shall incorporate state-level money transmitter licensing (MTL) compliance capabilities, including the ability to manage multi-state licensure requirements, bond/capital requirements tracking, and state-specific consumer protection compliance.
- **CR-US-06:** The Respondent shall demonstrate how the platform supports SEC climate-related disclosure requirements as they apply to digital asset issuers, including material climate risk disclosures and GHG emissions reporting where applicable.

## Singapore — MAS Digital Payment Token Services

**Applicable to:** Institutions regulated by the Monetary Authority of Singapore, or providing Digital Payment Token (DPT) services to Singapore persons.

- **CR-SG-01:** The proposed solution shall support compliance with the MAS Payment Services Act (PSA) licensing regime for Digital Payment Token services, including customer due diligence, technology risk management, and cybersecurity requirements under the PSA and MAS Guidelines.
- **CR-SG-02:** The solution shall support the MAS Notice PSN02 on Prevention of Money Laundering and Countering the Financing of Terrorism, including customer screening, transaction monitoring, and suspicious transaction reporting capabilities.
- **CR-SG-03:** The Respondent shall describe how the platform addresses the MAS stablecoin regulatory framework, including reserve asset requirements, redemption rights for stablecoin holders, and audit requirements under the Stablecoin Payment Services regime.
- **CR-SG-04:** The proposed solution shall support the MAS Technology Risk Management Guidelines, including IT governance, system availability, security controls, and software development lifecycle requirements.
- **CR-SG-05:** The solution shall incorporate the MAS Consumer Protection Measures, including cooling-off period requirements, risk disclosure standards, and restrictions on marketing to the general public for speculative digital assets.

## Hong Kong — SFC and HKMA Regulatory Regime

**Applicable to:** Institutions regulated by the Securities and Futures Commission or Hong Kong Monetary Authority, or marketing virtual asset trading services to Hong Kong investors.

- **CR-HK-01:** The proposed solution shall support compliance with the SFC Virtual Asset Service Provider (VASP) licensing regime, including requirements for safe custody of client assets, segregation of client money, and market surveillance capabilities.
- **CR-HK-02:** The solution shall support the Anti-Money Laundering Ordinance (AMLO) requirements as they apply to virtual asset service providers, including customer due diligence, record-keeping, and suspicious transaction reporting to the Joint Financial Intelligence Unit.
- **CR-HK-03:** The Respondent shall describe how the platform addresses the HKMA stablecoin issuer licensing regime, including reserve management, redemption requirements, and supervisory cooperation arrangements.
- **CR-HK-04:** The proposed solution shall support the SFC's investor protection requirements, including suitability assessments for virtual asset trading, risk disclosure, and restrictions on retail marketing of virtual assets.
- **CR-HK-05:** The solution shall incorporate the cross-border VASP arrangement capabilities required under the Travel Rule (FATF Recommendation 16), including originator/beneficiary information collection and transmission for virtual asset transfers.

## Switzerland — FINMA Regulatory Framework

**Applicable to:** Institutions operating under Swiss financial market supervision, or issuing tokens to Swiss investors, or conducting cryptoasset activities from Switzerland.

- **CR-CH-01:** The proposed solution shall support compliance with Swiss Financial Market Supervisory Authority (FINMA) guidance on blockchain/DLT-based financial market infrastructure, including the application of existing financial market laws to token issuances.
- **CR-CH-02:** The solution shall support the Swiss Anti-Money Laundering Act (AMLA) requirements, including customer due diligence, beneficial owner identification, enhanced due diligence for high-risk customers, and suspicious activity reporting.
- **CR-CH-03:** The Respondent shall describe how the platform addresses the Swiss Federal Act on Collective Investment Schemes (CISA) as it applies to tokenized collective investment schemes, including authorization requirements and investor protection provisions.
- **CR-CH-04:** The proposed solution shall support the Swiss banking and securities law requirements for token issuance and trading, including the distinction between payment tokens, utility tokens, and asset tokens, and the corresponding regulatory treatment under Swiss law.
- **CR-CH-05:** The solution shall incorporate the FINMA requirements for stablecoin issuers, including reserve asset segregation, audit requirements, and investor redemption rights.

## Japan — FSA Cryptoasset Exchange Service Providers

**Applicable to:** Institutions registered with the Financial Services Agency of Japan, or providing cryptoasset exchange services to Japanese residents.

- **CR-JP-01:** The proposed solution shall support compliance with the Japanese Payment Services Act (PSA) cryptoasset exchange service provider (CAESP) registration requirements, including segregation of customer assets, trading system security, and business continuity planning.
- **CR-JP-02:** The solution shall support the JFSA stablecoin regulatory framework, including the requirement that stablecoin issuance be restricted to banks, registered money transfer service providers, and trust companies, with appropriate custody and redemption mechanisms.
- **CR-JP-03:** The Respondent shall describe how the platform addresses the Japanese AML/CFT requirements under the Act on Prevention of Transfer of Criminal Proceeds, including customer identification, transaction monitoring, and suspicious activity reporting to the NRA.
- **CR-JP-04:** The proposed solution shall incorporate the JFSA investor protection requirements, including restrictions on margin trading, leverage, and marketing to retail investors, as well as mandatory disclosure of cryptoasset-related risks.
- **CR-JP-05:** The solution shall support the Japanese Act on Specified Commercial Transactions governing cryptoasset sales, including requirements for accurate representation of token characteristics, cooling-off rights, and dispute resolution mechanisms.

## UAE — ADGM, DIFC, and VARA Regulatory Frameworks

**Applicable to:** Institutions operating in or from the Abu Dhabi Global Market, Dubai International Financial Centre, or mainland UAE under the Virtual Assets Regulatory Authority, or marketing virtual asset services to UAE-based clients.

- **CR-UAE-01:** The proposed solution shall support compliance with the ADGM Financial Services Regulatory Authority (FSRA) Guidance on Crypto-Asset Activities, including the requirement for authorized activities relating to virtual assets, investor classification under the Financial Services and Markets Regulations, and associated regulatory permission categories.
- **CR-UAE-02:** The solution shall support the DIFC Digital Assets Law (DIFC Law No. 2 of 2024) requirements, including compliance with classification of digital assets as Digital Securities or Accepted Virtual Assets, authorized firm obligations for trading and custody, and associated DFSA rulebook provisions.
- **CR-UAE-03:** The Respondent shall describe how the platform supports the Dubai Virtual Assets Regulatory Authority (VARA) Virtual Asset Service Provider (VASP) licensing regime, including compliance with VARA's seven activity-specific rulebooks (Advisory, Broker-Dealer, Exchange, Lending/Borrowing, Payments/Remittances, Management/Investment, and Custody).
- **CR-UAE-04:** The proposed solution shall support UAE Central Bank AML/CFT requirements applicable to licensed VASPs and financial institutions, including customer due diligence, enhanced due diligence for high-risk clients, goAML suspicious activity reporting, and compliance with the National Risk Assessment framework.
- **CR-UAE-05:** The solution shall demonstrate support for the UAE Travel Rule requirements as mandated by CBUAE and VARA, including originator/beneficiary information collection and counterparty VASP verification for virtual asset transfers above threshold amounts.

## Australia — ASIC and AUSTRAC Regulatory Framework

**Applicable to:** Institutions operating in Australia, or providing digital asset products and services to Australian clients, or subject to Australian financial services law.

- **CR-AU-01:** The proposed solution shall support compliance with the Australian Securities and Investments Commission (ASIC) treatment of digital assets as financial products where they meet the definition of managed investment schemes, derivatives, or securities under the Corporations Act 2001, including product disclosure obligations, design and distribution requirements, and financial services licence (AFSL) holder responsibilities.
- **CR-AU-02:** The solution shall support the Australian Digital Asset Exchange (DAX) regulatory framework under the Treasury's crypto-asset licensing reforms, including compliance with custody, holding, and exchange service obligations once enacted, and demonstrate readiness to accommodate mandatory licensing requirements as they come into force.
- **CR-AU-03:** The Respondent shall describe how the platform addresses the AUSTRAC Digital Currency Exchange (DCE) registration requirements, including customer identification and verification (IDVC) procedures, ongoing transaction monitoring, and suspicious matter reporting under the Anti-Money Laundering and Counter-Terrorism Financing Act 2006.
- **CR-AU-04:** The proposed solution shall support the Australian Privacy Act 1988 (Privacy Act) requirements as they apply to digital asset platform operations, including data collection minimisation, cross-border transfer obligations, breach notification requirements, and the privacy rights of Australian users under the Australian Privacy Principles (APPs).
- **CR-AU-05:** The solution shall demonstrate compliance capabilities for the ASIC product intervention and design-and-distribution obligations (DDO) as they apply to digital asset products marketed to retail clients, including target market determination (TMD) documentation, distribution condition enforcement, and review trigger monitoring.

## Canada — CSA, FINTRAC, and OSFI Regulatory Framework

**Applicable to:** Institutions regulated by Canadian securities administrators or the Office of the Superintendent of Financial Institutions, or providing crypto-asset services to Canadian clients, or subject to Canadian AML/ATF obligations.

- **CR-CA-01:** The proposed solution shall support compliance with Canadian Securities Administrators (CSA) requirements applicable to crypto-asset trading platforms and issuers, including compliance with CSA Staff Notice 21-327 and National Instrument 31-103 obligations for restricted dealers, investment dealers, and registered platforms operating under prospectus exemptions or marketplace registration.
- **CR-CA-02:** The solution shall support compliance with FINTRAC reporting and record-keeping obligations for Money Services Businesses (MSBs) engaged in virtual currency exchange and transfer services, including Large Virtual Currency Transaction Reports (LVCTR), Suspicious Transaction Reports (STR), and 24-hour rule aggregation logic under the Proceeds of Crime (Money Laundering) and Terrorist Financing Act.
- **CR-CA-03:** The Respondent shall describe how the platform addresses provincial securities commission requirements across key Canadian jurisdictions, including the Ontario Securities Commission (OSC) requirements under OSC Rule 13-502, the Autorité des marchés financiers (AMF) crypto-asset guidelines for Québec, and the British Columbia Securities Commission (BCSC) requirements for crypto-asset issuers and trading platforms.
- **CR-CA-04:** The proposed solution shall demonstrate compliance with the Office of the Superintendent of Financial Institutions (OSFI) Guideline B-10 on Third-Party Risk Management and OSFI's Integrity and Security Guideline, including vendor due diligence, concentration risk controls, and cloud/technology outsourcing requirements applicable to federally regulated financial institutions (FRFIs) procuring digital asset infrastructure.
- **CR-CA-05:** The solution shall support the FATF Recommendation 16 Travel Rule requirements as implemented under FINTRAC's virtual currency regulations, including originator/beneficiary information collection for domestic and cross-border virtual currency transfers above the CAD 1,000 threshold, counterparty VASP verification, and compliant information transmission protocols.

## Saudi Arabia — SAMA and CMA Regulatory Framework

**Applicable to:** Institutions regulated by the Saudi Central Bank (SAMA) or the Capital Market Authority (CMA), or operating in or from the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia, or participating in Saudi Vision 2030 digital finance initiatives.

- **CR-SA-01:** The proposed solution shall support compliance with the Saudi Central Bank (SAMA) Digital Currencies Policy and its fintech regulatory sandbox requirements, including compliance with SAMA's requirements for pilot participation, participant eligibility criteria, consumer protection commitments, and obligations to exit the sandbox within the permitted window unless full licensing is obtained.
- **CR-SA-02:** The solution shall support compliance with the Capital Market Authority (CMA) digital securities and tokenized asset framework, including the CMA's requirements for offering tokenized securities under the Securities Regulations, applicable prospectus and disclosure obligations, and CMA Fintech Lab participation conditions for tokenized securities issuers.
- **CR-SA-03:** The Respondent shall describe how the platform addresses Saudi AML/CFT obligations under the Anti-Money Laundering Law (Royal Decree M/20) and its Implementing Regulations, including customer identification and verification, politically exposed person (PEP) screening, transaction monitoring, and reporting obligations to the Saudi Financial Intelligence Unit (SAFIU).
- **CR-SA-04:** The proposed solution shall demonstrate compliance with SAMA's Cybersecurity Framework and the National Cybersecurity Authority (NCA) Essential Cybersecurity Controls (ECC-1:2018) and Cloud Cybersecurity Controls (CCC-1:2020), including classification of assets by sensitivity level, access management, vulnerability management, and incident response and reporting obligations.
- **CR-SA-05:** The solution shall support data residency and sovereignty requirements applicable to Saudi Arabia, including compliance with the Saudi Personal Data Protection Law (PDPL), data localisation obligations for sensitive personal and financial data, cross-border data transfer restrictions, and requirements for consent management and data subject rights under SDAIA guidelines.

## South Korea — FSC and FIU Regulatory Framework

**Applicable to:** Institutions regulated by the Financial Services Commission (FSC) or operating virtual asset service provider businesses in South Korea, or providing digital asset products and services to Korean clients.

- **CR-KR-01:** The proposed solution shall support compliance with the Virtual Asset User Protection Act (VAUPA, effective July 2024), including mandatory segregation of user deposits in separately managed accounts at a financial institution, cold storage requirements for a minimum percentage of user virtual assets, insurance or reserve requirements against hacking or system failure losses, and unfair trading prohibitions covering market manipulation, wash trading, and use of undisclosed material information.
- **CR-KR-02:** The solution shall support the Financial Intelligence Unit (KoFIU) registration and reporting requirements for Virtual Asset Service Providers (VASPs) under the Act on Reporting and Using Specified Financial Transaction Information, including real-name verified account linkage with Korean commercial banks, suspicious transaction reporting (STR), currency transaction reporting (CTR) for transactions above the KRW threshold, and ongoing customer due diligence with enhanced measures for high-risk users.
- **CR-KR-03:** The Respondent shall describe how the platform addresses the FSC tokenized securities framework under the Electronic Securities Act and the Capital Markets Act, including the distinction between security tokens subject to securities regulation and utility or payment tokens subject to VAUPA, issuance and distribution compliance for tokenized securities through authorised electronic registration institutions, and investor suitability requirements for retail participation in tokenized security offerings.
- **CR-KR-04:** The proposed solution shall demonstrate compliance with the Korea Information Security Management System (ISMS) certification requirements mandated for VASPs by the Korea Internet and Security Agency (KISA), including information protection management, access control, cryptographic controls, incident response procedures, and annual recertification obligations. The platform shall also address the Personal Information Protection Act (PIPA) requirements, including data minimisation, purpose limitation, cross-border transfer restrictions requiring adequacy determination or consent, and data subject rights including the right to access, correction, and deletion.
- **CR-KR-05:** The solution shall support the FATF Travel Rule requirements as implemented under KoFIU's inter-VASP information sharing regulations, including originator and beneficiary identification for virtual asset transfers above the KRW 1,000,000 threshold, counterparty VASP verification, and compliance with the Korean Travel Rule solution interoperability requirements for domestic and cross-border transfers.

## Cross-Jurisdictional Considerations

- **CR-GLOBAL-01:** The Respondent shall describe how the platform supports multi-jurisdictional deployment, including the ability to apply jurisdiction-specific compliance rules based on user location, token classification, or transaction routing.
- **CR-GLOBAL-02:** The solution shall demonstrate capabilities for managing conflicting regulatory requirements across jurisdictions, including conflicting AML rules, data localization mandates, and cross-border transfer restrictions.
- **CR-GLOBAL-03:** The proposed solution shall support jurisdiction-aware sanctions and counterparty screening controls, including the ability to apply country-specific sanctions lists, beneficial ownership screening, and transfer restrictions based on the legal location of the issuer, investor, wallet provider, custodian, or settlement counterparty. The Respondent shall describe how screening decisions, overrides, and escalations are logged for audit purposes where a transaction spans multiple regulatory perimeters.
