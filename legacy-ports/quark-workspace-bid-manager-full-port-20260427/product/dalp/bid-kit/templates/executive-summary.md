# Executive Summary Template

## Instructions for the Agent

This template produces a standalone 3 to 4 page executive summary. It should be comprehensible without reading the full proposal. Apply all three rules files. Remember: no numbered headings, no em/en dashes, no AI-tell markers. Write as a senior blockchain solution architect who understands the client's domain.

Load `references/product-overview.md` and `references/differentiators.md` as primary sources. Load `references/use-cases-projects.md` if the client's asset class matches a past engagement.

---

## Document Format

```
# Executive Summary

## {Client Name}, {RFP Title / Engagement Description}

**Submitted by:** SettleMint
**Date:** {YYYY-MM-DD}
```

---

## Understanding Your Objectives

*1 to 2 paragraphs. Paraphrase the client's stated requirements and business context. Show that SettleMint understands the specific challenges this RFP aims to solve. Reference the client's industry, regulatory environment, and strategic goals where known.*

**Guidance**: Do not simply repeat the RFP text. Synthesize the requirements into a coherent narrative that demonstrates insight into the underlying business problem. Open with an acknowledgment that doing tokenization the right way is difficult, and frame the client's RFP as a smart approach to solving that difficulty.

---

## Our Proposed Solution

*2 to 3 paragraphs. Present DALP as the answer. Focus on the most relevant capabilities for this specific RFP.*

Structure:
- One-sentence positioning statement for DALP.
- How the platform's lifecycle management approach addresses the core challenge.
- The specific capabilities most relevant to this client (select from: DvP settlement, vault custody, yield management, compliance enforcement, multi-asset support, enterprise deployment, API-first integration).
- How the deployment model aligns with the client's infrastructure requirements.

Include a small mermaid diagram if it helps illustrate the solution approach for this specific client.

---

## Key Differentiators

*4 to 6 bullet points most relevant to this RFP. For each, one sentence stating the differentiator and one sentence explaining why it matters for this client.*

Select from:

- Unified lifecycle platform (not an integration project)
- Compliance embedded at the protocol level (ERC-3643)
- Production-ready operational infrastructure (DvP, vaults, yield management)
- Seven asset classes from day one
- API-first architecture for enterprise integration
- Enterprise-grade deployment and control
- Integrated observability without separate monitoring contracts
- Proven market data infrastructure

---

## Implementation Approach

*1 paragraph covering:*

- Phased methodology: discovery, configuration, integration, testing, go-live
- Timeline for first asset type (4 to 8 weeks)
- Timeline for additional asset types (2 to 4 weeks)
- Enterprise integration runs in parallel

## Expected Outcomes

*4 to 6 measurable outcomes the client can expect. Select and tailor from:*

- T+0 atomic settlement with zero counterparty risk
- Structurally impossible compliance violations through ERC-3643 transfer-path enforcement
- Significant reduction in operational overhead for corporate actions
- Real-time holdings visibility replacing quarterly statements
- Single platform replacing multiple vendor relationships
- Production observability from day one
- API-driven integration with existing institutional systems

---

## Why SettleMint

*1 paragraph. Closing statement reinforcing SettleMint's fit. Reference relevant project experience from use-cases-projects.md if the asset class matches. Mention willingness to demonstrate capabilities through a proof of concept.*

---

## Length Target

3 to 4 pages total. Every sentence should earn its place. If an evaluator reads only this document, they should understand: what the client needs, what SettleMint proposes, why it is the right choice, and what outcomes to expect.
