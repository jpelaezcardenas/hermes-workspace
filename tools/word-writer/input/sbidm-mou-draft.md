# Memorandum of Understanding

**Between SettleMint NV and SBI Digital Markets Pte. Ltd.**

---

## Memorandum of Understanding

**DATED** [DATE]

**BETWEEN:**

**(1)** **SETTLEMINT NV**, a limited liability company organised and existing under the laws of Belgium with commercial registration number 0661.674.810 and having its registered address at Arnould Nobelstraat 30-202, 3000 Leuven, Belgium ("**SettleMint**"); and

**(2)** **SBI DIGITAL MARKETS PTE. LTD.**, a company incorporated and existing under the laws of Singapore, having its registered office at [REGISTERED ADDRESS, SINGAPORE] ("**SBIDM**");

(each a "**Party**" and collectively the "**Parties**").

---

## Background

(A) SettleMint develops and operates the Digital Asset Lifecycle Platform ("**DALP**"), a regulated digital asset infrastructure platform enabling financial institutions to issue, manage, service, and settle tokenised financial instruments across their full lifecycle.

(B) SBIDM is a digital assets and financial technology business within the SBI Group, focused on advancing digital asset adoption across capital markets and financial services in Asia and globally.

(C) The Parties wish to collaborate to explore and develop business opportunities in the Territory in connection with DALP and digital asset solutions for capital markets, financial institutions, and regulated entities.

(D) This MOU records certain terms that the Parties have agreed in principle, on the basis set out below, relating to digital assets, tokenised securities, and associated infrastructure and services.

---

## 1. Term

1.1 This MOU shall become effective and valid from the date of execution by both Parties (the "**Effective Date**") and shall continue to be in effect for a period of twelve (12) months (the "**Term**"), unless terminated earlier pursuant to clause 8 of this MOU.

1.2 The Term may be extended by mutual written agreement of the Parties prior to its expiry.

---

## 2. Objectives

2.1 The Parties intend to explore and develop the following areas of collaboration:

(a) Identifying and pursuing business opportunities for DALP-powered digital asset solutions in the Territory, with a focus on tokenised financial instruments including but not limited to bonds, funds, deposits, and equity;

(b) Jointly engaging with prospective clients and financial institutions in the Territory to introduce and demonstrate DALP capabilities for regulated digital asset issuance, management, and servicing;

(c) Collaborating on thought leadership, market education, and industry engagement in connection with digital asset infrastructure and capital markets innovation;

(d) Exploring joint participation in regulatory sandbox programmes, proof-of-concept initiatives, and pilot projects with financial institutions in the Territory;

(e) Leveraging SBIDM's market access, networks, and capital markets expertise in combination with SettleMint's platform capabilities to develop integrated digital asset offerings.

---

## 3. Roles and Responsibilities

### 3.1 SettleMint shall:

(a) Provide SBIDM with access to technical information, demonstrations, and documentation relating to DALP;

(b) Appoint a designated technical and commercial contact to support joint business development activities;

(c) Provide pre-sales technical support, proof-of-concept environments, and hands-on demonstrations for prospective clients identified jointly with SBIDM;

(d) Provide sales training and enablement materials to SBIDM as reasonably required to support the Parties' joint marketing activities;

(e) Work with SBIDM in good faith to identify suitable commercial structures for any client engagements arising from the collaboration.

### 3.2 SBIDM shall:

(a) Leverage its networks, market presence, and relationships to identify and introduce potential clients and partners for DALP in the Territory;

(b) Appoint a designated commercial contact to lead joint business development activities with SettleMint;

(c) Allocate appropriate resources to facilitate technical understanding and validation of DALP with prospective clients;

(d) Participate in training, demos, and enablement programmes offered by SettleMint;

(e) Work with SettleMint in good faith to co-develop business proposals and commercial structures for opportunities identified jointly.

### 3.3 Coordination

The Parties shall each appoint one representative to coordinate the activities contemplated by this MOU and to assess progress on a regular basis. The Parties shall aim to meet (in person or by video conference) no less than once per quarter during the Term.

---

## 4. Territory

4.1 The Territory for the purposes of this MOU shall be [Asia-Pacific / Singapore and Japan / to be agreed], unless extended or varied by mutual written agreement of the Parties.

---

## 5. Exclusivity

5.1 **[OPTIONAL, FOR REVIEW]** During the Term, the Parties may agree in writing to operate on an exclusive or preferential basis in respect of specific client segments, geographies, or use cases within the Territory. Any such exclusivity arrangement shall be separately documented and agreed in writing by both Parties.

5.2 Unless and until a separate exclusivity arrangement is executed, nothing in this MOU shall restrict either Party from engaging with, partnering with, or entering into arrangements with any third party in the Territory or elsewhere.

---

## 6. Cost and Expenses

6.1 Each Party shall bear its own costs and expenses in connection with this MOU and any activities contemplated hereunder, unless otherwise agreed in advance and in writing by the Parties.

6.2 The commercial terms applicable to any specific client engagement, joint project, or definitive agreement arising from the collaboration shall be separately negotiated and documented by the Parties.

---

## 7. Definitive Agreements

7.1 This MOU is intended to provide a framework for the Parties to explore and develop their collaboration. Neither Party shall be obliged to enter into any definitive commercial agreement arising from the activities contemplated herein.

7.2 Any definitive agreement for the provision of services, licensing of software, joint delivery of projects, or other commercial arrangements shall be subject to separate negotiation, agreement, and execution by the Parties.

---

## 8. Termination

8.1 Either Party may terminate this MOU at any time by giving not less than thirty (30) days' written notice to the other Party.

8.2 Termination of this MOU shall not affect any rights or obligations that have accrued prior to termination, nor shall it affect the continued operation of clauses 9 (Confidentiality), 12 (Governing Law and Disputes), and any other provision that by its nature is intended to survive termination.

---

## 9. Confidentiality

9.1 Each Party shall treat and keep all Confidential Information of the other Party as secret and confidential. Neither Party shall, without the prior written consent of the disclosing Party, use the other Party's Confidential Information other than for the purposes of this MOU, or disclose it to any third party.

9.2 "**Confidential Information**" means any information relating to a Party, its business, technology, clients, or operations that is disclosed under or in connection with this MOU and which is either designated as confidential or which a reasonable person would regard as confidential in the circumstances of its disclosure.

9.3 The obligations of confidentiality in this clause shall not apply to information that:

(a) is or becomes publicly available other than through breach of this MOU;

(b) was already known to the receiving Party at the time of disclosure, free of any obligation of confidence;

(c) is independently developed by the receiving Party without reference to the disclosing Party's Confidential Information;

(d) is required to be disclosed by law, regulation, or order of a competent court or authority, provided that the receiving Party gives the disclosing Party as much advance notice as reasonably practicable.

9.4 The obligations under this clause shall survive termination or expiry of this MOU for a period of two (2) years.

---

## 10. Intellectual Property

10.1 Nothing in this MOU shall transfer, assign, or grant any rights in the intellectual property of either Party. Each Party retains full ownership of all intellectual property it owned prior to or developed independently of this collaboration.

10.2 Any intellectual property jointly developed by the Parties under this MOU shall be subject to separate written agreement between the Parties.

---

## 11. Non-Binding Nature

11.1 With the exception of clauses 9 (Confidentiality), 10 (Intellectual Property), 12 (Governing Law), 13 (Notices), and 15 (Entire Agreement), this MOU is not and shall not be legally binding on either Party. It records the mutual intentions of the Parties and shall not impose any legal obligation or liability.

11.2 Nothing in this MOU shall be construed to create or imply a joint venture, partnership, agency, fiduciary duty, or any other legal relationship between the Parties beyond that expressly set out herein.

---
