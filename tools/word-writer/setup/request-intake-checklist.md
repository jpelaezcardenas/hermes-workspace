# Request Intake Checklist

Use this before drafting. It prevents the most common failure mode: building the wrong document well.

## Confirm the assignment
- What is the document meant to achieve: inform, align, recommend, record, or request a decision?
- Who is the primary audience?
- Is this internal-only, client-facing, partner-facing, or mixed?
- What is the expected delivery format: markdown source only, DOCX, or both?
- Is there a deadline or meeting it supports?

## Confirm the document type
Choose the lightest format that still does the job.
- Executive memo for a decision, ask, or recommendation
- Report for multi-section analysis with evidence
- Structured briefing for situational awareness and implications
- Q&A pack for anticipated stakeholder questions
- Meeting summary for decisions, follow-ups, and accountability
- Status update for recurring progress, risks, and asks
- Research summary for synthesis of source material
- Operating note for rules, process guidance, or working instructions

## Confirm source quality
- Are source files present in `input/`?
- Are there conflicting inputs that need prioritization?
- Are claims traceable to a source, request note, or clearly labeled inference?
- Does the document need DALP or product truth verification?

## Confirm sensitivity
- Does the document include confidential names, metrics, customer details, or deal information?
- Does the stated audience actually need that detail?
- Should examples or identifiers be generalized?

## Confirm structure before writing
- What should the first page accomplish?
- Does the reader need a TOC, or would it just add weight?
- Which sections are mandatory?
- Which sections should be omitted to keep the document tight?

## Stop and reroute if needed
Do not continue in Word Writer if the document is actually:
- a proposal, bid, or persuasive response to win work
- an RFP, RFQ, RFI, or procurement questionnaire for others to answer
- a press release, media Q&A pack, or interview prep set
