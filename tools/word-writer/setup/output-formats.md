# Output Formats

## Rule
Choose the format that best serves the reader's task. Do not default everything into a report.

## Report
Best for:
- multi-section analysis
- evidence-led findings
- recommendations requiring context

Typical choice:
- medium to long
- often includes TOC
- may use appendix

## Executive Memo
Best for:
- decisions
- directional recommendations
- leadership communication

Typical choice:
- short
- usually no TOC
- recommendation-led opening

## Structured Briefing
Best for:
- fast situational understanding
- change updates
- implications and response framing

Typical choice:
- concise
- usually no TOC unless long

## Q&A Document
Best for:
- anticipated stakeholder questions
- internal enablement
- factual reference packs

Typical choice:
- direct answer first
- TOC only if long or multi-theme

## Meeting Summary
Best for:
- documenting decisions
- assigning follow-ups
- preserving discussion outcomes

Typical choice:
- no TOC
- actions and owners are mandatory when relevant

## Questionnaire / Response Pack
Best for:
- structured prompts
- concise answers
- comparison or status responses

Typical choice:
- answer blocks or tables
- avoid proposal-style persuasion

## Status Update
Best for:
- recurring internal updates
- steering check-ins
- delivery progress snapshots

Typical choice:
- no TOC
- one screen per section if possible
- decisions needed should be explicit, not implied

## Research Summary
Best for:
- synthesizing source material
- explaining findings and implications
- packaging research into a sendable document

Typical choice:
- TOC only if long or multi-theme
- include scope or method so confidence is legible
- separate sourced findings from interpretation

## Operating Note / Guidance Memo
Best for:
- documenting rules, process guidance, and working instructions
- clarifying exceptions and escalation paths
- preserving reusable operating guidance in a DOCX handoff

Typical choice:
- no TOC unless long and reference-heavy
- keep guidance concrete and owner-oriented
- include exceptions explicitly
