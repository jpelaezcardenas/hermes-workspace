# Canada

Last updated: March 2026

**Country:** Canada
**Regulatory Authority:** Canadian Securities Administrators (CSA), CIRO, FINTRAC, provincial securities regulators

## Key Regulations

### Securities-law registration framework for crypto trading platforms
- **Date enacted / effective:** Developed through 2021-2025
- **Summary:** Canadian regulators have used securities law to bring many crypto trading platforms into restricted-dealer, marketplace, and related registration pathways. Undertakings and terms/conditions have imposed detailed requirements around custody, client assets, leverage, and permitted tokens.
- **Scope:** Trading platforms, custody, staking, dealer/marketplace style activity.

### PCMLTFA / FINTRAC money services business regime
- **Date enacted / effective:** Virtual currency dealer obligations effective 2020
- **Summary:** Crypto businesses dealing in virtual currency are subject to Canadian AML/CFT obligations through FINTRAC. This includes registration, customer due diligence, recordkeeping, suspicious transaction reporting, and travel-rule style information obligations.
- **Scope:** AML/CFT, exchange, transfer, recordkeeping and reporting.

### CSA stablecoin/value-referenced crypto asset guidance
- **Date enacted / effective:** Major notices updated through 2023-2025
- **Summary:** Canadian regulators have taken a cautious approach to stablecoins, often calling them value-referenced crypto assets and permitting access only on restrictive terms. This is one of the stricter retail-access positions among major markets.
- **Scope:** Stablecoins, token listing, platform product governance.

## Licensing Requirements
Platforms commonly need securities-law registration/exemptive relief plus FINTRAC MSB registration. Custody, staking, derivatives, and stablecoin activities can trigger additional undertakings and restrictions, and provincial coordination remains important.

## Taxonomy
Canada does not rely on a simple token taxonomy; many platform positions are mediated through concepts such as crypto contracts, investment contracts, securities, and value-referenced crypto assets. Functional analysis matters more than marketing labels.

## Key Compliance Requirements
AML/KYC, travel rule, qualified-custodian arrangements, client-asset segregation, limits on proprietary use of client assets, token due diligence, and detailed reporting to regulators are central. Buyers should also expect strong governance around staking and stablecoin access.

## Recent Developments
2024-2025 saw tighter expectations for registered CTPs and continued scrutiny of offshore access. Canada remains one of the most prescriptive markets for platform governance, even without a standalone crypto act.

## Impact on RFP/Procurement
Canadian procurement heavily favors institutional controls, segregated custody, and evidence that product permissions can be constrained jurisdiction-by-jurisdiction. Platforms must support restrictive token policies and detailed supervisory reporting to pass enterprise review.
