# Cayman Islands

Last updated: March 2026

**Country:** Cayman Islands
**Regulatory Authority:** Cayman Islands Monetary Authority (CIMA)

## Key Regulations

### Virtual Asset (Service Providers) Act (VASP Act)
- **Date enacted / effective:** Initial law 2020; phased implementation thereafter
- **Summary:** The Cayman Islands created a dedicated statutory framework for virtual asset service providers and virtual asset issuances. The regime is explicit about what constitutes a virtual asset, what activities require registration or licensing, and how supervision applies.
- **Scope:** Virtual asset issuance, exchange, transfer, custody, and related services.

### AML Regulations and Guidance Notes
- **Date enacted / effective:** Ongoing
- **Summary:** Cayman overlays virtual asset regulation with strong AML/CFT duties, including customer due diligence, sanctions screening, suspicious activity reporting, and governance expectations. These apply alongside the VASP Act rather than instead of it.
- **Scope:** AML/KYC, risk assessment, sanctions, reporting.

### Securities Investment Business Act / other financial laws
- **Date enacted / effective:** Ongoing
- **Summary:** Where a tokenized product functions as a security, fund interest, or investment product, Cayman's existing financial-services legislation can also apply. This is highly relevant for institutional tokenization structures using Cayman vehicles.
- **Scope:** Tokenized funds, securities, investment business, institutional structuring.

## Licensing Requirements
VASP registration or licensing is required depending on the business activity and scale. Entities must also assess whether securities, funds, trust, or other financial-services permissions are needed for institutional structures.

## Taxonomy
Cayman statutorily defines “virtual assets” and VASP activities but still preserves separate treatment where a token is effectively a security or fund interest. Institutional structuring therefore requires both crypto and financial-services analysis.

## Key Compliance Requirements
AML/KYC, governance, custody/safeguarding, recordkeeping, outsourcing oversight, sanctions controls, and periodic reporting to CIMA are central. Institutional buyers often also require evidence around bankruptcy remoteness and administrator/trustee interfaces.

## Recent Developments
2024-2025 has been about continued maturation of licensing expectations and the interaction between digital assets and fund structures. Cayman remains attractive for sophisticated institutional and cross-border structures.

## Impact on RFP/Procurement
Cayman procurement is often driven by institutional structuring needs rather than retail-market features. Vendors that can support tokenized fund/share classes, strong custody controls, and regulated-entity reporting have the most procurement credibility.
