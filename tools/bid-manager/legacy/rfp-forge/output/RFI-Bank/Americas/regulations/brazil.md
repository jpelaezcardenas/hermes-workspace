# Brazil

Last updated: March 2026

**Country:** Brazil
**Regulatory Authority:** Central Bank of Brazil and CVM

## Key Regulations

### Law No. 14,478/2022 (Brazilian crypto framework)
- **Date enacted / effective:** Enacted 21 December 2022; implemented via later decrees
- **Summary:** Brazil created a federal legal framework for virtual asset service providers, defining virtual assets and establishing a basis for authorization and supervision. The law is principle-based, with detailed licensing rules delegated to the designated regulator.
- **Scope:** Virtual asset services, AML obligations, consumer protection, market organization.

### Decree No. 11,563/2023
- **Date enacted / effective:** Effective 2023
- **Summary:** This decree designated the Central Bank of Brazil as the main regulator for most virtual asset service providers, while preserving the CVM's authority over tokenized securities. It clarifies who enterprises must engage depending on product design.
- **Scope:** Regulatory allocation between central bank and securities regulator.

### CVM guidance on tokenized securities
- **Date enacted / effective:** Ongoing, including Parecer de Orientação and enforcement practice
- **Summary:** Brazilian securities law applies where tokens represent investment contracts or other securities. That means tokenization projects involving receivables, funds, or debt instruments must assess CVM perimeter risk separately from the crypto law.
- **Scope:** Security tokens, investment contracts, tokenized capital-markets products.

## Licensing Requirements
A VASP authorization regime under the Central Bank is the core path for non-securities crypto activity. Activities involving securities may require CVM licensing or operation through regulated market participants; AML registration and consumer-protection compliance are also essential.

## Taxonomy
Brazil defines “virtual assets” broadly but excludes national currency, foreign currency, electronic money, loyalty rewards, and securities. Classification therefore turns on whether the token is effectively a payment/crypto instrument or a regulated security/product.

## Key Compliance Requirements
AML/KYC, suspicious activity reporting, governance, custody/safeguarding, transaction monitoring, consumer disclosures, and cybersecurity are key. The split between Central Bank and CVM oversight makes token classification and legal-entity design especially important.

## Recent Developments
2024-2025 has been about operationalizing the authorization regime and coordinating prudential expectations. Brazil is also expanding institutional tokenization discussions through the central bank and securities sandbox ecosystem.

## Impact on RFP/Procurement
Brazilian enterprise buyers need vendors that can separate non-security virtual-asset flows from security-token workflows, provide Portuguese-ready compliance artifacts, and support future central-bank-grade licensing expectations. Flexible classification and reporting are procurement differentiators.
