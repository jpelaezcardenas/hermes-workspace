# Italy

Last updated: March 2026

**Country:** Italy
**Regulatory Authority:** CONSOB, Bank of Italy, and OAM

## Key Regulations

### Italian OAM VASP register
- **Date enacted / effective:** Operational from 2022
- **Summary:** Italy required certain virtual-asset service providers to register with the Organismo Agenti e Mediatori (OAM), creating a national baseline before MiCA. The register emphasized local establishment and reporting obligations.
- **Scope:** Exchange, wallet, and related VASP activities.

### MiCA
- **Date enacted / effective:** Applicable from 2024/2025
- **Summary:** MiCA is replacing the national registration-first model with a broader authorization regime. CONSOB and the Bank of Italy remain relevant where products overlap with securities, payments, or prudential concerns.
- **Scope:** CASP activities, public offers, stablecoins, conduct and safeguarding.

### Italian fintech / DLT experimentation rules
- **Date enacted / effective:** Ongoing since 2022 with updates
- **Summary:** Italy has also explored DLT and fintech pilots for tokenized financial instruments. These are separate from pure crypto-asset services and matter for enterprise issuance or post-trade design.
- **Scope:** Tokenized securities, fintech experimentation, institutional DLT use cases.

## Licensing Requirements
Legacy OAM registration has been a bridge, but MiCA CASP authorization is the long-term requirement for crypto services. Activities involving securities, payments, or e-money may require additional authorization from CONSOB or the Bank of Italy.

## Taxonomy
Italy adopts the MiCA taxonomy for crypto-assets while applying financial-instrument analysis to tokens that confer securities-type rights. Tokenized bonds or fund interests are not treated the same as utility or payment tokens.

## Key Compliance Requirements
AML/KYC, travel rule, periodic reporting, governance, custody controls, and consumer disclosures are key. Buyers should also expect attention to outsourcing governance and local reporting capabilities.

## Recent Developments
2024-2025 has been a transition period from OAM registration to MiCA, with ongoing regulatory interest in tokenized finance use cases. Italy is supportive of institutional experimentation but remains documentation-heavy.

## Impact on RFP/Procurement
Italian enterprise buyers usually want strong reporting, modular classification, and the ability to support both retail-crypto compliance and institutional tokenized-instrument workflows. Procurement often depends on legal configurability and regulator-friendly audit output.
