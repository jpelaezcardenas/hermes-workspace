# Sweden

Last updated: March 2026

**Country:** Sweden
**Regulatory Authority:** Finansinspektionen

## Key Regulations

### AML supervision of virtual asset providers
- **Date enacted / effective:** Pre-MiCA national regime
- **Summary:** Sweden supervised many crypto firms under AML rules before MiCA, concentrating on customer due diligence, monitoring, and governance. That experience informs how firms are being assessed during the MiCA transition.
- **Scope:** AML/KYC oversight for crypto exchange/custody style services.

### MiCA
- **Date enacted / effective:** Applicable from 2024/2025
- **Summary:** MiCA brings a broader licensing and market-conduct framework to Sweden. Supervisory focus is expected to remain conservative, especially for client-asset protection and operational resilience.
- **Scope:** CASP activities, issuers, stablecoins, conduct and safeguarding.

### Swedish financial-services perimeter
- **Date enacted / effective:** Ongoing
- **Summary:** Where a token qualifies as a financial instrument or e-money, ordinary financial-services legislation applies instead of MiCA. That keeps classification analysis central for tokenized securities projects.
- **Scope:** Securities, e-money, investment services, tokenized instruments.

## Licensing Requirements
MiCA authorization is required for CASP activities. Additional permissions may be needed if the business touches investment services, payments, or e-money; firms should not assume all tokenized products sit within MiCA.

## Taxonomy
Sweden follows the MiCA distinction between ARTs, EMTs, and other crypto-assets, while separating out financial instruments and e-money. Rights attached to the token remain the deciding factor.

## Key Compliance Requirements
AML/KYC, travel-rule controls, safeguarding, governance, outsourcing oversight, security controls, and incident reporting are the main requirements. Nordic buyers also tend to expect strong documentation and operational transparency.

## Recent Developments
2024-2025 has been about migrating from AML-first oversight into MiCA. Sweden remains cautious on retail speculation but open to well-governed institutional use cases.

## Impact on RFP/Procurement
Procurement in Sweden usually favours platforms with disciplined governance, minimal operational complexity, and clear evidence of segregation and auditability. Enterprise buyers tend to reject stacks that feel consumer-crypto-first.
