# Europe Digital Asset Regulations

Last updated: March 2026

## Regional Overview

Europe is moving toward a more harmonized digital-asset regime than any other major region. The EU's Markets in Crypto-Assets Regulation (MiCA) creates a passportable licensing and conduct framework for crypto-asset service providers, while the Transfer of Funds Regulation (TFR) extends the travel rule across the bloc. Even with MiCA, national competent authorities still matter because authorization, supervision, AML controls, securities-law perimeter questions, and prudential expectations remain locally administered.

For procurement, the key question in Europe is whether a platform must support MiCA-grade governance, TFR travel-rule workflows, local outsourcing expectations, and country-specific securities/custody overlays. Buyers operating across multiple EU states usually prefer vendors that can support CASP-style controls, passporting evidence packs, audit trails, segregation of client assets, and configurable onboarding for both regulated crypto-assets and tokenized financial instruments. Non-EU jurisdictions such as the UK and Switzerland remain strategically important but require separate legal and operating models.
