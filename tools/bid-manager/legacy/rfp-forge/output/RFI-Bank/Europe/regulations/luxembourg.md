# Luxembourg

Last updated: March 2026

**Country:** Luxembourg
**Regulatory Authority:** Commission de Surveillance du Secteur Financier (CSSF)

## Key Regulations

### Luxembourg blockchain laws
- **Date enacted / effective:** 2019, 2021, and later refinements
- **Summary:** Luxembourg amended its financial legislation in stages to recognize DLT-based issuance, transfer, and account-keeping of securities. That makes it relevant for regulated tokenization programs, especially for funds, bonds, and structured products.
- **Scope:** Tokenized securities, account-keeping, settlement and financial instruments.

### MiCA
- **Date enacted / effective:** Applicable from 2024/2025
- **Summary:** MiCA now governs most non-security crypto-asset issuance and services in Luxembourg. CSSF authorization expectations are important because the jurisdiction is a major domicile for fund and institutional structures.
- **Scope:** CASP activities, public offerings, stablecoins and service provider conduct.

### AML/CFT framework
- **Date enacted / effective:** Ongoing
- **Summary:** Luxembourg overlays digital-asset activity with strong AML/CFT expectations and outsourcing governance. This is especially relevant for institutional service providers and cross-border groups using Luxembourg entities.
- **Scope:** AML/KYC, sanctions, outsourcing, governance.

## Licensing Requirements
CASP authorization is needed for MiCA-regulated activities. Separate permissions may be required for investment firms, credit institutions, e-money institutions, fund service providers, or central account keepers depending on structure and asset type.

## Taxonomy
Luxembourg uses the MiCA framework for crypto-assets while keeping tokenized securities and fund interests within the established financial-services perimeter. Classification therefore turns heavily on whether the token embeds securities rights.

## Key Compliance Requirements
AML/KYC, travel rule, governance, outsourcing controls, client-asset protection, and documentation of legal classification are core. Institutional buyers also expect support for audit evidence, recordkeeping, and integration with existing fund or trustee operations.

## Recent Developments
2024-2025 activity has centred on MiCA transition planning and continued interest in institutional tokenization structures. Luxembourg remains particularly attractive for fund-linked and capital-markets implementations rather than retail-crypto models.

## Impact on RFP/Procurement
Procurement in Luxembourg typically emphasizes evidence packs, model governance, and compatibility with existing regulated-entity operating models. Vendors need to support both MiCA-style crypto operations and tokenized-securities workflows without forcing separate platforms.
