# Denmark

Last updated: March 2026

**Country:** Denmark  
**Regulatory Authority:** Danish Financial Supervisory Authority (Finanstilsynet)

## Key Regulations

### Markets in Crypto-Assets Regulation (MiCA) - Regulation (EU) 2023/1114
- **Date enacted / effective:** Entered into force 29 June 2023; fully applicable 30 December 2024
- **Summary:** MiCA is now the core framework for crypto-asset issuance, public offerings, admission to trading, and crypto-asset services in Denmark. Finanstilsynet's MiCA page states that the regime covers issuers, crypto-asset service providers (CASPs), investor protection, and stablecoin-related controls.
- **Scope:** CASP licensing, ARTs, EMTs, white papers, conduct, governance, safeguarding, and market integrity.

### Danish transition law implementing MiCA pathways
- **Date enacted / effective:** Act No. 481 of 22 May 2024; transition from 30 December 2024
- **Summary:** Denmark introduced national transition rules to operationalize MiCA domestically. Finanstilsynet states that existing crypto-asset service providers that were already operating before 30 December 2024 and that applied by that date may continue for up to 18 months unless their application is refused.
- **Scope:** Transitional continuation, application timing, and national implementation hooks.

### Existing Danish financial-services perimeter
- **Date enacted / effective:** Ongoing
- **Summary:** Denmark still applies ordinary financial-services, payment-services, e-money, AML, and securities rules where a tokenized product falls outside MiCA or is carved out of it. Token labels do not override legal classification.
- **Scope:** Tokenized securities, e-money, payments, and regulated financial instruments.

## Licensing Requirements
CASP authorization is now the main requirement for MiCA-covered services in Denmark. Finanstilsynet has published application forms for CASP authorization and for Article 60 MiCA notifications by certain already-authorized financial institutions. Financial institutions using the Article 60 route must notify Finanstilsynet at least 40 working days before starting the crypto-asset service.

## Taxonomy
Denmark follows the MiCA split between asset-referenced tokens (ARTs), e-money tokens (EMTs), and other crypto-assets. Financial instruments, deposits, structured products, and some e-money arrangements remain outside MiCA and must be assessed under existing sectoral law.

## Key Compliance Requirements
Core requirements include AML/KYC, sanctions screening, travel-rule controls, governance, incident handling, outsourcing oversight, white-paper governance where relevant, safeguarding of client crypto-assets and funds, and evidencing operational resilience. Danish buyers will expect implementation evidence, not just policy claims.

## Recent Developments
- **30 June 2024:** MiCA rules for ARTs and EMTs started applying across the EU.
- **5 July 2024:** Finanstilsynet published a market outreach notice on future crypto-asset services in the EU after MiCA, asking relevant financial firms whether they planned to rely on MiCA Article 60.
- **30 December 2024:** Full MiCA rules for CASPs and most other crypto-asset services became applicable in Denmark.
- **26 February 2025:** Finanstilsynet updated its dedicated practice page for the Danish crypto-asset market, including application mechanics, Article 60 notifications, and white-paper notification expectations.
- **2025-2026:** Denmark has shifted from an AML-first posture into full MiCA supervision, with a practical focus on authorization readiness and transitional compliance.

## Impact on RFP/Procurement
For Danish procurements, the real question is whether the platform is ready for supervised MiCA operations, not whether it merely supports wallets or token issuance. Buyers will care about authorization support, control evidence, recordkeeping, and the ability to localize white-paper, notification, and safeguarding workflows.
