# Hong Kong

Last updated: March 2026

**Country:** Hong Kong
**Regulatory Authority:** Securities and Futures Commission (SFC) and HKMA

## Key Regulations

### AMLO virtual asset service provider regime
- **Date enacted / effective:** Full licensing regime effective 1 June 2023
- **Summary:** Hong Kong's VASP regime under the Anti-Money Laundering and Counter-Terrorist Financing Ordinance created a clear licensing path for centralized virtual asset trading platforms. The SFC supervises licensing, token admission, custody, governance, and retail access conditions.
- **Scope:** Virtual asset trading platforms, custody, retail access, token listing and conduct.

### SFC/HKMA tokenization circulars
- **Date enacted / effective:** Expanded through 2023-2025
- **Summary:** Hong Kong has explicitly supported tokenized securities, tokenized deposits, and other institutional digital-asset use cases through a series of regulatory circulars. This is important because Hong Kong separates speculative-crypto oversight from broader tokenization development.
- **Scope:** Security tokens, tokenized products, custody, distribution and institutional tokenization.

### Stablecoin regime development
- **Date enacted / effective:** Legislation/policy advanced through 2024-2025
- **Summary:** Hong Kong has been building a dedicated stablecoin framework, especially for fiat-referenced issuers. This adds another layer for enterprise procurement where settlement or treasury use cases are in scope.
- **Scope:** Stablecoins, payments, reserve governance, issuer supervision.

## Licensing Requirements
Centralized trading platforms need SFC licensing under the VASP regime. Tokenized securities, brokerage, asset management, or advisory activity may also require traditional securities licences; stablecoin issuance will likely require a dedicated authorization path.

## Taxonomy
Hong Kong distinguishes virtual assets, securities tokens, and other regulated financial products based on legal rights and activity. Retail access is tightly managed, especially for platform-listed tokens.

## Key Compliance Requirements
AML/KYC, custody segregation, insurance/compensation arrangements, token due diligence, travel-rule support, governance, cybersecurity, and local reporting are essential. Hong Kong buyers also expect strong support for institutional tokenization workflows.

## Recent Developments
2024-2025 has seen Hong Kong deepen its pro-institutional tokenization strategy while keeping tight supervision on trading venues. The market is increasingly attractive for enterprise projects that need regulatory clarity and capital-markets alignment.

## Impact on RFP/Procurement
Hong Kong procurement rewards vendors that can handle both virtual-asset platform controls and tokenized-securities lifecycle management. Buyers will often ask for detailed control mapping to SFC expectations, not just generic crypto compliance claims.
