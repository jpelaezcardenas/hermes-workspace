# Malaysia

Last updated: March 2026

**Country:** Malaysia
**Regulatory Authority:** Securities Commission Malaysia and Bank Negara Malaysia

## Key Regulations

### Capital Markets and Services (Prescription of Securities) (Digital Currency and Digital Token) Order
- **Date enacted / effective:** Initial order 2019 with ongoing effect
- **Summary:** Malaysia formally brought certain digital currencies and digital tokens into the capital-markets perimeter, allowing the securities regulator to license and supervise relevant platforms and offerings. This gives the jurisdiction a clear legal footing for digital-asset market infrastructure.
- **Scope:** Digital asset exchanges, token offerings, capital-markets treatment.

### SC guidelines on Digital Asset Exchanges and fundraising
- **Date enacted / effective:** Updated over time through 2024-2025
- **Summary:** The SC has issued licensing, custody, listing, and operational requirements for recognized market operators dealing in digital assets. Malaysia therefore operates through a structured exchange-and-offering regime rather than an open-market approach.
- **Scope:** Exchange licensing, custody, token listings, fundraising platforms.

### BNM/payment and AML controls
- **Date enacted / effective:** Ongoing
- **Summary:** Bank Negara Malaysia remains important where products intersect with payments, e-money, or broader AML/CFT obligations. Enterprises should expect coordination between capital-markets and payment-system considerations.
- **Scope:** AML/KYC, payments perimeter, sanctions, operational controls.

## Licensing Requirements
Digital asset exchanges and certain token fundraising platforms require SC authorization, often as recognized market operators. Activities with payment or banking features may trigger additional review under BNM rules.

## Taxonomy
Malaysia distinguishes digital currencies and digital tokens brought into the capital-markets perimeter, while still applying separate analysis for securities, payments, and e-money features. Rights and business model matter more than labels.

## Key Compliance Requirements
AML/KYC, listing due diligence, custody safeguards, customer disclosures, reporting, cybersecurity, and governance are central. Buyers also care about local-language support and regulator-facing documentation.

## Recent Developments
2024-2025 has been about refining exchange and fundraising supervision rather than rewriting the framework. Malaysia remains structured and relatively clear for regulated market operators.

## Impact on RFP/Procurement
Malaysian procurement rewards vendors with exchange-grade controls, token-governance workflows, and the ability to align digital-asset operations with broader capital-markets compliance. A purely crypto-native product without regulated-market discipline is a weak fit.
