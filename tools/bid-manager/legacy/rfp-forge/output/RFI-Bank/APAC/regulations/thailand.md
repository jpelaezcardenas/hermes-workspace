# Thailand

Last updated: March 2026

**Country:** Thailand
**Regulatory Authority:** Securities and Exchange Commission of Thailand (SEC) and Bank of Thailand (BOT)

## Key Regulations

### Digital Asset Business Emergency Decree B.E. 2561 (2018) — with 2025 amendments
- **Date enacted / effective:** Effective 2018; significantly amended by two Royal Decrees effective April 13, 2025
- **Summary:** Thailand's core digital-asset business regime covers exchanges, brokers, dealers, ICO portals, fund managers, and advisors. The April 2025 Royal Decree on Digital Asset Businesses (No. 2), B.E. 2568, fundamentally changed how Thailand regulates foreign crypto platforms by introducing an extraterritorial licensing requirement: any offshore platform targeting Thai residents must now obtain an SEC licence. The Ministry of Digital Economy and Society (MDES) was granted authority to block platforms that fail to comply. Criteria for "targeting Thai users" include offering services in Thai language, accepting Thai baht, using .th domains, or maintaining local staff.
- **Scope:** Digital asset business licensing, exchanges, brokers, dealers, ICO portals; extraterritorial reach over offshore platforms.

### SEC licensing, token listing, and approved-asset rules
- **Date enacted / effective:** Updated continuously through 2024-2025
- **Summary:** The Thai SEC maintains an approved token list — as of June 2025, only 35 tokens are permitted for trading on licensed exchanges. Stablecoins USDC and USDT were officially approved by the SEC on March 16, 2025, and added to the permitted cryptocurrency list. SEC rules govern investor suitability, custody, market conduct, and disclosure. As of August 2025, revised rules on ICO governance and licensing requirements came into effect to improve investor information and protection.
- **Scope:** Token listing, stablecoin approval, custody, investor access, market supervision.

### Bank of Thailand and payment restrictions
- **Date enacted / effective:** Ongoing; CBDC-exchange sandbox development announced 2026
- **Summary:** Thailand remains cautious about using crypto-assets as payments even while licensing trading and investment activity. Licensed exchanges cannot facilitate crypto payments for goods or services. However, the BOT and SEC are developing a pilot linking Thailand's CBDC with licensed exchanges by Q2 2026, which could enable controlled crypto payment use cases in the future.
- **Scope:** Payments restrictions, banking interface, CBDC sandbox, settlement use cases.

### Tax incentive for crypto gains — five-year personal income exemption
- **Date enacted / effective:** January 1, 2025 through December 31, 2029
- **Summary:** Thailand introduced a five-year personal income tax exemption on capital gains from the sale or exchange of cryptocurrencies and digital tokens conducted through licensed digital asset exchanges. This measure is intended to stimulate domestic trading activity and encourage investment through licensed channels.
- **Scope:** Tax, investor incentives, licensed exchange activity.

## Licensing Requirements
A Thai SEC licence (with Ministry of Finance approval) is required for covered digital-asset business activities. As of early 2026, nine exchanges hold licences including Bitkub and Zipmex. Offshore platforms targeting Thai users must obtain a Thai licence or face criminal prosecution (imprisonment of two to five years and/or fines) and platform blocking. Only nine licensed exchanges as of early 2026; unlicensed foreign platforms are blocked without warning.

## Taxonomy
Thailand distinguishes cryptocurrencies from digital tokens and further separates investment-token concepts from payment-like uses. The approved token list (35 tokens as of June 2025) reflects a controlled, high-quality-admission model rather than open markets. Stablecoins are now formally included in the approved list.

## Key Compliance Requirements
AML/KYC, investor suitability, token due diligence, custody controls, cybersecurity, local-language disclosures, and restrictions on payment claims are central. New in 2025-2026: extraterritorial licensing compliance for offshore platforms, USDC/USDT approval-based stablecoin handling, ICO governance compliance, and Travel Rule implementation.

## Recent Developments

**April 13, 2025:** Two Royal Decrees took effect, introducing extraterritorial licensing and MDES website-blocking authority. Five major offshore platforms (Bybit, 1000X, CoinEx, OKX, XT.COM) identified as operating illegally; blocking orders issued by June 28, 2025.

**March 16, 2025:** SEC officially approved USDC and USDT for use in digital asset transactions — first formal stablecoin recognition in Thailand.

**January 1, 2025:** Five-year personal income tax exemption on crypto gains from licensed exchanges entered into force.

**August 2025:** SEC revised ICO governance and licensing requirements for improved investor disclosures. Digital asset operators suspended nearly 48,000 mule accounts during 2025 enforcement actions.

**February 20, 2026:** SEC filed a criminal complaint with the Economic Crime Suppression Division against a licensed Thai digital asset broker and its overseas global trading platform for cooperatively operating an unlicensed cross-border exchange. Platform blocking ordered effective March 22, 2026.

**March 15, 2026:** SEC proposed the "Travel Rule" for digital asset operators — mandatory collection and transmission of sender/recipient data for every transaction, aligned with FATF standards. Consultation ongoing. Data retention minimum of five years required.

**2026 roadmap:** SEC finalising formal guidelines for crypto ETFs and futures trading on the Thailand Futures Exchange (TFEX). Thailand's first green token (ESG-linked) expected in 2026. CBDC-exchange sandbox pilot with BOT targeted for Q2 2026.

## Impact on RFP/Procurement
Thai procurement rewards vendors that can support strong product gating, suitability controls, and token-governance workflows. The 2025 extraterritorial enforcement shift means that cross-border procurement teams must verify platform licensing status. Travel Rule readiness is now a near-term compliance requirement. Procurement from enterprises with regional operations should verify whether their digital asset platforms can handle the SEC-approved token list, CBDC interface capabilities, and stablecoin compliance under the approved framework.
