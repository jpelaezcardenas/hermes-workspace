## 1. Invitation to Tender Notice

First Abu Dhabi Bank PJSC ("FAB"), headquartered in Abu Dhabi, United Arab Emirates, hereby invites qualified and experienced vendors to submit proposals for the provision, implementation, integration, support, and ongoing operation of a **Digital Asset Lifecycle Management Platform**.

FAB is seeking a production-grade platform that enables the Bank to consolidate and scale its digital asset operating model across multiple regulated asset classes. The successful bidder will be expected to support FAB's transition from point-solution execution in selected digital asset use cases to an integrated, enterprise-grade lifecycle platform supporting issuance, servicing, settlement, reporting, reconciliation, monitoring, and redemption across current and future digital asset products.

FAB has already established live market and regulatory experience in digital assets, including:

- production issuance of a distributed ledger technology (DLT)-based digital bond via **HSBC Orion**, listed on the **Abu Dhabi Securities Exchange (ADX)**;
- live operation of a **CBUAE-approved** dirham-backed stablecoin initiative under the **Digital Dirham Stablecoin Consortium (DDSC)** operating model; and
- internal and ecosystem participation across permissioned and emerging digital asset market infrastructures in the UAE.

FAB therefore requires a vendor capable of serving a sophisticated banking client with existing production exposure, regulatory scrutiny, and high operational rigor. This Invitation to Tender (ITT) is issued for a defined scope and is intended to result in a contractual award to one prime vendor, subject to FAB governance approvals.

Vendors responding to this ITT must demonstrate deep expertise in regulated digital asset operations, financial market infrastructure integration, enterprise banking architecture, information security, and Middle East regulatory delivery.

Nothing in this ITT shall be construed as a commitment by FAB to award a contract, reimburse bid costs, or proceed with any procurement outcome. FAB reserves the right to amend, suspend, cancel, clarify, negotiate, shortlist, or re-issue this ITT at its sole discretion.

## 3. Executive Background and Strategic Context

### 3.1 FAB profile

FAB is the largest bank in the United Arab Emirates by assets and operates as a systemically important banking institution with a broad presence across corporate banking, investment banking, treasury, markets, transaction banking, and international operations. FAB's digital asset agenda is not exploratory in the abstract; it is tied to practical market execution, regulatory engagement, and institutional operating readiness.

### 3.2 Existing digital asset production experience

FAB has already progressed beyond pilot rhetoric into live production activity. Notable milestones include:

- the execution of a DLT-based digital bond transaction through **HSBC Orion**, listed on **ADX**, representing a landmark regional issuance;
- operational go-live of the **DDSC** dirham-backed stablecoin initiative with **CBUAE approval**; and
- active participation in a broader Abu Dhabi digital asset ecosystem involving regulated infrastructure, consortium participants, and cross-institution coordination.

FAB's immediate challenge is therefore not whether digital assets are strategically relevant. That question has been answered. The problem is operational scale.

### 3.3 Why FAB is procuring now

FAB's current digital asset operating model includes asset-specific tooling, market-infrastructure-specific processes, and use-case-specific controls. That model is acceptable for first-wave execution, but it is not sufficient for scalable institutional operations across multiple asset classes and multiple transaction types.

FAB now requires a platform that can:

- unify operating processes across asset classes;
- reduce dependency on isolated issuance rails or point solutions;
- support both integration with and transition from current infrastructure where appropriate;
- provide consistent control, governance, and observability across digital asset operations; and
- allow the Bank to onboard future asset classes without re-architecting the control plane each time.

### 3.4 Strategic vision

FAB's intended end-state is a **multi-asset digital operations capability** in which the Bank can manage regulated digital bonds, stablecoins, tokenized cash-like instruments, and future tokenized instruments within a common enterprise platform supporting:

- product configuration and setup;
- issuance workflows and approval chains;
- regulatory and exchange-facing workflows;
- servicing and lifecycle event management;
- settlement orchestration and reconciliation;
- transaction surveillance and exception handling;
- treasury, finance, and operations integration; and
- controlled expansion to new jurisdictions, counterparties, and market models.

FAB does not seek a narrow tokenization front end. FAB seeks an institutional operating platform.

## 4. Procurement Objectives

The objectives of this procurement are to select a vendor capable of delivering a platform and associated services that meet the following outcomes:

1. **Consolidation:** Replace or rationalize fragmented digital asset operating processes with a unified lifecycle platform.
2. **Scalability:** Support current products and future asset classes without bespoke reimplementation for each new use case.
3. **Regulatory control:** Enable auditable operations aligned to CBUAE, ADGM/FSRA, ADX, and internal control requirements.
4. **Operational resilience:** Provide a highly available, enterprise-grade operating environment suitable for banking and payment-critical flows.
5. **Interoperability:** Work with current FAB infrastructure, market rails, custody and key management, data platforms, and reporting environments.
6. **Transition flexibility:** Support integration with HSBC Orion where commercially and technically appropriate, or present a credible pathway for functional replacement or coexistence.
7. **Multi-chain readiness:** Support permissioned and approved public-chain interaction models where required for product evolution.
8. **Commercial defensibility:** Deliver a transparent total cost model and implementation plan over a five-year horizon.

## 6. Business and Functional Requirements

### 6.1 Unified multi-asset operating model

The platform must provide a common operating model across multiple digital asset classes while allowing asset-specific workflows, controls, approvals, and lifecycle rules. FAB expects a single platform fabric rather than independent modules stitched together with inconsistent control logic.

### 6.2 End-to-end lifecycle support

At a minimum, FAB requires support for the following lifecycle stages:

1. product setup and configuration;
2. pre-issuance approvals and governance;
3. issuance execution;
4. participant and entitlement administration;
5. servicing and lifecycle events;
6. settlement coordination and confirmation;
7. reconciliation and reporting;
8. maturity, redemption, burn, or close-out.

### 6.3 Integration with current digital bond infrastructure

FAB currently operates with relevant dependence on **HSBC Orion** for digital bond issuance. Bidders must present one or both of the following clearly:

- a robust **integration/coexistence model** with HSBC Orion; and/or
- a robust **migration/replacement pathway** that minimizes business, regulatory, and operational disruption.

Proposals that ignore existing infrastructure realities will be scored poorly.

### 6.4 ADX listing workflow support

The platform must support, either natively or through configurable workflow and integration layers, the steps needed for digital instruments requiring ADX-related workflows, including documentation state transitions, listing readiness events, audit evidence, approvals, and interfaces to downstream reporting and confirmation processes.

### 6.5 Stablecoin operational controls

For regulated stablecoin operations, FAB requires operational support for:

- mint / burn workflows;
- role segregation and approval chains;
- transaction visibility;
- configurable controls and exception queues;
- reserve and treasury reconciliation data support;
- daily operational reporting;
- business continuity for high-availability transaction processing.

### 6.6 Cross-chain and network model

FAB expects support for operations involving:

- permissioned networks;
- ADI Chain or analogous approved ecosystem environments;
- approved public-chain interaction models, where allowed;
- cross-network state awareness and transaction traceability.

FAB does not require speculative support for every blockchain under the sun. It does require a credible institutional answer for approved multi-network operations.

### 6.7 Control, transparency, and auditability

The platform must provide strong administrative and operational transparency, including:

- full audit logging;
- user and role activity trails;
- transaction and workflow lineage;
- operational dashboards;
- exception tracking;
- evidence export for audit and compliance.

### 6.8 Operating model alignment

The successful bidder must support an implementation consistent with banking operating realities, including separation of duties across technology, operations, treasury, compliance, audit, and business ownership functions.

## 7. Detailed Solution Specification

### 7.1 Core platform expectations

FAB requires a unified digital asset lifecycle platform capable of functioning as the system of orchestration and control for regulated digital asset operations. The platform should not merely mint tokens; it should coordinate the institutional business process around those assets.

The proposed solution should include:

- product definition and asset template management;
- workflow configuration for approvals and state transitions;
- entitlement and permissioning controls;
- event scheduling and lifecycle processing;
- transaction orchestration;
- API and message integration services;
- reporting and dashboarding;
- operational tooling for support and exception handling;
- resilience, security, logging, and administration layers.

### 7.2 Product and instrument configuration

FAB expects the platform to support configurable instrument models for current and future products. Bidders shall describe how their platform handles:

- asset metadata and parameter models;
- issuance constraints and permissions;
- holder / participant rules;
- transfer conditions;
- corporate action or servicing event setup;
- redemption rules;
- environment-specific configuration promotion.

### 7.3 Workflow and approval framework

The platform must support configurable workflows across issuance, servicing, and redemption stages. Examples include:

- maker-checker or multi-stage approval models;
- legal/compliance/operations sign-offs;
- exception routing;
- time-bound approvals;
- escalation to designated approvers;
- evidence capture linked to workflow steps.

### 7.4 Operational dashboards and support tooling

FAB requires operational dashboards and tools suited to a bank's control environment, including:

- status of active issuances;
- pending approvals;
- failed or stalled transactions;
- mint/burn volumes;
- reconciliation status;
- service health and latency views;
- audit and control event summaries.

### 7.5 Reporting support

The platform should support extraction or native presentation of data needed for:

- CBUAE operational/regulatory reporting for stablecoin operations;
- internal management reporting;
- ADX-related workflow evidence;
- finance and treasury reconciliation;
- audit evidence packs;
- board and risk committee reporting where needed.

### 7.6 Coexistence, migration, and replacement pathway

FAB expects bidders to state plainly which of the following they support:

- coexistence with current issuance rails;
- phased migration by product or market segment;
- full platform replacement;
- hybrid control-plane architecture.

Bidders should provide transition principles, dependencies, cutover risk, and rollback options.
