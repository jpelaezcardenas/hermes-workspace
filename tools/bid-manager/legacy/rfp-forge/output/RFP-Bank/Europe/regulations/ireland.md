# Ireland

Last updated: March 2026

**Country:** Ireland
**Regulatory Authority:** Central Bank of Ireland

## Key Regulations

### Irish VASP registration under AML law
- **Date enacted / effective:** Implemented 2021
- **Summary:** Ireland brought virtual asset service providers into its AML regime ahead of MiCA. Registration has been used to test governance, beneficial ownership transparency, and customer due-diligence arrangements.
- **Scope:** AML supervision of exchange, transfer, and custody-related VASP activities.

### MiCA
- **Date enacted / effective:** Applicable from 2024/2025
- **Summary:** MiCA is now the main framework for non-security crypto-assets and CASP operations. Ireland is important because many international financial-services groups use Irish entities and expect clear regulatory perimeter mapping.
- **Scope:** Crypto-asset issuance, CASP licensing, stablecoins, conduct obligations.

### Irish financial-services and outsourcing expectations
- **Date enacted / effective:** Ongoing
- **Summary:** Where crypto activities intersect with payments, e-money, or investment services, traditional Irish prudential and outsourcing expectations continue to apply. This is especially relevant for enterprise and group-wide operating models.
- **Scope:** Outsourcing, governance, payments/e-money, regulated financial services.

## Licensing Requirements
MiCA CASP authorization is required for regulated crypto-asset services. Additional permissions may be required for e-money, payments, or investment services, and international groups should assess whether Irish regulated-entity outsourcing standards apply to the platform stack.

## Taxonomy
Ireland uses MiCA categories for crypto-assets and traditional EU securities analysis for transferable securities and other financial instruments. Stablecoins may fall under EMT or ART classifications depending on structure.

## Key Compliance Requirements
AML/KYC, travel-rule support, governance, safeguarding, outsourcing oversight, operational resilience, and strong recordkeeping are central. Irish-regulated groups are particularly sensitive to board reporting and third-party risk management.

## Recent Developments
2024-2025 has been shaped by MiCA implementation and increased supervisory focus on governance maturity for firms seeking Irish market access. Institutional tokenization interest is rising, but buyers remain conservative on control frameworks.

## Impact on RFP/Procurement
Irish procurement teams prioritize vendor governance, operating-model clarity, and compatibility with broader regulated-entity controls. A platform that can align to both MiCA and enterprise outsourcing requirements is materially easier to approve.
