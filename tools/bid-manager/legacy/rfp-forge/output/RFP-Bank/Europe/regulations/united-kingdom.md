# United Kingdom

Last updated: March 2026

**Country:** United Kingdom
**Regulatory Authority:** Financial Conduct Authority (FCA); HM Treasury; Bank of England for systemic stablecoins

## Key Regulations

### Money Laundering Regulations cryptoasset registration
- **Date enacted / effective:** Cryptoasset business perimeter effective 2020
- **Summary:** The UK currently regulates many crypto firms primarily through AML registration under the Money Laundering Regulations. That does not create a full prudential market regime, but it gives the FCA leverage over governance, onboarding, travel rule, and financial-crime controls.
- **Scope:** Exchange, custody, and certain cryptoasset business activities for AML supervision.

### Financial promotions regime for cryptoassets
- **Date enacted / effective:** Expanded 8 October 2023
- **Summary:** The UK brought qualifying cryptoassets into the financial promotions regime, making marketing approvals, risk warnings, cooling-off measures, and channel governance central compliance topics. This has had major downstream effects on onboarding flows and retail go-to-market models.
- **Scope:** Promotions of qualifying cryptoassets to UK consumers.

### Proposed broader UK cryptoasset framework / stablecoin legislation
- **Date enacted / effective:** Consultations and draft legislation advanced through 2024-2025
- **Summary:** HM Treasury and the FCA are building a phased regime covering trading venues, custody, intermediation, issuance, market abuse, and prudential conduct for cryptoasset activities. Stablecoins used in payments are being integrated through a separate but related payments and systemic-oversight track.
- **Scope:** Trading, custody, issuance, intermediation, payments stablecoins, market abuse.

## Licensing Requirements
Today many firms need FCA MLR registration and may also need permissions under FSMA, payments, or e-money rules depending on the product. The future UK regime is expected to require activity-specific authorizations for exchanges, custodians, and other intermediaries.

## Taxonomy
The UK often distinguishes exchange tokens, security tokens, utility tokens, and e-money tokens, but the legal answer depends on whether rights and obligations make the instrument a specified investment or e-money. Stablecoins used for payments are receiving dedicated treatment.

## Key Compliance Requirements
AML/KYC, travel-rule controls, promotions governance, operational resilience, safeguarding, client disclosures, outsourcing management, and sanctions screening are core. UK buyers also care about governance processes for approving communications and restricting non-compliant retail journeys.

## Recent Developments
2024-2025 saw the FCA continue strict supervision of registered firms while HM Treasury refined the long-term regulatory perimeter. The UK remains open to institutional tokenization, but the retail-facing market has become notably more controlled.

## Impact on RFP/Procurement
Procurement teams in the UK typically want strong financial-promotion controls, configurable customer eligibility logic, and the ability to operate now under AML registration while scaling into a fuller future authorization model. Platforms that cannot evidence governance, warnings, approvals, and segmentation are hard to buy.
