# Finland

Last updated: March 2026

**Country:** Finland  
**Regulatory Authority:** Finnish Financial Supervisory Authority (FIN-FSA)

## Key Regulations

### Act on Virtual Currency Providers
- **Date enacted / effective:** Effective 1 May 2019
- **Summary:** Finland established an early registration regime for virtual currency providers before MiCA. That framework focused on AML/CFT compliance, management fitness, customer-asset handling, and supervisory registration.
- **Scope:** Exchange, custody, and related virtual currency provider activity under the legacy Finnish model.

### Markets in Crypto-Assets Regulation (MiCA) - Regulation (EU) 2023/1114
- **Date enacted / effective:** Entered into force 29 June 2023; ART/EMT provisions applicable from 30 June 2024; full application from 30 December 2024
- **Summary:** MiCA is now Finland's main framework for crypto-asset issuance and service provision. FIN-FSA states that MiCA regulates issuance, public offers, admission to trading, and the provision of crypto-asset services, and that providing crypto-asset services in the EU requires authorization.
- **Scope:** CASP authorization, issuance, white papers, stablecoins, conduct, and market-abuse-related obligations.

### FIN-FSA authorization and white-paper supervision under MiCA
- **Date enacted / effective:** Operational from 2024/2025
- **Summary:** FIN-FSA has published detailed guidance on crypto-asset activities, authorization expectations, and crypto-asset white papers. It specifically addresses approval mechanics for ART white papers and requires prior FIN-FSA approval before issuance in relevant cases.
- **Scope:** ART issuance, white-paper approvals, notification of material changes, and publication requirements.

## Licensing Requirements
Crypto-asset service providers need MiCA authorization to operate in Finland unless they rely on a valid passport from another EU Member State. FIN-FSA maintains a public register for CASPs authorized in Finland and a notifications list for firms passporting in from elsewhere in the EU. Issuers of ARTs and EMTs face additional authorization or notification requirements depending on the model.

## Taxonomy
Finland now uses the MiCA categories of ARTs, EMTs, and other crypto-assets. Financial instruments and other carved-out products still fall under separate regimes, so legal classification remains critical for tokenized securities and hybrid products.

## Key Compliance Requirements
The practical compliance stack includes AML/KYC, sanctions controls, transaction monitoring, governance, operational resilience, incident handling, white-paper production, marketing controls, and audit-ready recordkeeping. FIN-FSA's materials make clear that documentation quality matters as much as product design.

## Recent Developments
- **30 June 2024:** MiCA provisions for ARTs and EMTs became applicable.
- **30 December 2024:** Full MiCA application began for CASPs and broader crypto-asset activity.
- **2025:** FIN-FSA published and expanded dedicated guidance on crypto-asset activities, including public-register mechanics, authorization requirements, and cross-border service provision.
- **2025:** FIN-FSA also published detailed white-paper guidance for ARTs, including approval expectations, mandatory disclosures, and the requirement to notify planned significant changes at least 30 working days before they take effect.
- **2025-2026:** Finland has moved from its earlier VCP registration regime into a full MiCA supervisory model while retaining its reputation for conservative documentation and control expectations.

## Impact on RFP/Procurement
Finnish buyers are likely to prefer platforms that support formal approvals, white-paper workflows, auditability, and conservative governance. A vendor that cannot explain how it supports MiCA authorization, passporting, and documentation in practice will look weak in a Finnish procurement.
