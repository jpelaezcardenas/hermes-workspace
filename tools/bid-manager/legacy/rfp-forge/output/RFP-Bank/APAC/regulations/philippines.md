# Philippines

Last updated: March 2026

**Country:** Philippines
**Regulatory Authority:** Bangko Sentral ng Pilipinas (BSP) and Securities and Exchange Commission Philippines (SEC)

## Key Regulations

### BSP Virtual Asset Service Provider (VASP) framework — Circulars 944 and 1108
- **Date enacted / effective:** Core guidance from 2021 onward; indefinite licensing moratorium extended September 1, 2025
- **Summary:** The BSP regulates VASPs involved in exchange and transfer activity under Circular No. 944 (virtual currency exchange guidelines) and Circular No. 1108 (VASP guidelines). The framework emphasizes risk management, consumer protection, AML/CFT, and technology controls. As of May 2025, only 13 entities held VASP licenses, three of which were inactive. In August 2025 (Memorandum No. M-2025-031), the BSP extended its moratorium on issuing new VASP licenses indefinitely, citing heightened risks related to consumer protection and cybercrime. Only BSP-supervised financial institutions with "stable" ratings under the BSP's Supervisory Assessment Framework may continue to apply for VASP authority.
- **Scope:** Exchange, transfer, custody-adjacent payments activity, AML/CFT; no new entrant licenses under moratorium.

### SEC Philippines Crypto Asset Service Provider (CASP) Framework — Memorandum Circulars No. 4 and 5
- **Date enacted / effective:** Finalized May 30, 2025; effective July 5, 2025
- **Summary:** The SEC introduced a Crypto Asset Service Provider (CASP) framework distinct from the BSP's VASP framework, targeting crypto service providers in the capital markets context. The rules aim to curb scams and protect investors. Entities must meet SEC registration and compliance standards. The BSP has clarified that regulated entities should only transact with entities holding both BSP VASP authorization and SEC CASP registration where both are applicable.
- **Scope:** Capital markets-related crypto service providers, investor protection, scam prevention.

### SEC guidance on digital asset offerings and securities law
- **Date enacted / effective:** Ongoing
- **Summary:** The SEC continues to apply Philippine securities law to token offerings and investment-contract style products. Enterprise tokenization projects must analyze whether a token sits within the securities perimeter.
- **Scope:** Token offerings, investment contracts, security tokens.

### AML/CFT framework — AMLA and proposed Senate Bill 1557
- **Date enacted / effective:** Ongoing; Senate Bill 1557 filed November 26, 2025
- **Summary:** The Philippines imposes strong AML/CFT requirements on VASPs including customer due diligence, suspicious activity reporting, and sanctions controls. The Philippines was removed from the FATF grey list in February 2025. However, FATF's June 2025 targeted update rated the Philippines as still "Partially Compliant" under Recommendation 15 (VASPs), citing ongoing enforcement and supervision gaps, particularly around unhosted wallets and offshore exchanges. Proposed Senate Bill 1557 seeks to explicitly designate VASPs as covered persons under AMLA primary law, expand AMLC powers, and add cybercrime and other offenses as predicates.
- **Scope:** AML/KYC, reporting, monitoring, sanctions, FATF compliance improvement.

## Licensing Requirements
Covered VASP activity generally requires BSP licensing/registration, but the indefinite moratorium (effective September 1, 2025) means no new crypto exchanges or trading platforms can secure BSP licenses until the ban is lifted. The BSP will periodically review the moratorium based on industry developments. Securities-style products may require SEC CASP registration and possible Monetary Board coordination. Unregistered, unauthorized, or unlicensed VASPs — whether operating locally or offshore — are strictly prohibited from conducting business. NTC (National Telecommunications Commission) ordered internet service providers to block over 50 unregistered online trading platforms in late 2025.

## Taxonomy
The Philippines uses a functional split between virtual assets regulated for payments/exchange (BSP scope) and digital tokens qualifying as securities or investment contracts (SEC scope). VASP and CASP frameworks now run in parallel, each with distinct registration requirements.

## Key Compliance Requirements
AML/KYC, FATF Travel Rule compliance, consumer disclosures, transaction monitoring, operational resilience, cybersecurity, BSP/SEC dual-registration where applicable, five-year data retention, and local reporting are key. BSP's February 2026 memorandum clarified that regulated entities must obtain proof of BSP registration, SEC registration, and Anti-Money Laundering Council (AMLC) standing before entering into any business relationship with a VASP.

## Recent Developments

**February 2025:** Philippines removed from the FATF grey list — a significant milestone. BSP reaffirmed commitment to strengthening AML/CFT oversight of VASPs.

**May 30, 2025:** SEC finalized the Crypto Asset Service Provider (CASP) framework (Memorandum Circulars No. 4 and 5, effective July 5, 2025), creating a parallel crypto services registration track under the SEC.

**August 20, 2025:** BSP extended its VASP licensing moratorium indefinitely (Memorandum M-2025-031), citing continued consumer protection and cybercrime concerns. 13 entities licensed; three inactive.

**September-October 2025:** FATF June 2025 update confirmed Philippines remains "Partially Compliant" on virtual asset standards. BSP pledged to enhance monitoring, surveillance, and enforcement capabilities.

**November 26, 2025:** Senate Bill 1557 filed, seeking to entrench VASPs as covered persons in AMLA primary law and expand AMLC enforcement powers.

**February 8, 2026:** BSP issued memorandum directing all regulated entities to conduct enhanced due diligence before dealing with any VASP, and prohibiting transactions with unregistered, unauthorized, or unlicensed VASPs.

## Impact on RFP/Procurement
Philippine procurement requires platforms that can handle the dual BSP/SEC compliance environment. The indefinite licensing moratorium means the addressable market is limited to existing licensed VASPs and eligible BSP-supervised institutions — greenfield entrants are effectively blocked. Procurement teams should verify existing licensing status and Travel Rule capabilities, and expect increasing pressure toward FATF-compliant controls as the Philippines works to move from "Partially Compliant" to "Largely Compliant."
