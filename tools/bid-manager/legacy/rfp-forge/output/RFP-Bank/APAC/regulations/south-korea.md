# South Korea

Last updated: March 2026

**Country:** South Korea
**Regulatory Authority:** Financial Services Commission (FSC) and Financial Intelligence Unit (FIU)

## Key Regulations

### Act on Reporting and Use of Certain Financial Transaction Information
- **Date enacted / effective:** Applied to VASPs from 2021 onward
- **Summary:** South Korea requires VASPs to register and comply with stringent AML/CFT obligations, including real-name account arrangements and information-security standards. This has created one of the most demanding exchange operating environments in Asia.
- **Scope:** VASP registration, AML/CFT, banking interface, information security.

### Virtual Asset User Protection Act
- **Date enacted / effective:** Effective 19 July 2024
- **Summary:** The User Protection Act introduced explicit conduct, custody, insurance/reserve, and unfair-trading rules for virtual-asset businesses. It is a major step from AML-only oversight toward full market-conduct regulation.
- **Scope:** Custody, unfair trading, user asset protection, operational controls.

### Security Token Offering reforms
- **Date enacted / effective:** Developed through 2023-2025
- **Summary:** South Korea has separately advanced a framework for security tokens under capital-markets law. This reinforces the split between general virtual assets and tokenized securities.
- **Scope:** Security tokens, capital-markets activity, tokenized investment products.

## Licensing Requirements
VASP registration with FIU is essential, usually alongside stringent bank-account and information-security prerequisites. Security-token activity may require capital-markets licences or participation through regulated institutions.

## Taxonomy
South Korea distinguishes virtual assets from security tokens, with the latter governed separately under capital-markets principles. The split is operationally important for platform design and permissions.

## Key Compliance Requirements
AML/KYC, real-name account support, wallet segregation, reserve/insurance protections, unfair-trading surveillance, cybersecurity, and incident response are core. Korean buyers also expect strong local reporting and support for token admission governance.

## Recent Developments
2024-2025 was dominated by implementation of the User Protection Act and further work on the second phase of broader market-structure regulation. South Korea remains a tightly supervised but important market.

## Impact on RFP/Procurement
Procurement in Korea is control-intensive. Vendors must support strong exchange/custody controls, surveillance, and bank-integration workflows; consumer-crypto UX alone will not get through enterprise or regulator review.
