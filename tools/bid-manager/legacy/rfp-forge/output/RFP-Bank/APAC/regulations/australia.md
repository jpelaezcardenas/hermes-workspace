# Australia

Last updated: March 2026

**Country:** Australia
**Regulatory Authority:** ASIC, AUSTRAC, Treasury, Reserve Bank of Australia for some policy matters

## Key Regulations

### AUSTRAC digital currency exchange registration
- **Date enacted / effective:** Since 2018 under AML/CTF Act
- **Summary:** Australia requires digital currency exchanges to register with AUSTRAC and comply with AML/CTF obligations. This provides the core baseline for many crypto businesses even though it is not a full conduct/prudential licensing regime.
- **Scope:** AML/KYC, registration, monitoring, reporting for exchange services.

### Corporations Act / ASIC perimeter guidance
- **Date enacted / effective:** Ongoing
- **Summary:** ASIC applies existing financial-services law where crypto products are financial products, managed investment schemes, derivatives, or non-cash payment facilities. This means token classification and product design remain central compliance questions.
- **Scope:** Financial products, custody, derivatives, investment schemes, tokenized products.

### Treasury digital asset platform reform proposals
- **Date enacted / effective:** Consultations through 2023-2025
- **Summary:** Australia has been designing a broader digital-asset platform framework aimed at closing gaps left by the AML-only registration model. The direction is toward more explicit licensing without abandoning existing corporations law.
- **Scope:** Future exchange/custody licensing, market conduct, consumer protection.

## Licensing Requirements
Today firms often need AUSTRAC registration and may also require an Australian financial services licence depending on the product. A broader digital-asset platform licence may emerge, so procurement should anticipate future uplift in conduct requirements.

## Taxonomy
Australia distinguishes financial products from non-financial crypto assets, with separate AML treatment for digital currency exchanges. A token can therefore be outside securities law for one use case and inside it for another.

## Key Compliance Requirements
AML/KYC, transaction monitoring, customer disclosures, custody controls, cybersecurity, reporting, and careful product classification are essential. Institutional buyers also care about outsourcing governance and incident response.

## Recent Developments
2024-2025 has been defined by Treasury consultation and increasing focus on exchange failures, custody, and consumer protection. The regime is still in transition, but the direction is toward more explicit licensing.

## Impact on RFP/Procurement
Australian procurement favors vendors that can operate under today's mixed framework while scaling into a future full licensing model. Flexibility around financial-product versus non-financial-product flows is a major differentiator.
