# Bermuda

Last updated: March 2026

**Country:** Bermuda
**Regulatory Authority:** Bermuda Monetary Authority (BMA)

## Key Regulations

### Digital Asset Business Act (DABA) 2018
- **Date enacted / effective:** Enacted 2018; updated continuously
- **Summary:** Bermuda created one of the earliest and most comprehensive digital-asset business licensing regimes globally. DABA covers issuance, sale, redemption, custody, payment services, exchange operations, and other digital-asset business models under a single supervisory architecture. The BMA has since layered detailed codes of practice, statements of principles, and guidance onto DABA, making it function similarly to any other regulated financial services regime under the BMA.
- **Scope:** Digital asset business activities including custody, exchange, issuance, and payments.

### Digital Asset Issuance Act (DAIA) 2020
- **Date enacted / effective:** 2020; updated periodically
- **Summary:** Bermuda supplemented DABA with dedicated issuance oversight and disclosure rules for token offerings. DAIA provides a clearer route for compliant issuance than most larger markets and gives regulatory and legal certainty for ICO/TGE structures under Bermuda law.
- **Scope:** Token issuance, disclosures, approvals, issuer conduct.

### Digital Asset Business (Custody of Client Assets) Rules 2025
- **Date enacted / effective:** Published 2025
- **Summary:** These rules introduce specific obligations for DABA-licensed businesses providing custodial wallet services. They enhance and clarify existing custody requirements, including recordkeeping, reconciliations, segregation of client assets, and incident response. This is the most significant product-level update to the DABA framework in 2025.
- **Scope:** Client asset custody, segregation, recordkeeping, operational controls.

### Operational Resilience and Outsourcing Code (Consultation, 2025)
- **Date enacted / effective:** Consultation paper released 2025; final code expected late 2025/early 2026; compliance deadline 31 March 2028
- **Summary:** The BMA issued a Consultation Paper on a proposed Operational Resilience and Outsourcing Code, supported by Guidance Notes. Digital asset businesses will be expected to comply by 31 March 2028. In the interim, firms must consider and implement the BMA's operational resilience expectations on a risk-proportionate basis.
- **Scope:** Operational resilience, outsourcing management, business continuity.

### AML/ATF Framework
- **Date enacted / effective:** Ongoing; regularly updated
- **Summary:** Bermuda couples its crypto regime with robust AML/ATF, cybersecurity, and operational risk expectations. The BMA applies enhanced customer due diligence requirements across the regulated sector. Institutional participants must demonstrate mature control frameworks to obtain and retain a BMA licence.
- **Scope:** AML/KYC, cybersecurity, sanctions, operational risk.

## Licensing Requirements
A BMA digital asset business licence is required for covered activities, with classes ranging from testing (sandbox) to full operation. The BMA published the **Digital Asset Business Guidance for Prospective Applicants for Licensing (September 2025)**, which formally sets out clear expectations for applicants — including governance, three-lines-of-defence structures, AML/ATF controls, custody arrangements, and staffing. Depending on the structure, separate insurance, trust, or investment-business permissions may also apply.

## Taxonomy
Bermuda uses a statutory "digital asset" concept tied to DABA and DAIA. The focus is activity-based licensing rather than a retail taxonomy of utility/payment/security tokens. All cryptocurrencies, digital coins, and tokens are generally treated as "digital assets" for regulatory purposes unless specific statutory exceptions apply.

## Key Compliance Requirements
AML/KYC, sanctions, cybersecurity (Operational Cyber Risk Management Code, January 2024), custody safeguards (Custody of Client Assets Rules 2025), outsourcing management, business continuity, prudential governance, and regulatory reporting are core. Bermuda buyers also care strongly about incident response and insurance-adjacent risk controls.

## Recent Developments

**2025 was a significant year for DABA framework expansion:**

- **Digital Asset Business Thematic Review (1 October 2025):** The BMA completed a sector-wide thematic review focusing on vertical integration, governance, outsourcing, and head office requirements. The review identified sector patterns, trends, and emerging risks.
- **Discussion Paper on Asset Tokenisation (5 November 2025):** The BMA published a dedicated discussion paper on asset tokenisation, signalling supervisory interest in institutional tokenisation structures under the DABA/DAIA regime.
- **Embedded Supervision Initiative for DeFi:** The BMA issued a Call for Proposals for embedded supervision of decentralised finance, positioning Bermuda as one of the first jurisdictions to formally explore real-time DeFi oversight.
- **Payments regulatory reform:** The BMA is consulting on a new payments regulatory framework for Payment Service Providers, including replacing the Bermuda Money Service Business regime.
- **BMA Amendment Act 2025:** Became operative 1 January 2026, amending the BMA Act 1969 on information-sharing and fee schedules.

As of early 2026, Bermuda continues to reinforce its position as an institutional digital-asset domicile with one of the world's most mature licensing and supervisory architectures.

## Impact on RFP/Procurement
Bermuda procurement emphasises institutional robustness and regulator-facing documentation. Vendors must support controlled launch phases, formal reporting, strong custody and risk controls aligned with the 2025 Custody Rules, and operational resilience frameworks consistent with the forthcoming BMA Outsourcing Code. The BMA's active thematic review posture means vendors should expect ongoing supervisory scrutiny of governance and outsourcing arrangements.
