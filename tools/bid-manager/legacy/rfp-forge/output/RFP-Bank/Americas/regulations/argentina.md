# Argentina

Last updated: March 2026

**Country:** Argentina
**Regulatory Authority:** CNV (Comisión Nacional de Valores), UIF, BCRA, AFIP/ARCA

## Key Regulations

### Law 27,739 — VASP and Digital Asset Framework
- **Date enacted / effective:** Enacted March 15, 2024
- **Summary:** Argentina's foundational law for virtual asset regulation. Law 27,739 designated the CNV as the primary regulatory authority for the crypto industry, created a formal Virtual Asset Service Provider (VASP) definition covering exchanges, custodians, transfer platforms, and related services, and established the legal basis for mandatory VASP registration and AML/CFT obligations. The law also provided legal validity to crypto contracts and created a phased compliance framework.
- **Scope:** VASP definition, CNV authority, AML/CFT framework, legal validity of digital asset contracts.

### CNV Resolution 994/2024 — VASP Registration Framework
- **Date enacted / effective:** March 25, 2024
- **Summary:** Following Law 27,739, the CNV issued Resolution 994/2024 to establish the Registry of Virtual Asset Service Providers (PSAV). All businesses conducting crypto services in Argentina must register with this registry. Concurrent with this, Argentina's Financial Intelligence Unit (UIF) issued Resolution 49/2024 requiring registered VASPs to also register with the UIF as AML-regulated entities, creating a dual-registration requirement.
- **Scope:** Mandatory VASP registration, registry creation, AML reporting obligations.

### CNV Resolution 1058/2025 — Detailed VASP Operational Regulations
- **Date enacted / effective:** March 14, 2025; compliance deadlines phased by entity type
- **Summary:** The most detailed regulatory framework to date for Argentine VASPs. Resolution 1058/2025 establishes: mandatory segregation of company and client funds; annual external audits and monthly financial reporting to the CNV; appointment of a Compliance Officer and public relations manager; information security policies and procedure manuals; enhanced risk disclosure requirements; and minimum net worth requirements (ranging from approximately USD 85,000 to USD 170,000 depending on the activity category). **Compliance deadlines:** individuals registered as VASPs — July 1, 2025; Argentine-incorporated companies — August 1, 2025; foreign-incorporated companies — September 1, 2025.
- **Scope:** Operational conduct, custody, capital, governance, disclosure, and reporting for VASPs.

### Decree 640/2024 — Tokenisation Legal Framework
- **Date enacted / effective:** July 18, 2024
- **Summary:** Argentina issued a legal framework for the tokenisation of assets (goods and financial instruments), providing a basis for structured token issuance beyond pure spot-crypto use cases. This is directly relevant for enterprise tokenisation and digital asset platform deployments.
- **Scope:** Asset tokenisation, legal framework for tokenised representations of goods.

### UIF Resolution 49/2024 — AML Obligations for VASPs
- **Date enacted / effective:** March 2024
- **Summary:** All registered VASPs must register with the UIF as AML-regulated entities and comply with customer due diligence, transaction monitoring, and suspicious activity reporting obligations aligned with FATF standards. Monthly volumes exceeding 35,000 UVA (approximately USD 29,246) trigger full AML monitoring obligations.
- **Scope:** AML/KYC, transaction monitoring, STR reporting.

### CNV Enforcement — ARGt Cease-and-Desist (March 2026)
- **Date enacted / effective:** March 11, 2026
- **Summary:** The CNV issued a cease-and-desist order against the issuers of ARGt, a virtual coin presented as a stablecoin with guaranteed returns. The CNV ruled that ARGt constitutes a security under the Argentine Securities Act, and that its public offering was irregular without prior CNV authorisation. Critically, the CNV clarified that **stablecoins per se are not securities**, but that the economic reality and offering method of ARGt triggered the Howey-equivalent investment contract test. The CNV also reaffirmed that VASPs may facilitate crypto exchange but may not conduct public offerings of assets qualifying as securities without CNV approval.
- **Scope:** Securities classification of tokens, public offering rules, VASP activity scope.

## Licensing Requirements
Argentina requires VASP registration (not a traditional licence) with the CNV under Law 27,739 and Resolution 994/2024. Full operational compliance under Resolution 1058/2025 has been mandatory since September 2025 for foreign entities. There are five VASP categories (exchange, transfer, custody, issuance, and related services), each with distinct minimum net worth requirements. Non-compliant platforms risk court-ordered blocking of operations.

## Taxonomy
Virtual assets are digital representations of value for AML and supervisory purposes under Law 27,739. Stablecoins per se are not securities, but tokens that satisfy the investment contract test (per the March 2026 CNV enforcement action) will require prior CNV authorisation for public offering. Tokenisation of goods is explicitly supported by Decree 640/2024.

## Key Compliance Requirements
VASP registration (CNV + UIF), AML/KYC (FATF-aligned), monthly CNV financial reporting, annual external audit, client fund segregation, Compliance Officer appointment, information security policies, capital adequacy by category, tax and foreign-exchange reporting. As of early 2026, the VASP registry has moved into **active enforcement phase** — non-registration carries operational risk.

## Recent Developments

**Argentina's regulatory landscape advanced rapidly in 2024–2026:**

- **Law 27,739 and VASP registry (2024):** Argentina built its full regulatory framework in under 12 months — from statutory authority to operational registry to detailed rules.
- **Resolution 1058/2025 (March 2025):** Introduced the operational layer of VASP regulation — fund segregation, audits, governance, and net worth requirements. Registration deadlines passed through Q3 2025.
- **VASP registry active enforcement phase (early 2026):** The CNV shifted to active enforcement. Non-compliant platforms face suspension, fines, or operating bans. This signals the end of the informal crypto era in Argentina.
- **ARGt cease-and-desist (March 11, 2026):** First major CNV enforcement action against a virtual coin offering. Signals that securities law applies to tokens structured as investment contracts, regardless of their "stablecoin" label.
- **Argentina receives USD 91 billion in crypto transactions (July 2023–June 2024):** The highest in Latin America per Chainalysis — driven largely by inflation hedging via USDT/USDC stablecoins.
- **Tokenisation legal framework (Decree 640/2024):** Provides enterprise-grade tokenisation use cases with legal backing.

## Impact on RFP/Procurement
Argentine enterprises need platforms that are fully CNV/UIF compliant — segregated custody, audit trails, monthly reporting, and robust AML. The active enforcement posture means compliance gaps carry direct operational risk. The 2026 enforcement environment, combined with the ARGt precedent, makes legal classification analysis and securities-law support a platform differentiator. Vendors must also support high policy volatility, FX controls, and inflation-driven stablecoin demand.
