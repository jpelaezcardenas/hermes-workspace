# Middle-East-Africa Digital Asset Regulations

Last updated: March 2026

## Regional Overview

The Middle East has become one of the most structured growth zones for digital assets, led by the UAE's federal, ADGM, and Dubai VARA frameworks, along with Bahrain's long-standing crypto-asset module. Saudi Arabia and Qatar remain more cautious, while Israel uses a mix of securities and financial-services oversight. In Africa, South Africa has moved furthest with formal crypto-asset FSP licensing, while Nigeria, Kenya, and others are still refining dedicated licensing and market-conduct rules.

Procurement in the region typically turns on licensing perimeter management, local hosting/governance expectations, and AML controls. Buyers often need a platform that can support multiple supervisory models simultaneously: exchange/custody licensing in GCC markets, securities-style treatment for tokenized instruments, and bank-grade transaction monitoring where regulators remain cautious. Cross-border rollouts should assume entity-by-entity licensing rather than a passporting model.

## Country Index

| Country | Primary Regulator | Key Framework | Classification Approach | Status |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| United Arab Emirates | VARA, SCA, CBUAE, FSRA ADGM, DFSA DIFC | VARA Rulebooks; SCA virtual asset framework; ADGM FSMR | Virtual assets with activity-based licensing; securities handled separately | Highly developed multi-zone regime |
| Saudi Arabia | SAMA; CMA | Restricted market; fintech sandbox and securities rules | No general crypto licensing framework yet | Cautious, pilot-oriented |
| Bahrain | Central Bank of Bahrain | CBB Rulebook Volume 6 - Crypto-Asset Module | Crypto-assets and licensed crypto-asset services | Operational licensing regime |
| Qatar | QCB; QFCRA/QFCA | QFC Digital Assets Framework 2024 | Tokenized assets and digital asset services within QFC perimeter | Onshore cautious, QFC opened |
| South Africa | FSCA; SARB; FIC | FAIS declaration + AML amendments | Crypto assets as digital representations of value | Licensed CASP/FSP model live |
| Nigeria | SEC Nigeria; CBN; NFIU/SCUML | SEC digital asset rules and VASP amendments | Digital assets, VASPs, and investment-style tokens | Framework liberalizing after banking restrictions eased |
| Kenya | Capital Markets Authority; CBK; Parliament | Proposed VASP Bill / tax rules | No final dedicated taxonomy yet | Draft legislation stage |
| Israel | ISA; Capital Markets Authority; BoI | Financial services licensing + securities/tax treatment | Virtual assets generally treated as financial business/templates/assets/property; securities analysis for tokens | Maturing but not fully unified |
