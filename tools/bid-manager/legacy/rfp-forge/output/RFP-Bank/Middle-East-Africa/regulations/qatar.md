# Qatar

Last updated: March 2026

**Country:** Qatar
**Regulatory Authority:** Qatar Central Bank (QCB) and Qatar Financial Centre Regulatory Authority (QFCRA)

## Key Regulations

### QFC Digital Assets Framework
- **Date enacted / effective:** Launched 2024
- **Summary:** Qatar opened a more explicit route for digital-asset activity through the Qatar Financial Centre, providing a controlled environment for tokenization and digital-asset services. This is a significant shift from the historically restrictive onshore stance.
- **Scope:** Digital asset services and tokenization within the QFC perimeter.

### Onshore restrictions from QCB/QFCRA legacy position
- **Date enacted / effective:** Earlier restrictions remain relevant outside the new framework
- **Summary:** Qatar had long maintained a restrictive posture toward crypto-asset services outside controlled channels. Enterprises therefore need to distinguish carefully between onshore Qatar and activity housed within the QFC framework.
- **Scope:** Restrictions on unauthorized crypto activity; jurisdictional perimeter management.

### AML/CFT and financial-services controls
- **Date enacted / effective:** Ongoing
- **Summary:** Digital-asset projects in Qatar remain subject to rigorous AML/CFT and governance expectations, especially where international counterparties or tokenized financial assets are involved.
- **Scope:** AML/KYC, governance, reporting, institutional tokenization controls.

## Licensing Requirements
Licensing is currently most viable through the QFC digital-asset framework for qualifying activities. Onshore activity outside that perimeter remains much more constrained and requires careful legal analysis.

## Taxonomy
Qatar is moving toward a functional digital-asset and tokenization taxonomy within QFC, while preserving a cautious stance outside that regime. Jurisdictional location is therefore part of the classification analysis.

## Key Compliance Requirements
AML/KYC, governance, custody controls, incident reporting, outsourcing management, and local perimeter discipline are key. Enterprises should assume close scrutiny of cross-border flows and entity setup.

## Recent Developments
The 2024 launch of the QFC framework is the major recent development. Through 2025, the key question has been how quickly the controlled framework develops into a practical market for institutional deployments.

## Impact on RFP/Procurement
Qatar procurement turns on legal-entity design and regulatory-perimeter clarity. Vendors that can operate inside a permissioned institutional framework and support strong governance will be much more viable than general-market crypto platforms.
