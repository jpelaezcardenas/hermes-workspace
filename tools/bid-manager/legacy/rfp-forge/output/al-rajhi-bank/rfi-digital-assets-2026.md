# 1. Introduction and Purpose

Al Rajhi Bank hereby invites qualified market participants to submit information regarding their capabilities in relation to digital asset infrastructure, tokenization platforms, and associated operating, compliance, and governance frameworks.

This Request for Information (“RFI”) has been prepared for exploratory and market-assessment purposes. The Bank is in an evaluation phase and is seeking to better understand the maturity of available platforms, the practical readiness of vendors, and the extent to which contemporary digital asset solutions may support Shariah-compliant capital markets and treasury-related use cases within the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia and, where relevant, across broader Gulf Cooperation Council (“GCC”) markets.

The Bank emphasizes at the outset that this exercise is not a procurement process and should not be interpreted as a commitment to pursue implementation, commercial negotiations, pilot deployment, or contract award. Rather, the present exercise is intended to support structured learning, internal assessment, and the consideration of potential future options.

Given Al Rajhi Bank’s institutional profile, Islamic banking model, and regulatory environment, respondents are expected to provide measured, evidence-based, and implementation-oriented responses. Marketing language without substantiation will not be considered sufficient.

The Bank is particularly interested in understanding how market solutions may support the tokenization and lifecycle management of Shariah-compliant financial instruments and real-world assets, including but not limited to real estate-linked structures, sukuk-related use cases, and investment fund structures, subject at all times to applicable legal, regulatory, operational, and Shariah governance requirements.

---

# 2. Institutional Background and Strategic Context

Al Rajhi Bank is one of the leading financial institutions in the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia and among the largest Islamic banks globally. The Bank maintains a significant position across retail banking, corporate banking, treasury, and investment-related activities, and operates within a framework that places strong emphasis on prudence, trust, operational resilience, and Shariah compliance.

The Bank recognizes the accelerating global and regional interest in digital infrastructure for capital markets, asset servicing, post-trade modernization, and selected forms of real-world asset digitization. Within the Kingdom, this broader context is influenced by Saudi Vision 2030 and related financial sector development priorities, including capital market modernization, innovation, private-sector development, and the deployment of secure and scalable digital infrastructure.

Within this broader strategic context, entities in the Al Rajhi ecosystem have commenced selective exploratory activity relevant to tokenization and digital market infrastructure. Such activity includes, for example, recent exploration at subsidiary level in relation to real-world asset tokenization and capital markets modernization. At the same time, the Bank notes that exploratory activity at subsidiary or ecosystem level should not be interpreted as confirmation of parent-bank production deployment or strategic commitment at enterprise scale.

The purpose of this RFI is therefore to support disciplined market understanding. The Bank wishes to examine the available vendor landscape, deployment models, governance controls, and implementation considerations associated with digital asset platforms before determining whether any future structured initiative should be considered.

This RFI should be read in light of the following institutional principles:

- Any potential future use case must align with Al Rajhi Bank’s Islamic banking identity and governance standards.
- Any platform under consideration must be capable of supporting Shariah-compliant instruments and workflows.
- Any future deployment in the Kingdom must be compatible with applicable Saudi legal and regulatory expectations, including those of the Capital Market Authority (“CMA”) and the Saudi Central Bank (“SAMA”), as relevant.
- Data sovereignty, cyber resilience, operational control, and auditability are foundational requirements rather than optional features.
- The Bank is seeking clarity, not promotion. Respondents should distinguish clearly between live capability, configurable capability, roadmap capability, and conceptual capability.

---

# 3. Objectives of this RFI

The principal objectives of this RFI are as follows:

1. **To understand the market landscape** for digital asset infrastructure and tokenization platforms relevant to banking, treasury, and capital markets institutions.
2. **To assess platform maturity** in supporting institutional-grade issuance, servicing, governance, reporting, and redemption processes.
3. **To evaluate Shariah-compliant applicability** of available solutions across relevant Islamic finance use cases.
4. **To identify deployment models** compatible with Saudi data residency, security, and operational resilience expectations, with on-premises deployment regarded as mandatory for any serious future consideration.
5. **To assess vendor readiness** to support a large, conservative, highly regulated Islamic financial institution.
6. **To understand integration complexity** with banking, treasury, custody, compliance, and capital markets environments.
7. **To obtain high-level commercial insight** into typical pricing and operating models without requesting binding price quotations.
8. **To inform any subsequent internal decision** as to whether a more formal Request for Proposal (“RFP”), proof-of-concept exercise, or other next-step process should be initiated.

The Bank is not, through this document, seeking immediate detailed implementation plans, final legal terms, or committed commercial proposals. However, respondents should provide sufficient depth to allow a serious assessment of institutional fit, governance readiness, and practical viability.
