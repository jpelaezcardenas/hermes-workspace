## 1. Purpose of Inquiry

Tamara is gathering market information for Sharia-compliant tokenized lending. This RFI is exploratory and intended to test vendor maturity, implementation realism, and control-model fit.
## 2. Information Objectives

The buyer wants evidence on workflow maturity, architecture, integrations, security, resilience, regulatory fit, and commercial/delivery models. Relevant regulatory context includes SAMA, CMA, AAOIFI; market initiatives include Saudi consumer finance innovation. The response must explicitly address Sharia governance, AAOIFI-aligned review, product permissibility controls, and evidence suitable for Sharia-board review.
## 3. Requested Vendor Input

Respondents should describe support for:
- Islamic lending product templates
- investor/funder participation and servicing workflows
- Sharia review and reporting outputs

Responses must clearly mark current, configurable, partner-enabled, roadmap, and unsupported capabilities.
## 4. Follow-up Path

The buyer may use responses to shape a later RFP, pilot, market sounding, or internal strategy paper. Vague product marketing will be scored down.
