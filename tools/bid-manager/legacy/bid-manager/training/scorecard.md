## Calibration Notes
- If average > 42/50 for 5 consecutive reviews: tighten scoring standards
- If average < 30/50 for 5 consecutive reviews: check if expectations are realistic vs content quality
- Target steady-state: 38-44/50 range for well-written proposals

### Writing quality micro-check: section openers and closes
Use this check whenever Writing Quality is 3/5 or lower but the content is technically correct. First sentence must answer or stake a claim, not restate the question or give generic context. Final sentence must land a platform-specific advantage, boundary, or decision consequence, not drift into filler or reassurance. If either end of the section could be copied into a competitor response without change, score Writing down by one point. If both opener and closer are specific, direct, and evaluatively useful, that is usually the difference between a competent section and a persuasive one.

### Writing quality micro-check: proof-before-detail and boundary adjacency
Use this when a section sounds informed but still feels slippery under review. The first proof element, named workflow, regulatory template, deployment fact, or verified architecture point, should appear before the paragraph drifts into secondary explanation. If a section makes a capability claim that depends on configuration or integration, the boundary statement must sit next to that claim, not three sentences later. Score Writing down by one point when proof arrives too late, or when the capability boundary forces the evaluator to infer what is native versus integration-dependent. Reward sections that establish the claim, anchor it quickly with evidence, and state the operating boundary in the same paragraph.
