# Request for Information

# Digital Sukuk Issuance and Lifecycle Management Platform

**Issued by:** Gulf Development Bank (GDB)
**RFI Reference:** GDB-DIG-2026-017
**Date of Issue:** 15 March 2026
**Response Deadline:** 15 April 2026
**Classification:** Confidential

---

## Background

Gulf Development Bank (GDB) is a multilateral development institution headquartered in Manama, Bahrain, with a mandate to finance infrastructure, social development, and economic diversification projects across the Gulf Cooperation Council (GCC) member states. GDB has total assets exceeding USD 28 billion and maintains credit ratings of Aa2 (Moody's) and AA (Fitch).

GDB has historically funded its lending operations through conventional bond issuances. In alignment with its 2025-2030 Strategic Plan and the growing demand from Islamic institutional investors, GDB's Board of Directors has approved a programme to issue Sharia-compliant sukuk instruments on a digital platform. The programme envisions an initial issuance of USD 500 million in ijara sukuk, followed by subsequent issuances of murabaha and wakala structures totaling up to USD 2 billion over three years.

GDB's Sharia Supervisory Board, consisting of five scholars appointed by the Board of Directors, must approve all structural elements of any sukuk programme. The Board operates under AAOIFI Financial Accounting Standard No. 17 (Investments) and Sharia Standard No. 17 (Investment Sukuk) and requires that the digital platform preserves the economic substance of Sharia-compliant structures rather than merely replicating conventional bond mechanics with different terminology.

## Objectives

GDB seeks information from qualified technology providers regarding platforms capable of supporting the full lifecycle of digital sukuk instruments, including structuring, issuance, profit distribution, secondary trading, and maturity or dissolution. The platform must accommodate multiple sukuk structures and ensure compliance with AAOIFI standards, the Central Bank of Bahrain (CBB) regulations, and the broader GCC regulatory landscape.

## Scope of Information Requested

Respondents are asked to address the following areas in detail.

### Section 1: Sukuk Structure Support

1.1 Describe how your platform supports the issuance and lifecycle management of ijara (lease-based) sukuk, including the representation of the underlying lease asset, periodic rental payment calculations, and the distinction between fixed-rate and floating-rate rental structures.

1.2 Describe how your platform supports murabaha (cost-plus financing) sukuk, including the representation of the underlying commodity or asset, the cost-plus markup structure, and deferred payment schedules.

1.3 Describe how your platform supports wakala (agency-based) sukuk, including the representation of the wakala agreement, the agent's (wakeel's) authority boundaries, expected profit rate versus actual return mechanics, and incentive fee structures.

1.4 Explain how your platform ensures that the digital representation of sukuk maintains the economic substance required by Sharia principles, specifically: (a) asset-backing verification, ensuring that every sukuk token is linked to an identifiable underlying real asset or economic activity; (b) risk-sharing between issuer and sukuk holders; and (c) prohibition of guaranteed returns (the distinction between expected profit rates and guaranteed interest payments).

1.5 Describe how your platform handles the identification and tracking of underlying assets for asset-backed sukuk, and how it ensures that the total sukuk issuance does not exceed the value of the underlying asset pool.

### Section 2: Sharia Governance and Compliance

2.1 Describe how your platform integrates Sharia board approval workflows into the sukuk issuance and modification lifecycle. Specifically, how are Sharia board resolutions recorded, and how does the platform prevent issuance or modification of sukuk structures that have not received Sharia board approval?

2.2 Explain how your platform accommodates AAOIFI standards, specifically Financial Accounting Standards No. 17 (Investments) and No. 33 (Investment in Sukuk, Shares, and Similar Instruments), and Sharia Standards No. 17 (Investment Sukuk) and No. 59 (Sale of Debt).

2.3 Describe how the platform ensures compliance with Central Bank of Bahrain (CBB) Rule Book Volume 6 (Capital Markets) requirements for digital asset issuance, including investor categorization, disclosure requirements, and reporting obligations.

2.4 How does your platform handle the prohibition of trading debt at a discount (bay' al-dayn), specifically in the context of secondary market transfers of murabaha sukuk?

### Section 3: Profit Distribution and Financial Mechanics

3.1 Describe how your platform calculates and distributes periodic profit payments to sukuk holders. Address specifically: (a) the distinction between profit-sharing distributions and interest payments in the platform's data model and terminology; (b) pro-rata distribution calculations based on holding period and denomination; (c) support for both fixed expected profit rates and variable returns linked to underlying asset performance.

3.2 Explain how your platform handles the distribution waterfall for wakala sukuk, including: (a) actual portfolio return calculation; (b) expected profit rate payment to sukuk holders; (c) incentive fee calculation for the wakeel when actual returns exceed expected rates; and (d) loss allocation when actual returns fall below expected rates.

3.3 Describe how the platform manages dissolution (maturity) and early dissolution events, including: (a) scheduled maturity with return of capital; (b) early dissolution triggers (e.g., total loss of underlying asset, regulatory event); (c) exercise price calculation for ijara sukuk (purchase undertaking); and (d) pro-rata distribution of residual value.

### Section 4: Regulatory Compliance and Investor Management

4.1 Describe your platform's investor onboarding and identity verification capabilities, with specific reference to GCC regulatory requirements including CBB regulations, UAE Securities and Commodities Authority (SCA) requirements, and Saudi Capital Market Authority (CMA) rules.

4.2 Explain how the platform enforces investor eligibility rules across multiple GCC jurisdictions simultaneously, including qualified investor classification, minimum investment thresholds, and jurisdiction-specific restrictions.

4.3 Describe AML/CFT compliance capabilities aligned with FATF recommendations and GCC-specific requirements, including MENAFATF mutual evaluation criteria.

4.4 How does the platform generate regulatory reports compliant with CBB reporting requirements, including periodic disclosure, material event notification, and register maintenance?

### Section 5: Technology Architecture and Deployment

5.1 Describe the underlying blockchain or distributed ledger technology used by your platform, including consensus mechanism, transaction throughput, and finality guarantees.

5.2 Explain your platform's deployment model options, with specific reference to data residency requirements within GCC member states and the ability to operate nodes within Bahrain's jurisdiction.

5.3 Describe the platform's integration capabilities with existing financial infrastructure, including: (a) core banking systems; (b) SWIFT/ISO 20022 messaging; (c) existing CSD/registry systems; and (d) market data providers for rental/profit rate benchmarks.

5.4 Provide details on the platform's security architecture, including key management, access control, and audit trail capabilities.

5.5 Describe disaster recovery and business continuity provisions, including RPO and RTO targets.

### Section 6: Implementation and Support

6.1 Provide a high-level implementation timeline for the initial ijara sukuk issuance, including key milestones from contract signing to first issuance.

6.2 Describe your organization's experience with Islamic finance technology implementations, including any relevant case studies or references.

6.3 Describe your ongoing support model, including SLA terms, update and upgrade frequency, and dedicated account management.

## Response Format

Respondents should structure their response to address each section and sub-question in the order presented. Include diagrams where they clarify architecture or workflows. Responses should not exceed 30 pages excluding appendices.
