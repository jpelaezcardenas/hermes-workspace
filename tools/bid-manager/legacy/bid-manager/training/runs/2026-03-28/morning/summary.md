## Key Lesson Learned

**Structured products expose the importance of honest capability boundaries as a competitive weapon.** The biggest improvement between loops came from reframing "what DALP doesn't do" (waterfall calculation, collateral analytics, substitution eligibility) not as gaps to minimize, but as evidence of architectural discipline. Positioning the platform-vs-servicer boundary as the correct architectural decision, and explicitly stating why compiling deal logic into smart contracts is the wrong approach, turned an honesty obligation into a differentiation argument. This pattern is reusable: whenever a capability boundary exists, explain *why* it is the right boundary, not just that it exists.
