# Request for Information: Digital Platform for Sharia-Compliant Sukuk Issuance and Lifecycle Management

## Issuing Institution
Al Noor Development Bank is a multilateral development institution headquartered in the Gulf Cooperation Council region. The bank is evaluating digital infrastructure to support the issuance, administration, and supervision of Sharia-compliant sukuk instruments for sovereign, quasi-sovereign, and development-finance use cases.

## Background
The bank intends to modernize its issuance and post-issuance operating model for sukuk by introducing a digital asset platform that can support primary issuance, investor registry management, periodic profit distribution, transfer controls, and auditable governance workflows. The target operating model must preserve institutional controls, meet regional regulatory expectations, and support future expansion across multiple jurisdictions and sukuk structures.

The initial program scope covers ijara, murabaha, and wakala sukuk structures. The bank expects the selected platform to support a phased rollout beginning with a permissioned deployment for qualified participants, followed by broader market participation as regulatory approvals mature.

## Objectives
The selected platform should enable the bank to:

- issue sukuk instruments on a controlled digital infrastructure
- manage investor eligibility and transfer restrictions in line with Sharia and securities requirements
- automate periodic profit distributions and maturity events
- maintain auditable workflows for approvals, ownership changes, and supervisory review
- integrate with existing banking, treasury, and reporting environments

## Information Requested
Please describe your platform's capabilities, delivery boundaries, and implementation considerations in relation to the following requirements.

## Functional Requirements
### Sukuk Structure Support
Describe support for ijara, murabaha, and wakala sukuk structures. Clarify how the platform represents asset-backed or asset-based instruments, ownership interests, periodic profit entitlements, and maturity redemption events.

### Issuance and Distribution
Explain how the platform supports primary issuance, allocation to approved participants, denomination controls, subscription tracking, and issuance finalization. State whether book-building or auction-based allocation is native, configurable, or integration-dependent.

### Profit Distribution and Redemption
Explain how periodic profit distributions are scheduled, calculated, approved, and executed. Describe support for maturity redemption, partial redemption, and early dissolution scenarios.

### Investor Eligibility and Transfer Controls
Describe how the platform enforces investor classification, jurisdiction restrictions, concentration limits, and transfer approval rules. Explain how Sharia-specific transfer restrictions can be reflected in the operating model.

### Governance and Approval Workflows
Describe approval workflows for issuance, distribution events, registry updates, and exceptional actions. Explain support for maker-checker controls, segregation of duties, and audit evidence.

### Identity, KYC, and Documentation
Explain how the platform manages participant onboarding, identity verification, document collection, and eligibility evidence. Clarify whether integrations with external KYC providers are supported.

### Integration Requirements
Describe integration support for core banking systems, treasury systems, payment rails, reporting tools, and supervisory access. State whether ISO 20022-based integration is supported.

### Deployment and Security
Describe deployment topology options, environment isolation, disaster recovery, audit logging, access control, and supervisory read access. State how the platform supports data residency requirements.

### Reporting and Oversight
Explain what operational, compliance, and audit reporting is available to issuers, operators, and regulators. Describe the evidence available for supervisory reviews.

## Non-Functional Requirements
The bank expects the platform to support institutional uptime targets, clear recovery procedures, role-based access control, immutable audit history, and secure API-based integration. Please identify which elements are native platform capabilities and which depend on deployment architecture or external systems.

## Response Instructions
Please provide a structured response covering:

- direct answer to each requirement area
- clear distinction between native capability, configuration, and integration scope
- deployment assumptions and delivery boundaries
- relevant standards support
- key implementation considerations for a first sukuk program

## Evaluation Priorities
Responses will be assessed against the following priorities:

- accuracy and honesty of capability representation
- suitability for regulated capital markets use cases
- support for Sharia-compliant operating models
- operational control and auditability
- integration readiness with institutional systems
- scalability for future issuance programs
