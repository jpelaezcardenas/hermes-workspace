## Key Lesson Learned

**Client-centric framing is the single largest scoring lever for content refresh exercises.** Loop 1 accurately described every DIDX improvement but scored 3/5 on both Flow and Client Centricity because each description led with what the technology does rather than what the client gains. Loop 2 restructured the same information — same facts, same technical accuracy — but led every section with the client's operational experience: "upgrades without maintenance windows," "applications experience no interruption," "for your business continuity planning." The result was +8 points without adding any new technical content. The lesson: in content refresh exercises, the refresh quality is determined not by how much new information is surfaced from the codebase, but by how effectively that information is translated into the evaluator's decision-making language.
