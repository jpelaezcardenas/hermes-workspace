# Request for Information

## Digital Platform for Tokenized Limited Partner Interests

**Issuing Entity:** Meridian Capital Partners, Luxembourg  
**Date Issued:** March 2026  
**Response Due:** April 15, 2026  
**RFI Reference:** MCP-RFI-2026-003

---

## Background

Meridian Capital Partners ("Meridian") is a Luxembourg-domiciled alternative investment fund manager (AIFM) authorized under the EU Alternative Investment Fund Managers Directive (AIFMD). Meridian manages EUR 4.2 billion across growth equity and venture strategies, with additional licensing from the Monetary Authority of Singapore (MAS) as a Registered Fund Management Company.

Meridian intends to launch the Meridian Technology Growth Fund IV ("Fund IV"), a EUR 500 million closed-end growth equity fund. Meridian is exploring digital infrastructure to represent LP interests as tokenized fund units, enabling more efficient capital call administration, distribution processing, secondary transfer facilitation, and investor reporting.

The fund will target qualified investors across two jurisdictions: the European Union (under MiCA and AIFMD frameworks) and Singapore (under the Securities and Futures Act). Meridian currently works with Apex Group as its fund administrator and uses Salesforce for investor relationship management and eFront for portfolio management.

## Scope of Information Requested

Meridian invites technology platform providers to describe their capabilities across the following areas. Responses should address current production capabilities, clearly distinguish between native features and integration-dependent functionality, and identify any gaps or limitations honestly.

### Section 1: Fund Unit Representation

1.1 Describe how your platform represents LP interests as digital tokens, including support for configurable denomination (EUR 100,000 minimum subscription) and fractional unit precision.

1.2 Explain how the platform tracks capital commitments, funded amounts, and unfunded obligations for each LP.

1.3 Describe support for multiple share classes or series within a single fund vehicle, if applicable.

### Section 2: Compliance and Investor Eligibility

2.1 Describe how the platform enforces dual-jurisdiction compliance: EU qualified investor requirements (under MiCA and AIFMD) and Singapore accredited investor criteria (under MAS Securities and Futures Act).

2.2 Explain the identity verification model and how investor eligibility claims are managed, updated, and re-verified over time.

2.3 Describe transfer restriction enforcement capabilities: lock-up periods (3-year minimum from subscription), GP consent requirements for secondary transfers, and right of first refusal (ROFR) mechanisms with configurable exercise windows.

### Section 3: Capital Calls and Distribution

3.1 Describe how the platform supports capital call workflows, including drawdown notice generation, commitment tracking, and LP notification.

3.2 Explain how distributions are processed, including the ability to handle distribution waterfall calculations (return of capital, preferred return hurdles, carried interest splits, and catch-up provisions).

3.3 Describe the platform's capabilities for automating or supporting NAV reporting and reconciliation with external fund administrators.

### Section 4: Secondary Transfers

4.1 Describe how the platform facilitates secondary transfers of LP interests, including compliance re-verification of buyers and integration with existing transfer restriction rules.

4.2 Explain how GP consent workflows are handled for secondary transfers.

4.3 Describe any marketplace or matching functionality for secondary LP interest transactions.

### Section 5: Integration and Reporting

5.1 Describe API capabilities for integration with fund administration platforms (specifically Apex Group), CRM systems (Salesforce), and portfolio management systems (eFront).

5.2 Describe the investor portal capabilities, including visibility into commitment status, capital call history, distribution history, and current NAV per unit.

5.3 Explain the platform's ability to generate regulatory reports compliant with AIFMD Annex IV reporting and MAS Form 1/1A requirements.

### Section 6: Security and Data Residency

6.1 Describe data protection measures, including encryption standards and access controls.

6.2 Explain how the platform supports data residency requirements where EU investor data must remain in EU infrastructure and Singapore investor data must remain in Singapore infrastructure.

6.3 Provide details on security certifications held (SOC 2 Type II, ISO 27001, GDPR compliance).

### Section 7: Deployment and Implementation

7.1 Describe deployment options (cloud, on-premises, hybrid) and indicative timelines for initial production deployment.

7.2 Provide a high-level implementation approach, including phasing and key milestones.

7.3 Describe your commercial model (licensing structure, support tiers, professional services).

## Submission Instructions

Responses should be structured to address each section above. Respondents are encouraged to include relevant platform screenshots, architecture diagrams, and references to comparable deployments. Meridian values transparency regarding capability boundaries and appreciates honest disclosure of any gaps or limitations.

Please direct all responses and queries to the Meridian Capital Partners Digital Infrastructure Program Office.
