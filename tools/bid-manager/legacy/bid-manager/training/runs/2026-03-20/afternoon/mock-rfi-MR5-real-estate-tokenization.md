# Request for Information

## Fractional Ownership of Commercial Real Estate Assets via Digital Token Infrastructure

**Issued by:** Brickstone Digital Assets LLC
**RFI Reference:** BDA-RFI-2026-007
**Date of Issue:** 15 March 2026
**Responses Due:** 15 April 2026

---

## Background

Brickstone Digital Assets is a Dubai-based PropTech firm licensed by the Dubai Virtual Assets Regulatory Authority (VARA) and registered with the Abu Dhabi Global Market (ADGM). Brickstone seeks to launch a platform enabling fractional ownership of Grade A commercial properties across the UAE, starting with three initial assets:

- Dubai Marina office tower (AED 450 million)
- DIFC retail complex (AED 280 million)
- Jebel Ali logistics warehouse (AED 180 million)

Each property is held in a dedicated Special Purpose Vehicle (SPV). Target investors include UAE residents (retail, minimum AED 5,000 investment) and international qualified investors (minimum AED 100,000).

---

## Scope of Information Requested

Brickstone invites technology platform vendors to provide detailed information addressing the following areas. Respondents should clearly distinguish between capabilities available today, capabilities requiring integration with third-party systems, and capabilities on product roadmaps.

### Section A: Platform and Company Overview

1. Provide a description of your company, including founding date, headcount, geographic presence, and relevant certifications (ISO 27001, SOC 2 Type II, or equivalent).
2. Describe your platform's architecture at a high level, including deployment model options (cloud, on-premises, hybrid).
3. List all asset classes your platform supports natively and describe how real estate tokenization is handled.

### Section B: Token Design and Issuance

4. Describe how fractional ownership of a real estate SPV is represented as digital tokens, including the token standard used and how fractional units are configured.
5. Explain the asset creation workflow, from property registration through token deployment. Include screenshots or workflow descriptions where available.
6. How does the platform capture property-specific metadata (GPS coordinates, property classification, building specifications, RERA registration numbers)?

### Section C: Investor Onboarding and Compliance

7. Describe your investor onboarding process, including identity verification, KYC/AML procedures, and document collection capabilities.
8. Explain how the platform supports tiered investor categories with different eligibility criteria (UAE retail investors vs. international qualified investors).
9. How does the platform enforce RERA compliance, including foreign ownership restrictions for specific property types and geographic zones?
10. Describe how AML/CFT compliance aligned with UAE Central Bank guidelines and FATF recommendations is achieved, including ongoing transaction monitoring and suspicious transaction reporting.

### Section D: Rental Yield Distribution

11. Describe how the platform handles automated quarterly rental yield distribution to all token holders.
12. How are deductions for property management fees, maintenance reserves, and applicable VAT calculated and applied before distribution?
13. Can the platform generate investor statements (quarterly and annual) showing holdings, yield received, capital gains/losses, and current NAV?

### Section E: Secondary Market and Transfer Controls

14. Describe the secondary market mechanism available for token holders to trade their positions. Can token holders list tokens for sale with price limits, and can buyers browse available listings?
15. What transfer restrictions can be enforced (minimum holding requirements, pre-emption rights, maximum holder counts)?
16. How does the platform ensure compliance re-verification of buyers before transfer completion on the secondary market?

### Section F: Valuation and Data Feeds

17. How does the platform integrate with external property valuation firms for periodic NAV updates?
18. Is there a mechanism to reconcile and flag valuation discrepancies from multiple independent sources exceeding a defined threshold (e.g., 10%)?

### Section G: Governance

19. Describe any governance mechanism allowing token holders to vote on major decisions such as property disposition, major capital expenditure, or change of property manager.
20. What quorum and approval threshold mechanisms are supported?

### Section H: Exit and Liquidation

21. When an underlying property is sold, describe how the platform handles pro-rata distribution of sale proceeds to token holders and retirement of the associated tokens.

### Section I: Technology and Integration

22. Describe integration capabilities with UAE national identity systems (Emirates ID verification) for resident investor onboarding.
23. What APIs, SDKs, or integration surfaces does the platform expose for connection with external systems (property management, accounting, CRM)?
24. Describe the platform's monitoring, alerting, and operational observability capabilities.

### Section J: Security and Resilience

25. Describe your key management approach, including custody integration and wallet security.
26. What are the platform's availability, disaster recovery, and business continuity commitments?
27. Describe audit trail and regulatory reporting capabilities.
