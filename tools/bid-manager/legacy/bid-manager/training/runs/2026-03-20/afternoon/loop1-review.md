## Top 3 Strengths

1. **Honest capability boundaries** — Every section where DALP cannot fully deliver (governance voting app, secondary market order book, tax calculations, Emirates ID, valuation discrepancy flagging) is stated directly with clear language about what is available and what requires integration.
2. **Strong visual evidence** — 11 screenshots placed inline with descriptive captions. Good variety across dashboard, asset designer, compliance, real estate, monitoring, and identity modules.
3. **Clean IP compliance** — Zero violations across all five IP checklist categories.

## Top 3 Weaknesses (with fix instructions)

1. **Skeleton coverage gaps** — The response skips several rfi-full skeleton sections (Ecosystem and Distribution, Target Operating Model, Blockchain and Ledger Support, Wallet Infrastructure). **Fix:** Add at minimum a condensed Target Operating Model section showing governance roles mapped to Brickstone's org, and a Blockchain and Ledger section establishing EVM-native positioning.

2. **Weak evaluator value threading** — Several sections answer the technical question but don't connect to what it means for Brickstone's business. Section C.10 on AML/CFT is a good example: it explains the architecture but doesn't say "this means your compliance team can demonstrate to VARA that every transfer was verified before execution." **Fix:** Add a business-value closing sentence to each major section, framing the capability in terms of Brickstone's operational reality.

3. **Coverage Summary table needs enrichment** — The table is useful but flat. **Fix:** Add a brief "Implication" column or convert the most important rows into short prose paragraphs that explain not just what is available but why it matters for Brickstone's three-property launch.
