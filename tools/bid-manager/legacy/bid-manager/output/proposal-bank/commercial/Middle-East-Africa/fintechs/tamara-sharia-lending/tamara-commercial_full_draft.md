## 2. Investment Rationale

### 2.1 Cost of Current Approach

The current approach to Sharia-compliant funding involves manual processes, fragmented systems, and limited investor transparency. These constraints create hidden costs across multiple dimensions:

**Sharia compliance overhead**: Manual tracking of board approvals, manual generation of compliance evidence, and ad hoc documentation for Sharia reviews consume significant compliance team time. The inability to demonstrate systematic Sharia compliance limits investor appetite from institutions with strict Sharia governance requirements.

**Operational overhead**: Manual reconciliation across merchant systems, funding partners, and internal systems requires dedicated operations staff. Data inconsistencies between systems create exception-handling workarounds that consume additional resources.

**Investor limitation**: Limited transparency on pool composition and performance restricts investor appetite, resulting in higher funding costs or constrained capacity. The inability to demonstrate granular Sharia compliance audit trails limits participation from institutional investors with Sharia governance requirements.

**Regulatory compliance cost**: Manual evidence production for regulatory reviews (SAMA, CMA) consumes compliance team time. Ad hoc reporting approaches require repeated effort for each regulatory request rather than automated generation from authoritative data.

The platform addresses these constraints through automation, integration, and audit capabilities that collapse the hidden cost structure while enabling scale and Sharia compliance.

### 2.2 DALP Economic Impact

DALP changes the economics by providing a unified platform for asset lifecycle management, investor reporting, regulatory compliance, and Sharia governance.

| Cost Driver | Current State | DALP Impact | Mechanism |
|-------------|---------------|-------------|-----------|
| Sharia compliance | 20+ hours per review | 2-3 hours | Automated evidence generation |
| Operations staff | 4-6 FTE for reconciliation | 1-2 FTE reduction | Automated reconciliation |
| Investor onboarding | 2-3 weeks per investor | 2-3 days | Self-service portal with Sharia verification |
| Regulatory reporting | 40+ hours per report | 2-3 hours | Automated generation |
| Exception handling | 15-20% of volume | <5% | Real-time validation |
| Merchant onboarding | 4-6 weeks | 1-2 weeks | API-first integration |

The platform enables approximately 60-70% reduction in operational overhead for funding management, with the percentage varying based on current process maturity and volume profile. Sharia compliance overhead reduction is significant given the current manual approach.

### 2.3 ROI Framework

Return on investment combines multiple value drivers:

**Cost avoidance**: The platform avoids costs that would be incurred through alternative approaches, including build costs, integration costs, and compliance costs associated with assembling point solutions.

**Operational efficiency**: Direct labor savings from automation and process improvement translate to cost reduction that compounds annually.

**Revenue enablement**: Improved investor transparency enables access to larger investor pools, including institutional investors with Sharia governance requirements. Access to Sharia-conscious institutional capital can reduce funding costs by 50-150 basis points on applicable volumes.

**Risk reduction**: Improved audit trails and compliance evidence reduce regulatory risk, Sharia board review effort, and remediation costs.

The ROI calculation depends on assumptions about current cost structure, transaction volumes, and funding scale. Detailed ROI modeling is available through commercial validation workshops.

## 3. Commercial Model

### 3.1 Licensing Structure

The platform operates on an annual subscription model with tiered options:

| Tier | Description | Included Elements |
|------|-------------|-------------------|
| Foundation | Core platform capabilities | Asset lifecycle, compliance modules, API access |
| Professional | Enhanced capabilities | Advanced reporting, Sharia governance, analytics, additional integrations |
| Enterprise | Full capabilities | Dedicated environment, custom development, premium support |

For Tamara's requirements, the Professional tier is recommended, providing the capability scope required for Sharia-compliant tokenized lending with appropriate Sharia governance, integration, and reporting capabilities.

### 3.2 Pricing Components

**Platform Subscription** (Annual)

- Environment: Production plus DR
- Users: Unlimited business users
- API calls: 100,000/month included
- Support: Standard business hours
- Sharia Governance Module: Included

**Additional Components**

- Additional API calls: Tiered pricing per 10,000 calls
- Additional environments: Dev, test, UAT available
- Extended support: 24/7 coverage available
- Premium support: Dedicated support resources available

### 3.3 Volume Economics

Transaction processing pricing provides economies of scale:

| Volume Tier | Price Per Transaction |
|-------------|----------------------|
| First 50,000 | $0.18 |
| 50,001 - 200,000 | $0.14 |
| 200,001 - 500,000 | $0.12 |
| Above 500,000 | $0.10 |

Volume pricing applies to asset lifecycle events including creation, state changes, profit calculations, and transfers. The volume tier structure enables cost reduction as platform adoption increases.

**Vendor consolidation**: Single platform for digital asset lifecycle reduces vendor management overhead, integration complexity, and support coordination.
