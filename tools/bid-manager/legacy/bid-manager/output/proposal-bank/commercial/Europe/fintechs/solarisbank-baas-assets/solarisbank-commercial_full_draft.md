## 1. Executive Summary

### 1.1 Programme Context

Solarisbank's procurement for BaaS digital asset module addresses a strategic imperative to extend the platform's value proposition to digital asset capabilities, enabling partners to offer tokenized products within the existing BaaS framework. The programme responds to three converging drivers: partner demand for digital asset products, competitive pressure to offer comprehensive embedded finance, and regulatory readiness for MiCA and DORA.

The programme represents a significant investment in platform infrastructure. The commercial proposal provides the financial framework that enables Solarisbank to evaluate the investment against alternative approaches and make an informed procurement decision.

### 1.2 Recommended Commercial Model

SettleMint recommends a commercial model comprising platform licensing, implementation services, and ongoing support, structured to align with Solarisbank's BaaS operational model and partner scaling expectations. The model specifically addresses multi-tenant requirements and MiCA/DORA compliance through appropriate capability tiers.

The model provides predictable cost structures through the implementation phase with clear transitions to operating cost normalization. Volume-based pricing elements are included for transaction processing and partner onboarding at rates that enable economic scale as the platform matures.

## 2. Investment Rationale

### 2.1 Cost of Current Approach

The alternative to platform acquisition involves either building internally or using multiple point solutions. Each approach creates hidden costs:

**Build cost**: Internal development of digital asset capability requires specialized expertise, significant development time, and ongoing maintenance. The build approach creates technical debt and delays time-to-market.

**Point solution cost**: Assembling multiple vendor solutions creates integration complexity, operational overhead, and vendor coordination burden. Each integration creates data reconciliation requirements and potential for inconsistency.

**Operational cost**: Both approaches require dedicated operational resources for digital asset management, compliance monitoring, and incident response. The BaaS model requires multi-tenant operational capability that is difficult to achieve with non-native approaches.

**Compliance cost**: Meeting MiCA and DORA requirements requires specific technical capabilities and evidence generation that generic solutions may not provide.

The platform addresses these constraints through a purpose-built solution with BaaS-native architecture and embedded regulatory compliance.

### 2.2 DALP Economic Impact

DALP changes the economics by providing a unified platform with BaaS-specific capabilities.

| Cost Driver | Alternative Approach | DALP Impact | Mechanism |
|-------------|---------------------|-------------|-----------|
| Development time | 18-24 months | Immediate | Pre-built platform |
| Integration | 6+ months | 2-3 months | Pre-built adapters |
| Operational staff | 6-8 FTE | 2-3 FTE | Multi-tenant automation |
| Compliance setup | 3-6 months | Included | Pre-built MiCA/DORA |
| Partner onboarding | 4-6 weeks per partner | 1-2 weeks | Self-service |

The platform enables approximately 50-60% reduction in operational overhead for digital asset services, with the percentage varying based on current approach and scale.

### 2.3 ROI Framework

Return on investment combines multiple value drivers:

**Cost avoidance**: The platform avoids build costs, integration costs, and compliance setup costs that would be incurred through alternative approaches.

**Operational efficiency**: Direct labor savings from automation and process improvement translate to cost reduction that compounds annually.

**Revenue enablement**: Digital asset capabilities enable new partner revenue streams and existing partner expansion.

**Risk reduction**: Pre-built compliance reduces regulatory risk and remediation costs.

The ROI calculation depends on assumptions about partner scale and revenue model. Detailed ROI modeling is available through commercial validation workshops.

## 3. Commercial Model

### 3.1 Licensing Structure

The platform operates on an annual subscription model with tiered options:

| Tier | Description | Included Elements |
|------|-------------|-------------------|
| Foundation | Core platform capabilities | Asset lifecycle, basic compliance, API access |
| Professional | Enhanced capabilities | Multi-tenant, MiCA/DORA, advanced reporting |
| Enterprise | Full capabilities | Dedicated environment, custom development, premium support |

For Solarisbank's requirements, the Professional tier is recommended, providing multi-tenant capability with MiCA/DORA compliance at appropriate scale.

### 3.2 Multi-Tenant Pricing

The platform supports multi-tenant BaaS deployment with the following pricing structure:

**Platform Subscription** (Annual)

- Base platform: Multi-tenant deployment with unlimited partners
- Environment: Production plus DR
- API calls: 250,000/month included
- Support: Standard business hours
- MiCA/DORA Compliance Module: Included

**Partner Capacity**

- Up to 10 partners: Included in platform subscription
- Additional partners: Per-partner fee (volume discounts available)

### 3.3 Transaction Pricing

Transaction processing pricing provides economies of scale:

| Volume Tier | Price Per Transaction |
|-------------|----------------------|
| First 100,000 | $0.10 |
| 100,001 - 500,000 | $0.08 |
| 500,001 - 1,000,000 | $0.06 |
| Above 1,000,000 | $0.05 |

Volume pricing applies to asset lifecycle events including creation, state changes, and transfers.

### 4.2 Investment Structure

Implementation investment is structured as fixed-price with milestone payments:

| Milestone | Payment Schedule | Amount |
|-----------|-----------------|--------|
| Contract signature | 20% | Upon contract signature |
| Design approval | 20% | Upon design sign-off |

| UAT go-live | 20% | Upon UAT entry |

### 4.3 Investment Assumptions

The implementation investment assumes:

- Standard scope as defined in RFP
- Solarisbank provides dedicated project team (4-6 resources)
- Solarisbank provides timely access to systems and stakeholders
- No significant scope changes
- Remote delivery model with quarterly on-site workshops

Assumptions that prove incorrect may result in change requests with associated cost impact.

## 5. Ongoing Investment

### 5.1 Annual Operating Cost

Platform operation involves recurring costs:

**Platform Subscription**: Annual license fee as defined in Section 3.2, includes MiCA/DORA compliance modules

**Support Services**: Annual support retainer providing:

- Access to support team during business hours
- Incident response and escalation
- Access to knowledge base and documentation
- Quarterly platform updates

**Infrastructure Costs**: Direct costs for cloud infrastructure, paid to cloud provider, estimated based on deployment configuration

### 5.2 Cost Progression

| Year | Platform | Support | Infrastructure | Total |
|------|----------|---------|---------------|-------|
| Year 1 (partial) | Prorated | Included | Estimated | Launch + ongoing |
| Year 2 | Full | Full | Full | Full operating |
| Year 3 | Full | Full | Full | Full operating |

Year 1 reflects partial-year operation based on implementation timeline.

### 5.3 Cost Predictability

The commercial model emphasizes predictability:

- Platform subscription is fixed regardless of transaction volume within tier
- Partner capacity is defined with clear expansion pricing
- Support is fixed regardless of incident volume within normal ranges
- Infrastructure scales predictably based on environment configuration

Hidden cost escalation is avoided through defined cost boundaries and scope management.

## 6. Value Analysis

### 6.1 Buy vs. Build Comparison

Building equivalent capability internally would require:

| Component | Build Cost Estimate | DALP Comparison |
|-----------|---------------------|-----------------|
| Platform development | 18-24 months | Available immediately |
| Multi-tenant architecture | 6-9 months | Pre-built |
| MiCA/DORA compliance | 6-12 months | Pre-certified |
| Integration development | 6-9 months | Pre-built adapters |
| Operational capability | 12-18 months | Production-ready |
| Total estimated build | 48-72 months | Significant acceleration |

The platform approach provides time-to-value measured in weeks rather than years, with reduced execution risk and proven regulatory compliance.

### 6.2 Single-Platform Economics

Using DALP for BaaS digital asset module creates operational leverage:

**Future extension potential**: The platform supports additional asset types, markets, and use cases without additional platform investment. This creates incremental value as Solarisbank's partner digital asset strategy evolves.

**Operational leverage**: The operational model, support infrastructure, and team capability transfer across use cases, reducing incremental operating cost for additional programmes.

**Vendor consolidation**: Single platform for digital asset lifecycle reduces vendor management overhead, integration complexity, and support coordination.

### 6.3 Risk-Adjusted Value

The proposal value analysis incorporates risk adjustment:

| Factor | Impact | Mitigation |
|--------|--------|------------|
| Implementation overrun | Potential 10-20% | Milestone structure |
| Integration complexity | Potential delays | Early integration scoping |
| Regulatory change | Unknown | Configuration flexibility |
| Partner adoption variance | Revenue impact | Tiered pricing |

The risk-adjusted value considers probability-weighted outcomes across scenarios.
