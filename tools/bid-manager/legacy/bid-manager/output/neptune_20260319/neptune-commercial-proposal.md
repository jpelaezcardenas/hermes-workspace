## Executive Summary

Neptune Maritime Leasing is pursuing one of the most ambitious programmes in maritime finance: the tokenization of its entire fleet of special purpose companies, with the objective of announcing at Posidonia 2026 that Neptune has become the first shipping company to digitize its SPC fleet using blockchain-based infrastructure. This is not a pilot programme or a conceptual exercise. It is a board-approved initiative with a defined timeline, a clear commercial rationale, and a forcing function that concentrates decision-making: Posidonia opens in Athens in early June 2026.

The commercial opportunity is substantial. Neptune operates a corporate structure comprising a Jersey holding company, a Marshall Islands sub-holding, and more than sixty special purpose companies, each owning a single vessel. The current model relies on concentrated maritime capital pools, manual compliance tracking per entity, and no digital distribution channel for investor participation. Tokenizing the SPC fleet transforms how Neptune raises capital, onboards investors, distributes yield, and governs its corporate hierarchy. The long-term vision extends to 200 to 300 vessels across Neptune and its affiliate fleets, with the potential to offer a white-label tokenization platform for the broader shipping industry.

Neptune faces a strategic choice: build a custom tokenization capability in-house, assemble it from multiple specialized vendors, or adopt a purpose-built platform that covers the full digital asset lifecycle. SettleMint's recommendation is clear. DALP (Digital Asset Lifecycle Platform) provides the complete infrastructure required to issue, govern, and service tokenized SPC equity across Neptune's fleet, with compliance enforcement, identity management, custody integration, and operational tooling built into a single platform. DALP eliminates the need to assemble and maintain separate systems for issuance, compliance, settlement, and servicing, and it avoids the 12 to 24 months of engineering effort that an internal build would demand.

The recommended model is phased, moving from a pilot deployment of 3 to 5 SPCs through to full fleet deployment and Posidonia readiness in under three months:

- **Phase 0 (Foundation):** Deploy the DALP platform instance, configure the identity and compliance framework, deploy 3 to 5 pilot SPC equity tokens, and enable MindWave to begin custom UI development. Duration: 6 weeks.

- **Phase 2 (Posidonia Readiness):** All 60 SPCs deployed, investor portal functional, at least one SPC with minted tokens and active investors. Target: early June 2026.
- **Phase 3 (Scale):** Post-Posidonia expansion to 200 to 300 vessels, secondary trading capability, and white-label platform potential.

## Investment Rationale

### The Cost of the Current Approach

Neptune's current operating model for managing its SPC fleet carries costs that are not always visible on a balance sheet but are material in aggregate. Understanding these costs is essential context for evaluating the DALP investment.

**No digital distribution channel.** Neptune's sixty-plus SPCs represent a significant portfolio of maritime assets, but there is no digital mechanism for distributing ownership interests in these entities to investors. Capital raising relies on traditional, relationship-driven channels that limit the investor base to established maritime capital pools. This concentration creates dependency on a narrow set of funding sources and limits Neptune's ability to access institutional capital from investors who increasingly expect digital infrastructure for asset access, reporting, and governance participation.

**Concentrated maritime capital pools.** Maritime finance has historically operated within a closed ecosystem of specialized lenders, private equity firms, and family offices. While these relationships are valuable, they constrain Neptune's ability to diversify its capital base. Tokenization opens the possibility of fractional participation in individual SPCs, enabling a broader range of institutional investors to gain exposure to maritime assets without taking full vessel-level positions. Without a digital platform, this diversification remains theoretical.

**No investor portal or self-service infrastructure.** Investors in Neptune's SPCs currently have no unified digital interface for viewing their positions, tracking distributions, monitoring compliance status, or participating in governance decisions. Information flows through manual channels: email, spreadsheets, periodic reports. For a structure with five investors at the parent level and the ambition to scale beyond that, this approach creates administrative friction that grows with each additional investor and entity.

**Manual compliance tracking per entity.** Each SPC may operate under different compliance requirements depending on its flag state, the jurisdiction of its investors, and the specific terms of its financing arrangements. Tracking compliance manually across sixty-plus entities means maintaining separate records, performing separate checks, and relying on human processes to catch exceptions. The administrative burden scales linearly with entity count, and the risk of compliance gaps increases with every manual handoff.

**Friction in investor onboarding.** Onboarding new investors to an SPC requires KYC/AML verification, eligibility checks, regulatory classification, and documentation. Without a digital identity and credentialing framework, this process is repeated from scratch for each investor-entity combination. An investor participating in multiple SPCs must be verified multiple times through disconnected processes. This friction discourages participation and increases the cost per investor relationship.

**No integrated audit trail.** Compliance officers, regulators, and institutional investors increasingly expect a single, immutable record of all ownership changes, compliance checks, and governance actions. Neptune's current approach distributes this information across multiple systems and manual processes, making it difficult to produce the kind of comprehensive audit trail that institutional investors and regulators demand.

**Scaling costs that grow linearly with entity count.** Perhaps the most significant structural issue is that Neptune's current operating model does not scale sub-linearly. Adding the sixty-first SPC costs roughly the same in administrative overhead as the first. Adding the two-hundredth vessel to the affiliate fleet replicates the same compliance tracking, investor communication, and governance administration as every vessel before it. There is no template effect, no automation benefit, no configuration reuse. Each entity is managed as a standalone operation because the infrastructure does not support anything else.

**Opportunity cost of delayed capital formation.** Every month that Neptune operates without a digital investor access channel is a month where potential capital is not flowing into the fleet. Maritime assets are capital-intensive, and the ability to access broader institutional capital pools through tokenized fractional participation could materially reduce Neptune's cost of capital. The delay is not theoretical: competitors and adjacent industries are already exploring tokenized asset structures, and the first-mover advantage at Posidonia has a finite window.

The aggregate effect of these limitations is that Neptune's SPC fleet, despite its underlying asset value and operational track record, is harder to capitalize, more expensive to administer, and less attractive to institutional investors than it needs to be. The Posidonia announcement is not just a brand exercise; it signals that Neptune has solved these structural constraints.

### Why DALP Changes the Economics

DALP addresses Neptune's cost structure through five mechanisms that compound across the sixty-plus SPC fleet.

**Configuration-driven SPC deployment, not sixty bespoke builds.** DALP's equity factory pattern deploys individual SPC equity tokens through a standardized `createEquity()` function. Each call deploys a complete token with proxy contract, access manager, token identity, and trusted issuers registry. The configuration for each SPC (compliance modules, jurisdiction rules, investor caps, transfer restrictions) is parameterized, not custom-coded. Once Neptune establishes the template for the first few SPCs, deploying the remaining fifty-five is a scripting exercise measured in hours, not a development project measured in months. This is the single most important economic advantage DALP provides for Neptune's specific use case.

**Reusable compliance templates across the fleet.** DALP ships with 18 compliance module types covering country restrictions, investor accreditation, holding period enforcement, supply limits, transfer approvals, and more. Neptune configures these modules once per jurisdiction pattern and applies them across all SPCs that share the same regulatory profile. If thirty SPCs share the same flag state and investor eligibility rules, those thirty SPCs share the same compliance configuration. Changes to compliance rules propagate without modifying smart contract code. For a fleet operating across multiple jurisdictions, this reusability reduces both the initial configuration effort and the ongoing compliance maintenance burden.

**Production-grade platform versus in-house build.** Building the equivalent capability in-house would require smart contract engineering, compliance rule engine development, identity management, custody orchestration, settlement logic, observability tooling, and API integration layers. Based on SettleMint's experience across dozens of institutional engagements, this represents 12 to 24 months of engineering effort and a permanent team of 6 to 12 engineers. DALP converts this capital-intensive, unpredictable build into a predictable operating expense with no permanent engineering overhead.

**MindWave partnership model reduces SettleMint services scope.** Neptune's relationship with MindWave, an Athens-based development firm, provides a natural division of labour. SettleMint provides the DALP platform (tokenization backbone, compliance engine, identity framework, APIs). MindWave builds the custom investor-facing UI, handles front-end integration, and manages Neptune-specific workflows. This model means Neptune is not dependent on SettleMint for all custom development, and SettleMint's commercial offer stays focused on the platform itself rather than professional services.

**Institutional credibility through proven infrastructure.** For Neptune's Posidonia announcement to carry weight with maritime industry participants, institutional investors, and potential white-label partners, the underlying platform must be demonstrably production-grade. DALP's track record with tier-1 banks (OCBC, Commerzbank, Standard Chartered), sovereign entities (Saudi RER, State Bank of India), and central bank programmes provides the institutional credibility that validates Neptune's tokenization initiative as infrastructure, not experiment.

## Licensing Model

### Platform Licensing Philosophy

DALP uses a platform licensing model, not a per-transaction or per-asset fee structure. This distinction is fundamental to how SettleMint serves regulated institutions, and it is directly relevant to Neptune's use case.

There are no per-transaction fees. Neptune will not be charged per mint, transfer, settlement, or compliance check. Deploying sixty SPC equity tokens and processing transfers across all of them incurs no incremental licensing cost. There are no per-token fees. Creating the sixty-first SPC token costs the same as creating the first. There are no per-user fees for end-investors. As Neptune onboards investors, whether five or fifty, investor growth is a business outcome, not a cost driver.

Licensing is structured as an annual or monthly subscription, giving Neptune budget predictability and avoiding the variable-cost surprises common in transaction-based pricing models. This approach reflects SettleMint's belief that platform adoption should accelerate usage, not constrain it. For Neptune's use case specifically, where sixty-plus SPCs will each generate compliance checks on every transfer, identity verifications on every investor action, and yield distributions to all token holders, a per-operation pricing model would create cost anxiety that works against the programme's success.

### What Neptune Is Buying

When Neptune invests in DALP, it is acquiring five distinct but interconnected capabilities that together form a complete digital asset operating system.

**Issuance capability.** DALP provides the ability to create, configure, and deploy digital asset tokens representing SPC equity. This is not limited to a single token type; the platform supports bonds, equities, funds, stablecoins, deposits, real estate, and precious metals, plus the Configurable Token for custom asset types. For Neptune, this means the SPC equity tokens are the first asset class, but the platform can support additional asset classes (bond issuances at the SPC level, fund products at the fleet level) without additional platform investment.

**Compliance capability.** DALP provides 18 compliance module types that enforce regulatory and structural rules at the smart contract level. This is fundamentally different from post-trade compliance monitoring. Every transfer is validated before execution. If a transfer would violate a configured rule, it is blocked at the protocol level. This ex-ante enforcement provides deterministic compliance: the rule is either enforced or it is not, with no middle ground.

**Identity capability.** DALP integrates with OnchainID to provide identity and credential management for investors. Rather than managing investor eligibility manually, Neptune can configure the identity registry to require specific claims (KYC verification, accreditation status, jurisdictional eligibility) before allowing token holdings. The identity framework supports multiple identity providers, multiple credential types, and claim revocation.

**Settlement capability.** DALP provides atomic settlement for digital asset transactions. For Neptune, this means yield distributions are atomic: either all investors receive their distributions or none do. The platform also supports Delivery-versus-Payment (DvP) and Exchange-versus-Payment (XvP) for more complex settlement scenarios.

**Servicing capability.** DALP provides the operational tools to manage digital assets over their lifecycle: yield distributions, corporate actions, airdrops, and reporting. For Neptune, the servicing capability means SPC dividends can be distributed to all token holders with a single transaction, rather than manual wire transfers to each investor.

The licensing model is designed so that Neptune's economics improve as scale increases. More SPCs, more investors, more transactions: all drive business value without driving incremental platform cost.

### Platform Licensing in Practice: How It Works

Understanding how the platform license operates in practice helps Neptune's finance and procurement teams evaluate the commercial framework.

**License activation.** The license is activated when SettleMint provisions the DALP environment. At that point, Neptune has access to the development environment and can begin configuration and testing. The development license rate applies from activation until production go-live.

**Production go-live.** Production go-live occurs when Neptune confirms that the platform is ready for live SPC tokens and investor operations. This is a formal milestone documented in the Statement of Work. From the go-live date, the production license rate applies.

**Tier upgrades.** If Neptune's requirements evolve (for example, expanding from 60 to 300 SPCs, or requiring Enterprise-tier capabilities beyond the current scope), the license can be upgraded. Tier upgrades are typically priced on a pro-rata basis for the remainder of the then-current term.

**Renewal.** At the end of the initial 2-year term, the license renews automatically unless either party provides 90 days written notice of non-renewal. Renewal pricing is at then-current list prices, with multi-year commitments receiving preferential terms.

**Termination.** If Neptune terminates for convenience, the license terminates at the end of the notice period. Neptune retains access to the platform during the notice period and has 90 days post-termination to export data. Token contracts on the blockchain remain operational independently of the DALP platform.

Neptune's primary use case centres on equity tokens for SPC ownership, but the license does not restrict Neptune from exploring other asset types (for example, bond tokens for SPC debt instruments or fund tokens for fleet-level aggregate exposure) as the programme matures.

**Compliance modules.** All 18 compliance module types are included. For Neptune, the most immediately relevant modules are: CountryAllowListComplianceModule (jurisdiction-based investor restrictions), InvestorCountComplianceModule (cap on investors per SPC), TransferApprovalComplianceModule (approval workflows for secondary transfers), TokenSupplyLimitComplianceModule (cap on total supply per SPC), SMARTIdentityVerificationComplianceModule (KYC/identity enforcement), CappedComplianceModule (per-investor holding limits), and TimeLockComplianceModule (lock-up period enforcement). Additional modules for collateral backing, country blocklists, and transfer windows are available as Neptune's programme evolves.

### How Compliance Modules Work in Practice

Understanding how compliance modules work in practice helps Neptune's compliance team evaluate whether DALP meets their requirements.

**Ex-ante enforcement.** Compliance modules enforce rules before a transaction completes. When an investor initiates a transfer, the smart contract checks the transfer against all configured compliance modules before executing. If any module returns a failure, the transfer is rejected. This is fundamentally different from post-trade monitoring, where violations are detected after the fact.

**Composable modules.** Multiple compliance modules can be applied to a single token. For example, an SPC token might have CountryAllowListComplianceModule (to restrict investor jurisdictions), CappedComplianceModule (to limit the maximum tokens any single investor can hold), and TransferApprovalComplianceModule (to require governance approval for transfers exceeding a threshold). All modules must pass for the transfer to execute.

**Parameter-driven configuration.** Each compliance module is configured through parameters, not custom code. The CountryAllowListComplianceModule takes a list of allowed country codes as a parameter. The InvestorCountComplianceModule takes a maximum investor count as a parameter. Changing a compliance rule is as simple as updating a parameter; no smart contract modification is required.

**Audit trail.** Every compliance check is logged on-chain. If a transfer is rejected, the reason is recorded in the transaction receipt. This provides a complete audit trail for regulators and compliance officers.

**Full API surface.** REST API (v2), oRPC, GraphQL (via subgraph), event webhooks, SDK (@settlemint/dalp-sdk), and CLI (301 commands across 26 command groups). MindWave will use these API surfaces to build Neptune's custom investor portal and integrate with Nathek's core banking systems.

**Addon capabilities.** Vault management, XvP/DvP settlement, token sales and primary offerings, airdrop distribution (push airdrop, vesting airdrop, time-bound airdrop for SPC dividend distribution), fixed yield schedules, and data feeds.

**Observability stack.** Pre-built Grafana dashboards, three-pillar observability (metrics via VictoriaMetrics, logs via Loki, traces via Tempo), and automated alerting. Neptune's operations team can monitor platform health, transaction volumes, compliance events, and integration status from a single interface.

### The API-First Architecture

DALP is built as an API-first platform. Every capability available through the web interface (dApp) is also available through the API. This is essential for Neptune's architecture because it enables MindWave to build the investor portal without being dependent on SettleMint's web development roadmap.

The API surface includes: REST API v2 for standard CRUD operations and asset lifecycle management, oRPC for type-safe API calls from strongly-typed clients, GraphQL via subgraph for flexible query operations and dashboard data, event webhooks for real-time notifications when specific events occur, SDK (@settlemint/dalp-sdk) for programmatic interaction from JavaScript/TypeScript environments, and CLI (301 commands across 26 command groups) for operational automation and scripting.

For MindWave's investor portal, the relevant API capabilities include: identity management (create and manage investor identities, issue and revoke claims), token operations (query token balances, initiate transfers, view transaction history), compliance operations (check compliance status, configure compliance modules), and yield distribution (initiate airdrops, query distribution history).

**Platform updates.** Access to all platform releases, security patches, compliance module updates, and new capabilities during the license term. Updates are managed by SettleMint under the Managed SaaS model, with coordinated change windows and no disruption to Neptune's operations.

SettleMint operates and manages the full DALP platform on dedicated cloud infrastructure. DALP is deployed within Neptune's own cloud environment (AWS, Azure, GCP) and managed by Neptune's operations team. SettleMint provides Helm charts, configuration guidance, and platform support. ### On-Premises

DALP is deployed within Neptune's own data centre. This model supports air-gapped environments and provides maximum infrastructure control. It requires client-provisioned Kubernetes, self-managed databases, a private container registry, and HSM or Vault-based key management.

This model is not recommended for Neptune's initial deployment given the Posidonia timeline and Neptune's current infrastructure capabilities.

### Hybrid

DALP components are split across environments. Common patterns include running the application layer in the cloud while key management operates on-premises, or operating a primary environment on-premises with a cloud-based disaster recovery site.

This model may become relevant as Neptune scales to 200 to 300 vessels and potentially operates tokenization infrastructure for other shipping companies.

### Deployment Model Recommendation Summary

For Neptune's initial engagement, the recommendation is unambiguous: Managed SaaS. The rationale is grounded in three practical constraints that all point to the same conclusion.

**Focus constraint.** Neptune's team should be spending the next twelve weeks on SPC configuration, compliance framework design, investor onboarding, and MindWave coordination, not on infrastructure operations. The deployment model can evolve. If Neptune's Phase 3 expansion to 200 to 300 vessels, or a white-label offering for other shipping companies, creates requirements for infrastructure control that Managed SaaS cannot satisfy, migrating to Private Cloud or Hybrid is a well-defined process supported by DALP's Helm-based deployment architecture. ### Phase 0 Detailed Activities

**Week 1-2: Environment and Identity Setup**

The first two weeks focus on getting the DALP platform operational and establishing the identity framework that underpins the entire programme. SettleMint provisions the Managed SaaS environment, which includes the DALP dApp (web administration interface), the DAPI (Durable API service for programmatic access), the indexer (for querying on-chain data), the signer service (for transaction signing), and the observability stack (Grafana dashboards, VictoriaMetrics for metrics, Loki for logs, Tempo for traces).

Simultaneously, the OnchainID identity registry is configured. This is the foundational layer for investor management: every investor who will hold SPC equity tokens must have an OnchainID identity established in the registry. The identity registry supports claim-based verification, where investor credentials (KYC verification, accreditation status, jurisdictional eligibility) are issued as claims on the identity. For Neptune's initial 5 investors, this means establishing their identities, issuing the relevant claims, and configuring the identity registry to recognize these claims for compliance purposes.

The RBAC (Role-Based Access Control) model is configured during this period. Neptune defines the roles that will interact with the platform: who can deploy new SPC tokens, who can configure compliance modules, who can approve investor onboarding, who can initiate yield distributions, and who can view reporting. The RBAC model supports hierarchical organization structures, meaning Neptune can map its existing organizational hierarchy onto DALP's role model.

**Week 3-4: Compliance Framework Configuration**

The compliance framework is the heart of the tokenization programme. Each SPC equity token must have compliance modules configured that reflect the legal and regulatory requirements applicable to that specific SPC. The compliance modules enforce rules ex-ante: before any transfer is executed, the smart contract checks that the transfer complies with all configured rules. If any rule fails, the transfer is blocked.

For Neptune, the relevant compliance modules include: CountryAllowListComplianceModule to restrict investor participation to approved jurisdictions (initially the jurisdictions of the 5 institutional investors), InvestorCountComplianceModule to enforce the maximum number of investors per SPC as permitted under the applicable securities exemption, TransferApprovalComplianceModule to require governance approval for any secondary market transfers, TokenSupplyLimitComplianceModule to cap the total token supply at the SPC's authorized equity value, SMARTIdentityVerificationComplianceModule to enforce KYC/AML verification before any token holding is permitted, CappedComplianceModule to limit the maximum tokens any single investor can hold, and TimeLockComplianceModule to enforce any lock-up periods applicable to the SPC's financing structure.

The compliance framework configuration in Phase 0 establishes the standard template that will be applied to all 60 SPCs. This template represents the "80 percent case": the compliance rules that apply to the majority of SPCs. For any SPCs with unique requirements (for example, an SPC with investors from a jurisdiction not covered by the standard template), the compliance framework supports adding SPC-specific modules on top of the template.

**Week 5-6: Pilot SPC Deployment**

The final two weeks of Phase 0 focus on deploying the first 3 to 5 pilot SPC equity tokens. These pilots are selected to represent different vessel types, flag states, and financing structures, providing coverage of the variation Neptune expects across the full fleet. Deploying these pilots validates the entire chain: the compliance configuration, the identity framework, the RBAC model, and the operational procedures.

Each pilot SPC is deployed using the `createEquity()` function, which creates a complete ERC-3643 token contract with: a proxy contract for upgradeability, an access manager for role-based permissions, a token identity containing metadata about the SPC and its underlying vessel, and a trusted issuers registry specifying which identities are authorized to receive tokens from this SPC.

The pilot deployment also includes minting an initial token supply and allocating it to the initial investors. For the 5 investors, this means their OnchainID identities are verified, their eligibility claims are confirmed, and tokens representing their SPC ownership are minted and transferred to their wallets.

MindWave uses the pilot deployment period to validate their API integration. The integration guide and sandbox access provided in Week 2 enable MindWave to begin building the investor portal UI against the development environment. By the end of Phase 0, MindWave should be able to query token balances, view transaction history, and initiate transfers through the API, validating that the integration approach is sound before they invest in building the full portal.

**Week 5-6: Yield Distribution and Integration Testing**

The yield distribution mechanism uses DALP's airdrop capability to distribute SPC dividends to token holders. When an SPC generates distributable income (from charter hire, vessel sale proceeds, or other sources), the yield distribution process begins at Neptune's instruction: the distribution amount and per-token allocation are specified, DALP's airdrop function calculates the allocation for each token holder based on their token balance at the snapshot date, and the tokens are distributed to all eligible holders in a single atomic transaction.

## Commercial Terms

### Contract Structure

A typical DALP engagement is governed by a layered contract structure that separates the platform relationship from specific implementation work. For Neptune, the recommended structure is:

| Agreement | Purpose | Term |
|---|---|---|
| Master Services Agreement (MSA) | Governs the overall commercial relationship, including general terms, liability, confidentiality, and dispute resolution | Duration of the engagement |
| Platform License Agreement (PLA) | Grants Neptune the right to use the DALP platform under the Enterprise tier; specifies the Managed SaaS deployment model and included capabilities | Initial 2-year term from go-live |
| Statement of Work (SOW) | Defines the implementation scope for Phases 0 and 1, including deliverables, acceptance criteria, timeline, and milestone-based payment schedule | Fixed-term per phase |
| Support Schedule | Specifies the Premium support tier, SLA targets, escalation procedures, and maintenance windows | Co-terminus with the Platform License Agreement |

This layered structure allows individual components to be amended without renegotiating the entire relationship. For example, Phase 3 expansion work would be covered under a new Statement of Work without modifying the MSA or PLA.
