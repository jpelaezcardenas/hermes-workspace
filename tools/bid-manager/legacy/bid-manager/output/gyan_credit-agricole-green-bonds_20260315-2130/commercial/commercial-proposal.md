## Executive Summary

This commercial proposal covers the licensing, implementation, support, governance, and commercial assumptions for a DALP-based tokenized green bonds platform programme for Crédit Agricole CIB. The recommended commercial structure is designed to align with the institution's phased rollout model, preserve budget transparency, and avoid hidden dependency costs during implementation and early production operation.

The proposed commercial posture favors a configuration-led base platform with clearly separated workstreams for platform subscription, implementation services, optional partner integrations, infrastructure, and support. That separation is important for institutional procurement because it allows architecture, security, finance, and business sponsors to assess value and delivery risk independently rather than through an opaque bundled quote.

## Commercial Principles

1. Pricing separates recurring platform rights, implementation effort, support services, and buyer-controlled third-party spend.
2. Assumptions are declared up front for environments, legal entities, users, and integration scope.
3. Change requests apply only when scope moves beyond the agreed baseline; normal configuration within agreed workstreams is planned into the implementation model.
4. Expansion to additional entities, products, or corridors should reuse the base control plane where possible rather than fund reimplementation.

It is intended to provide a stable commercial base for the initial programme and for measured expansion after governance approval.

## Implementation Model

The recommended implementation model is structured to give Crédit Agricole CIB objective commercial checkpoints rather than a single blended project cost with weak traceability. Each phase ends with specific evidence and sign-off outputs so that steering committees can approve the next funding and delivery step against real progress.

| Phase | Duration | Commercial basis | Key deliverables |
|---|---|---|---|
| Discovery and control mapping | 3 weeks | Fixed fee | Scope baseline, RACI, control requirements, integration inventory |
| Foundation and environments | 3 weeks | Fixed fee | Platform baseline, IAM approach, monitoring hooks, admin controls |
| Configuration and interfaces | 5 weeks | Fixed fee | Product workflows, role policies, data mappings, interface contracts |
| SIT and UAT readiness | 4 weeks | Fixed fee | Test evidence, operational procedures, reporting and export validation |
| Launch and hypercare | 4 weeks | Fixed fee | Controlled go-live, defect triage, handover, production review |
| **Total baseline** | **19 weeks** | **Milestone-based** | **Launch-ready first release** |

## Exclusions and Optional Items

The following items are excluded from the commercial baseline unless specifically contracted:

- Buyer cloud or infrastructure consumption charges.
- Third-party connectivity fees, market-infrastructure fees, or operator onboarding charges.
- External legal, tax, registry, ESG assurance, or market-specific advisory services.
- Net-new custom interfaces outside the agreed implementation baseline.
- Non-standard migration or remediation work on legacy data beyond the agreed target scope.

## Commercial Fit Summary

The proposed commercial model is designed to give Crédit Agricole CIB three things procurement teams consistently ask for: price transparency, traceable assumptions, and credible scale economics. It does not rely on low headline pricing offset by hidden dependency costs. Instead, it separates what DALP delivers directly from what must be coordinated with the buyer's environment and third parties, making the commercial package easier to compare, negotiate, and govern.
