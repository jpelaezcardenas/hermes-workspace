## Executive Summary

Tabby's BNPL portfolio growth demands a funding infrastructure that scales faster than traditional warehouse facilities allow. Tokenized receivables provide the answer: broader investor access, faster capital deployment, and transparent pool performance that institutional funders require.

The commercial question is whether Tabby should build this infrastructure internally, assemble multiple vendors, or deploy a single platform. DALP provides the single-platform path: receivable pool structuring, tranche tokenization, investor compliance, waterfall distribution, and audit trail in one integrated system.

## Commercial Decision Framework

### Build vs. Buy

| Factor | Internal Build | DALP Platform |
|--------|---------------|---------------|
| Time to first pool | 18-24 months | 6 months |
| Specialized team required | 10+ engineers + compliance | Existing team + SettleMint |
| Dual-jurisdiction compliance | Manual, unproven | Configurable, production-tested |
| Investor onboarding speed | Weeks | Days |
| 5-year TCO | €5M-€8M | €2.1M-€2.8M |

### Funding Advantage

Tokenized receivables unlock a broader investor base than bilateral warehouse facilities:

- Warehouse facilities: 2-4 bank counterparties, 6-12 month approval cycles
- Tokenized receivables: 20+ institutional investors, configurable per pool, subscription in days

The diversification benefit alone reduces Tabby's counterparty concentration risk and provides pricing competition among funders.
