## Executive Summary

Sarwa needs a tokenized investment platform that can operate at retail scale under UAE regulatory supervision. The commercial decision is straightforward: building this internally would require 18+ months and a specialized engineering team that Sarwa does not have. Assembling multiple point solutions creates integration debt and a fragmented compliance posture that regulators will question.

DALP provides a single platform covering asset configuration, compliance enforcement, custody integration, settlement, and distribution automation. ## Commercial Decision Framework

### Build vs. Buy

| Factor | Internal Build | DALP Platform |
|--------|---------------|---------------|
| Time to first product | 18-24 months | 5 months (22-week implementation) |
| Engineering team required | 8-12 specialized engineers | Sarwa's existing team + SettleMint implementation |
| Compliance readiness | Unproven; requires SCA validation | Production-proven; audited modules |
| Product launch velocity | Weeks per product (custom development) | Days per product (configuration) |
| 5-year TCO | €4M-€6M | €2.1M-€2.5M |

### Revenue Opportunity Cost

Every month of delayed product launch represents lost revenue. Sarwa's existing investor base can begin accessing tokenized products within 22 weeks of contract signature with DALP. An internal build delays that revenue by 12-18 additional months.

At conservative assumptions (1,000 active investors in tokenized products generating €50/year in platform fees), a 12-month delay represents €50,000 in direct lost revenue and significantly more in missed customer acquisition opportunity.
