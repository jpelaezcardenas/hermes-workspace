# 1. Executive Summary

## 1.1 Commercial Recommendation

SettleMint recommends a commercial model for the Central Bank of Bahrain's digital asset regulatory platform based on private cloud deployment with enterprise support. The model provides the Central Bank with full control over infrastructure and data residency while leveraging DALP's pre-built capabilities to minimize implementation risk and time-to-value.

The recommended approach combines platform licensing, implementation services, and ongoing support into a structured commercial framework that provides transparency, predictability, and alignment with the Central Bank's procurement objectives.

## 1.3 Why This Commercial Model

The proposed model reflects SettleMint's experience with regulated institutions in the Middle East. # 2. Investment Rationale

## 2.1 The Cost of Current Approach

The Central Bank of Bahrain faces a decision about how to build digital asset regulatory infrastructure. The alternative approaches each carry distinct cost profiles and risk characteristics.

**Build Approach:** Developing a bespoke platform internally would require significant engineering investment, regulatory expertise, and ongoing operational capability. The cost of building compliant digital asset infrastructure from scratch typically ranges from USD 8-15 million for initial development, with annual operational costs of USD 2-4 million. More critically, build approaches carry execution risk: most financial institutions that attempt bespoke development experience significant delays, scope creep, and regulatory challenges that extend timelines by 12-24 months beyond initial estimates.

**Multi-Vendor Assembly:** Assembling a solution from multiple point vendors introduces integration complexity, vendor coordination overhead, and governance burden. Each vendor brings separate contractual relationships, support escalations, and technology roadmaps. The operational cost of managing a multi-vendor environment typically adds 30-50% to the base technology cost through integration work, reconciliation effort, and coordination overhead. The governance burden also slows decision-making, as changes requiring coordinated vendor response take weeks or months rather than days.

**Platform-Based Approach:** DALP provides a pre-built, production-tested platform that addresses the full digital asset lifecycle. The platform has been refined through 14 production deployments, including sovereign-scale programmes in the Gulf region. This approach reduces implementation risk, accelerates time-to-value, and provides a single point of accountability for platform operations.

## 2.2 How DALP Changes the Economics

DALP transforms the economics of digital asset regulatory infrastructure through three mechanisms:

**Configuration Over Development:** DALP provides pre-built asset templates, compliance modules, and operational workflows that would otherwise require custom development. A bond tokenization capability that might take 6-9 months to build bespoke is available through configuration in days. The compliance library includes templates for major regulatory frameworks, eliminating the need to build compliance logic from first principles. This compression of configuration time relative to development time directly reduces implementation investment.

**Shared Investment Recovery:** The DALP platform represents accumulated investment across multiple deployments and use cases. SettleMint's customers collectively benefit from the platform's maturity, as each deployment contributes to bug fixes, performance improvements, and capability enhancements that flow to all customers. This shared investment model means the Central Bank benefits from capabilities funded by previous deployments without bearing the full cost of their development.

**Operational Efficiency:** DALP's unified operational model eliminates the reconciliation overhead that characterises multi-vendor environments. The platform's on-chain compliance enforcement reduces manual review burden. The comprehensive audit trail reduces evidence-gathering costs for regulatory and internal audit review. These operational efficiencies compound over the platform's lifecycle, reducing the ongoing cost of operations relative to alternative approaches.

## 2.3 ROI Framework

The return on investment for DALP manifests across four value dimensions:

| Value Driver | Mechanism | Quantification Approach |
|--------------|-----------|-------------------------|
| Cost Avoidance | Avoided custom development, reduced integration effort | Compare implementation cost to build estimate |
| Operational Efficiency | Reduced reconciliation, automated compliance, streamlined audit | Measure operational staff hours saved |
| Risk Reduction | Production-tested platform, regulatory alignment, security certifications | Assess avoided regulatory and operational risk |
| Strategic Enablement | Faster time-to-market for digital asset initiatives | Evaluate opportunity cost of delayed capability |

SettleMint will work with the Central Bank during Phase 1 to develop specific ROI metrics aligned with the programme's objectives.

# 3. Proposed Commercial Model

## 3.1 Commercial Structure

SettleMint proposes a commercial model consisting of four components:

**Platform License:** DALP platform access under a term license. The license provides access to the DALP software, including the application layer, API services, middleware components, and smart contracts. License terms are annual with three-year commitment for pricing purposes.

**Implementation Services:** Professional services for programme delivery following SettleMint's 19-week methodology. Implementation includes discovery, configuration, integration, testing, and go-live support. Services are priced on a fixed-fee basis with defined deliverables and acceptance criteria.

**Environment Fees:** Monthly fees for the platform infrastructure. Environment fees cover the Kubernetes clusters, database, object storage, blockchain network, and observability stack required to operate DALP. Fees are per-environment (development, staging, production) and fixed monthly.

**Support Services:** Ongoing support services following the go-live transition. Support tiers define response times, escalation paths, and service scope. The Enterprise tier (24/7) is recommended for regulatory infrastructure.

## 3.2 Deployment Model: Private Cloud

The proposed commercial model is based on private cloud deployment within the Central Bank's specified cloud environment (AWS, Azure, or GCP). This deployment model provides:

**Data Residency:** All data remains within Bahrain. No data is transferred to external regions or third parties without explicit Central Bank approval.

**Infrastructure Control:** The Central Bank maintains control over infrastructure sizing, network configuration, and security policies. SettleMint provides platform management but does not have independent access to production infrastructure.

**Cost Transparency:** Environment fees are directly tied to infrastructure costs (compute, storage, network). The Central Bank can optimize costs through right-sizing and resource management.

**Regulatory Alignment:** Private cloud deployment aligns with CBB's data localisation requirements and regulatory expectations for supervised market infrastructure.

# 5. Implementation Investment

## 5.1 Phase-Based Investment

Implementation investment is structured across the five delivery phases:

| Phase | Duration | Key Deliverables | Investment Basis |
|-------|----------|-----------------|------------------|
| Phase 1: Discovery and Requirements | 2 weeks | Requirements document, architecture, roadmap | Fixed fee |
| Phase 2: Foundation and Setup | 3 weeks | Provisioned environments, network setup | Fixed fee |
| Phase 3: Configuration and Compliance | 4 weeks | Configured platform, compliance setup | Fixed fee |
| Phase 4: Integration and Testing | 4 weeks | Integrated system, test reports, UAT | Fixed fee |
| Phase 5: Go-Live and Hypercare | 6 weeks | Production deployment, knowledge transfer | Fixed fee |

## 5.2 Client Investment Contribution

Successful implementation requires active participation from the Central Bank's team. The following client-side investments are assumed:

**Personnel:** Project Manager, Technical Lead, DevOps/Infrastructure, Compliance/Risk resources available per the RACI matrix.

**Infrastructure:** Cloud account access, Kubernetes clusters, database instances, networking, DNS, TLS certificates. SettleMint will provide specific requirements during Phase 1.

**Integration:** API access, credentials, and test environments for core banking, custody, AML, and reporting systems.

**Decision-Making:** Timely responses to clarification requests, sign-off on configuration decisions, participation in gate reviews.

SettleMint will provide detailed responsibility definitions during the programme kick-off.

## 5.3 Investment Protection

SettleMint provides the following protections for the Central Bank's implementation investment:

**Deliverable Acceptance:** Each phase concludes with formal acceptance criteria and gate review. Payment is triggered by acceptance, not by time elapsed.

**Knowledge Transfer:** The implementation methodology includes explicit knowledge transfer during Phase 5 (Hypercare). By programme conclusion, the Central Bank's team will have operational capability to manage the platform independently.

**Documentation:** All implementation artefacts (configuration documentation, integration specifications, operational procedures, runbooks) are delivered as part of the implementation investment.
