# RFI Structure Template

Use this template for Request for Information documents. RFIs are lighter than full RFPs, focused on capability assessment and market sounding rather than binding vendor selection.

---

## 1. Cover Page

- Document title: "Request for Information: [Scope Description]"
- Issuing organization
- RFI reference number (format: `RFI-[ORG]-[YYYY]-[NNN]`)
- Issue date
- Response deadline
- Classification level
- Primary contact
- **Disclaimer:** "This RFI is issued for information-gathering purposes only. It does not constitute a commitment to issue an RFP, enter into a contract, or procure any services. Responses will not be scored or ranked."

## 2. Introduction and Purpose

### 2.1 About the Issuing Organization
- Brief organizational overview (3–5 sentences)
- Regulated status and primary jurisdictions

### 2.2 Purpose of This RFI
- What information the organization is seeking
- How responses will be used (inform RFP scope, assess market maturity, identify potential vendors)
- Explicit statement: non-binding, no commitment to proceed

### 2.3 Background and Context
- Business drivers and strategic context
- Current state and challenges
- High-level desired future state

## 3. Scope Overview

### 3.1 Areas of Interest
- Capability domains the organization wants to explore
- Asset classes, geographies, or use cases in scope
- Deployment model preferences (if any)

### 3.2 Out of Scope
- Areas explicitly excluded from this RFI

## 4. Information Requested

*Organize by domain. Each section contains open-ended but specific questions. Avoid yes/no, ask for descriptions with evidence.*

### 4.1 Company Overview
- Company background, ownership structure, and financial position
- Relevant market experience (financial services, digital assets, capital markets)
- Number and profile of clients in regulated financial institutions
- Geographic presence and support model
- Strategic direction and R&D investment

### 4.2 Solution Overview
- High-level solution architecture and technology stack
- Deployment options (SaaS, private cloud, on-premises, hybrid)
- Multi-tenancy approach
- Core capabilities and key differentiators
- Product roadmap highlights (next 12–24 months)

### 4.3 [Domain-Specific Questions: e.g., Digital Asset Capabilities]
- What asset types does the platform support? Provide specific examples of production deployments.
- Describe the asset lifecycle management capabilities (issuance, servicing, redemption).
- What blockchain networks/protocols are supported? How are new networks added?
- How does the platform handle interoperability between on-chain and off-chain systems?

### 4.4 [Domain-Specific Questions: e.g., Integration and Interoperability]
- What standard financial messaging formats are supported (ISO 20022, FIX, SWIFT)?
- Describe the API architecture and developer experience.
- How does the platform integrate with core banking systems, custodians, and market infrastructure?
- What data migration tools and services are available?

### 4.5 [Domain-Specific Questions: e.g., Security and Compliance]
- What security certifications does the organization hold?
- Describe the key management architecture.
- How does the platform support regulatory reporting and compliance monitoring?
- What AML/KYC capabilities are built in vs. integrated from third parties?

### 4.6 [Domain-Specific Questions: e.g., Operations and Support]
- Describe the SLA framework and uptime commitments.
- What is the typical implementation timeline for an organization of our profile?
- How is the platform monitored and operated? What visibility does the client have?
- Describe the support model (tiers, response times, escalation).

### 4.7 Commercial Model
- High-level pricing model (no detailed pricing required at RFI stage)
- Licensing approach (subscription, perpetual, usage-based, hybrid)
- Typical engagement structure (phases, duration, resource requirements)

## 5. Reference Information

### 5.1 Client References
- Provide 2–3 references from comparable organizations (financial institutions preferred)
- For each: organization type, geography, scope, go-live date, and scale

### 5.2 Case Studies
- Provide 1–2 published or redacted case studies demonstrating relevant capabilities

- Maximum page count: [15–25 pages recommended]

- Include an executive summary (max 1 page)
- File format: PDF
