# Buyer Persona: Well-Funded Fintech or Digital Asset Native

**Archetype:** A Series B+ fintech ($30M–$200M raised), licensed digital asset exchange, tokenization platform, or digital securities firm. Think Sygnum, Securitize, Backed Finance, or a well-funded regional player building digital asset infrastructure.

## Profile

A technology-native financial services company that already operates in the digital asset space or is entering it. Has strong engineering talent but needs to accelerate time-to-market rather than build everything in-house. Already holds or is applying for relevant licenses (MiCA, VARA, MAS). Moves fast, iterates frequently, and values developer experience over enterprise sales processes. Has a board that wants to see revenue growth, not 18-month implementation timelines.

## Priorities

1. **Speed to market**: needs to launch or expand within 2–4 months, not 12. The platform must be deployable quickly with minimal professional services overhead.
2. **API-first architecture**: everything must be programmable. REST/GraphQL APIs, webhooks, SDKs in multiple languages. The platform is infrastructure, not a destination, their product team builds the customer experience.
3. **Developer experience**: quality documentation, sandbox environments, clear error messages, active developer community. Their engineers will evaluate the platform before procurement signs off.
4. **Flexible pricing**: no $500K upfront license fees. Usage-based or transaction-based pricing that scales with their business. Free tier or sandbox for development.
5. **Composability**: they don't want a monolith. They want to use specific modules (tokenization engine, compliance module, custody integration) without buying the whole stack.

## Evaluation Culture

Engineering-led evaluation. CTO or VP Engineering makes the technical decision; CEO/CFO approves the commercial terms. Evaluation is fast (2–6 weeks). Proof of concept is a developer building something with the platform in a hackathon-style sprint, not a formal POC with governance. Documentation quality and API design are evaluated as seriously as feature lists. Reference checks happen on Twitter, GitHub, and developer forums, not just formal reference calls.

## Red Lines

- No platforms without comprehensive, public API documentation
- No vendors that require professional services for basic deployment
- No pricing models with large minimum commitments or long-term lock-in
- No solutions that can't be trialed independently (self-service sandbox required)
- No closed-source black boxes without API transparency

## Common RFP Patterns

Rarely issue formal RFPs. More likely to send a 5–10 page requirements document or a structured evaluation matrix. Evaluate 3–5 platforms simultaneously through hands-on testing. Decision within 4–8 weeks. May start with a paid pilot ($10K–$50K) before full commitment. Value transparent pricing published on the website over custom enterprise quotes.
