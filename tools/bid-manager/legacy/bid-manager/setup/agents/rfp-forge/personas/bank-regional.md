# Buyer Persona: Regional Bank

**Archetype:** Gulf-based bank (Mashreq, Emirates NBD digital arm), Southeast Asian bank (Kasikornbank, CIMB), African bank (Stanbic, Ecobank), or mid-tier European bank digitizing operations.

## Profile

A $10B–$100B asset bank operating in 1–5 countries, typically dominant in its home market. Has watched tier-1 peers move into digital assets and feels the pressure to follow, either from regulators introducing digital asset frameworks, corporate clients asking about tokenized instruments, or board-level mandates to modernize. Limited internal blockchain expertise. Current technology stack may include legacy core banking that needs modernization alongside the digital asset initiative.

## Priorities

1. **Digital transformation, not just digital assets**: the platform should leapfrog them, not just add a feature. They want to skip the mistakes tier-1 banks made.
2. **Cost efficiency**: cannot justify tier-1 pricing. Need predictable, scalable cost models. Per-transaction or usage-based pricing preferred over large upfront licenses.
3. **Speed to market**: first-mover advantage matters in their market. Want production deployment in 3–6 months, not 18 months.
4. **Regulatory alignment**: must support local regulatory framework (VARA, SAMA, BNM, SEC Thailand, etc.) with evidence of compliance, not just promises.
5. **Vendor hand-holding**: need implementation support, training, and knowledge transfer. They're building capability, not just buying software.

## Evaluation Culture

Smaller evaluation teams (5–8 people). CEO or Group Head may be directly involved. Decisions faster than tier-1 (3–6 months). More weight on demonstrations and proof-of-concept. Value vendor willingness to co-create and customize. Price sensitivity is high, will negotiate hard on commercial terms.

## Red Lines

- No solutions requiring large internal DevOps teams to operate
- No vendors without regional presence or timezone-aligned support
- No platforms that can't demonstrate the specific local regulatory framework
- No pricing models that create unpredictable cost growth

## Common RFP Patterns

30–40 page RFPs. Emphasis on use-case demonstrations over abstract requirements. Often include a "pilot phase" as part of the procurement process. Value references from similar-sized institutions in the same region. May issue an RFI first to learn the market before committing to a full RFP.
