## 1. The CML Framework

### Why Maturity Level Matters

Not all customers need the same thing. A central bank running a live CBDC pilot (CML 4) has radically different needs than a regional bank that just formed a "blockchain committee" (CML 1). Tailoring your proposal to the wrong maturity level is a common failure mode:

- **Proposal too advanced for CML 1-2:** Overwhelms with production details when they need education and vision
- **Proposal too basic for CML 4-5:** Feels condescending and signals you don't understand their sophistication
- **Pricing misaligned:** CML 1-2 needs low-commitment entry points; CML 4-5 needs enterprise scale pricing

### The Five Levels

```
CML 5: SCALING        ████████████████████ → Enterprise-wide, multi-asset, expanding
CML 4: OPERATING      ███████████████      → Live in production, expanding use cases
CML 3: IMPLEMENTING   ██████████           → Committed project, budget allocated
CML 2: EXPERIMENTING  █████                → POC/pilot, small team, exploring
CML 1: EXPLORING      ██                   → Researching, no POC yet, learning
```

### Maturity Distribution (Enterprise Financial Services, 2026)

Based on market observation, the approximate distribution of enterprise financial services customers:

| Level | Percentage | Trend |
|---|---|---|
| CML 1: Exploring | 25-30% | Shrinking (late majority entering) |
| CML 2: Experimenting | 30-35% | Largest segment (most active) |
| CML 3: Implementing | 20-25% | Growing rapidly |
| CML 4: Operating | 10-15% | Growing |
| CML 5: Scaling | 3-5% | Small but growing |

## 2. Level 1: Exploring

### Characteristics

**Where they are:**
- Formed a blockchain/DLT working group or committee
- Conducting market research and attending conferences
- Reading reports (WEF, BIS, national regulators)
- May have attended vendor demonstrations
- No formal project, no allocated budget, no POC
- Decision to proceed requires business case development

**Typical team:**
- 1-2 people assigned part-time to "explore blockchain"
- Often an innovation officer, strategy analyst, or CTO's delegate
- No dedicated blockchain developers or architects

**Budget status:**
- No specific project budget
- May have a small innovation budget (€10K-€50K) for exploration
- Formal budget requires business case approval

**Timeline:**
- No defined timeline for decision
- Could be 6-18 months before moving to CML 2

### Typical Questions They Ask

- "What is tokenization and how does it work?"
- "What are other banks/institutions doing?"
- "What regulations apply to our jurisdiction?"
- "What would a pilot look like?"
- "How much does it cost to get started?"
- "What's the business case for our institution?"
- "Is this technology mature enough for production?"

### What They Need from a Vendor

| Need | How to Deliver |
|---|---|
| Education | White papers, webinars, briefings on blockchain for their sector |
| Peer examples | Anonymized case studies showing what others have achieved |
| Business case support | ROI frameworks, TCO comparisons, market analysis |
| Low-risk exploration | Sandbox access, demo environments, no-commitment workshops |
| Regulatory clarity | Regulatory framework summaries for their jurisdiction |
| Vision | What the future looks like and why they should act now |

### Proposal Tone & Depth

- **Tone:** Educational, consultative, non-pushy
- **Depth:** High-level capability overview, not technical deep-dives
- **Length:** Short, 5-10 pages for an initial briefing document
- **Emphasis:** Vision, market context, peer activity, business case potential
- **Avoid:** Dense technical specifications, complex pricing models, pressure to commit

### Pricing Sensitivity

- Very price-sensitive because there's no budget
- Entry point must be very low or free: sandbox access, workshops, discovery sessions
- Frame as "investment in evaluation" not "platform purchase"
- Offer: Free sandbox, €5K-€15K discovery workshop, €15K-€50K business case development engagement

### Decision Speed

- Slow. Decisions require building internal consensus from scratch.
- Don't expect a buying decision within 6 months.
- Invest in relationship building, not closing.

### Key Risks

- They may never move beyond exploring (analysis paralysis)
- The internal champion may leave or lose organizational support
- Priority may shift to other digital transformation initiatives
- Regulation may slow down (reducing urgency)

### Engagement Strategy

1. Invite to webinars, roundtables, and industry events
2. Provide educational content (white papers, regulatory guides)
3. Offer a free sandbox or demo environment
4. Propose a low-commitment discovery workshop (1-2 days, €5K-€15K)
5. Help them build an internal business case (provides enormous value and builds trust)
6. Check in quarterly, don't over-pursue, but stay visible

## 3. Level 2: Experimenting

### Characteristics

**Where they are:**
- Running or planning a POC/pilot
- Small dedicated team (2-5 people)
- Limited budget allocated (typically from innovation budget, not production IT)
- Testing specific use cases with internal or sandbox environments
- Evaluating 2-4 vendors in parallel
- Success criteria for POC may be vague

**Typical team:**
- Innovation lead or digital assets head (part-time or full-time)
- 1-2 developers or architects assigned to evaluation
- Occasional input from compliance, security, operations
- No dedicated production support team yet

**Budget status:**
- POC/pilot budget: €20K-€200K
- Production budget not yet approved (depends on POC results)
- Budget owner is typically innovation or digital transformation team

**Timeline:**
- POC duration: 4-12 weeks typical
- Decision on production investment: 2-6 months after POC
- Total cycle to CML 3: 6-12 months

### Typical Questions They Ask

- "Can you show us a working demo for our specific use case?"
- "How long does a POC take?"
- "What does the POC scope look like?"
- "What makes your platform different from [competitor]?"
- "Can we evaluate this without a long-term commitment?"
- "What does production deployment look like if the POC succeeds?"
- "What infrastructure do we need?"

### What They Need from a Vendor

| Need | How to Deliver |
|---|---|
| Quick POC setup | Pre-configured sandbox, use case-specific templates |
| Technical support during evaluation | Dedicated technical resource for POC period |
| Clear POC success criteria | Proposed evaluation framework with measurable outcomes |
| Path to production | Documented journey from POC to production |
| Competitive differentiation | Clear comparison with alternatives they're evaluating |
| Internal sell support | Materials to help them present POC results to leadership |

### Proposal Tone & Depth

- **Tone:** Technical but accessible, confidence-building
- **Depth:** Medium, enough to run a meaningful POC, not full production specification
- **Length:** 10-20 pages for a POC proposal
- **Emphasis:** Speed, simplicity, demonstrable outcomes, path to production
- **Avoid:** Overwhelming with production-grade requirements, long-term pricing complexity

### Pricing Sensitivity

- Budget-conscious but willing to invest for credible POC
- POC pricing should be attractive (break-even or modest margin)
- Frame POC investment as risk reduction: "€30K POC vs. €500K wrong decision"
- Offer: POC packages (€20K-€75K), with clear scope and deliverables

### Decision Speed

- Moderate. POC decision can be made in 2-4 weeks.
- Post-POC production decision takes 2-6 months (needs broader organizational buy-in).
- POC results are the critical input for production investment decision.

### Key Risks

- POC may "succeed" but production investment gets deprioritized
- POC scope too ambitious → fails to deliver within timeline/budget
- Internal champion doesn't have enough authority to drive production decision
- Competitor offers a lower-cost or faster POC
- POC evaluated by team who doesn't represent production stakeholders

### Engagement Strategy

1. Propose a structured POC with clear success criteria and timeline
2. Assign a dedicated technical resource for the POC period
3. Define the POC → production path upfront (no ambiguity about what comes next)
4. Help them prepare a POC results presentation for leadership
5. Introduce production-relevant stakeholders (CISO, compliance) early to avoid post-POC surprise objections
6. Follow up within 1 week of POC completion with a production proposal

## 4. Level 3: Implementing

### Characteristics

**Where they are:**
- Committed to production deployment
- Budget allocated by business unit or IT leadership
- Project team assembled (5-15 people)
- Production timeline defined
- Vendor selected or in final selection
- Integration planning underway with existing systems

**Typical team:**
- Project manager or program lead (full-time)
- 2-5 developers/architects (full-time)
- Compliance officer engaged (part-time)
- Security team involved (part-time)
- Operations representative (part-time)
- Procurement actively engaged

**Budget status:**
- Defined project budget: €100K-€2M+
- Budget approved through formal investment process
- Multi-year commitment often required

**Timeline:**
- Implementation: 3-6 months
- Production go-live: defined target date
- Post-launch support: ongoing

### Typical Questions They Ask

- "What's the detailed implementation plan and timeline?"
- "How does this integrate with [specific system]?"
- "What are the production infrastructure requirements?"
- "What SLAs do you commit to?"
- "What's the training plan for our team?"
- "How do we handle regulatory approval?"
- "What's the go-live readiness checklist?"

### What They Need from a Vendor

| Need | How to Deliver |
|---|---|
| Detailed implementation methodology | Phase-gated project plan with milestones |
| Integration architecture | Specific integration design for their systems |
| Production SLAs | Formal SLA commitments with penalty clauses |
| Training program | Structured training for operations, development, and admin teams |
| Regulatory support | Guidance on regulatory submissions and approvals |
| Risk mitigation | Rollback plans, parallel running strategies, contingency planning |

### Proposal Tone & Depth

- **Tone:** Professional, detailed, committed
- **Depth:** Full depth, detailed technical specifications, integration architecture, project plan
- **Length:** 30-80 pages for a full proposal/RFP response
- **Emphasis:** Execution capability, timeline confidence, risk mitigation, support commitment
- **Avoid:** High-level vision (they're past that), vague commitments, missing details

### Pricing Sensitivity

- Price is important but subordinate to capability and risk
- Willing to pay premium for proven execution capability
- TCO matters more than initial cost
- Multi-year contracts preferred for predictability
- Budget is approved but may need re-approval if pricing exceeds estimates

### Decision Speed

- Fast for vendor selection (2-4 weeks if POC was done)
- Implementation decisions move at project pace
- Escalation capability is critical, blockers must be resolved quickly

### Key Risks

- Implementation delays erode stakeholder confidence
- Integration complexity exceeds estimates
- Regulatory approval takes longer than expected
- Team readiness gaps (learning curve)
- Scope creep from business stakeholders adding requirements mid-project

### Engagement Strategy

1. Provide a detailed implementation plan with weekly milestones
2. Assign a dedicated implementation team with named resources
3. Establish regular governance calls (weekly during active implementation)
4. Proactively identify and mitigate integration risks
5. Provide structured training with certification
6. Plan for go-live support (dedicated resources for first 2-4 weeks)

## 5. Level 4: Operating

### Characteristics

**Where they are:**
- Live in production with at least one asset class or use case
- Operational team managing day-to-day
- Dealing with real production challenges (performance, incidents, regulatory updates)
- Beginning to consider expansion to additional asset classes or use cases
- Moving from "project" to "program" mindset

**Typical team:**
- Dedicated blockchain operations team (3-10+ people)
- Architecture and engineering team (3-8 people)
- Compliance monitoring team
- Production support (often 24/7 for financial services)

**Budget status:**
- Operational budget (annualized platform costs, support, infrastructure)
- Expansion investment budget for additional use cases
- Budget owner shifted from innovation to business line P&L

**Timeline:**
- Ongoing operational relationship
- Expansion projects: 2-6 months per additional asset class
- Contract renewals: annual or multi-year

### Typical Questions They Ask

- "How do we optimize performance for our growing transaction volume?"
- "Can we add [new asset class] without major re-implementation?"
- "What's on your product roadmap that benefits us?"
- "How do we upgrade without downtime?"
- "What are other production clients doing that we should consider?"
- "Can we get advanced support / dedicated account team?"

### What They Need from a Vendor

| Need | How to Deliver |
|---|---|
| Reliable production support | Enterprise support with SLA enforcement |
| Platform evolution | Regular updates, new features, security patches |
| Expansion capability | Quick path to add new asset classes |
| Performance optimization | Ongoing tuning, capacity planning, monitoring |
| Strategic partnership | Joint roadmap influence, advisory board |
| Best practices sharing | Insights from other production deployments |

### Proposal Tone & Depth

- **Tone:** Partnership-oriented, strategic, collaborative
- **Depth:** Focused on specific expansion or optimization topics
- **Length:** Varies, expansion proposals 10-20 pages; strategic reviews 5-10 pages
- **Emphasis:** Partnership value, innovation pipeline, production maturity
- **Avoid:** Re-selling what they already bought; treating them like a new prospect

### Pricing Sensitivity

- Moderate. Understand the value and willing to pay for quality.
- Expansion pricing should reflect relationship value (loyalty pricing)
- Concerned about cost predictability as scale grows
- Volume discounts and enterprise agreements become relevant

### Decision Speed

- Fast for operational decisions (days to weeks)
- Moderate for expansion projects (1-3 months)
- Contract renewals should be smooth if relationship is healthy

### Key Risks

- Platform performance issues erode confidence
- Competitor offering catches up or surpasses current capabilities
- Key personnel changes (on either side) disrupt the relationship
- Budget pressure leads to platform cost reduction demands
- Regulatory changes require unplanned platform modifications

## 6. Level 5: Scaling

### Characteristics

**Where they are:**
- Multiple asset classes live in production
- Enterprise-wide adoption of blockchain/DLT infrastructure
- Blockchain/DLT is a core part of technology strategy, not an innovation experiment
- Driving industry initiatives, consortiums, or market infrastructure
- May be creating new business lines based on tokenized assets

**Typical team:**
- Dedicated blockchain center of excellence (10-30+ people)
- Full engineering, operations, compliance, and product teams
- Executive-level blockchain strategy ownership
- Contributing to industry standards and working groups

**Budget status:**
- Significant annual investment (€1M-€10M+ across platform, team, and operations)
- Strategic budget, not discretionary innovation budget
- Multi-year enterprise agreements

**Timeline:**
- Continuous deployment and expansion
- Strategic reviews quarterly
- Platform evolution drives business strategy

### Typical Questions They Ask

- "How do we scale to [10x/100x] current volumes?"
- "Can we white-label or embed the platform in products we sell to our clients?"
- "How do we interoperate with other institutions' DLT platforms?"
- "What's the governance model for multi-party networks we're building?"
- "How do we contribute to platform development?"
- "What strategic partnership models are available?"

### What They Need from a Vendor

| Need | How to Deliver |
|---|---|
| Enterprise scalability | Multi-instance, multi-region, horizontal scaling |
| Strategic partnership | Joint ventures, revenue sharing, co-development |
| White-label / embedding | Platform-as-infrastructure for their products |
| Interoperability | Cross-chain, cross-platform, cross-institution |
| Industry leadership | Joint thought leadership, standards bodies, conferences |
| Priority roadmap influence | Advisory board, dedicated product management liaison |

### Proposal Tone & Depth

- **Tone:** Strategic peer, co-creation, joint roadmap
- **Depth:** Strategic and architectural, focus on multi-year evolution
- **Length:** Strategic proposals 15-30 pages; joint roadmap documents
- **Emphasis:** Partnership, co-creation, mutual growth, industry leadership
- **Avoid:** Vendor-client dynamics; they want a strategic partner, not a supplier

### Pricing Sensitivity

- Low sensitivity to unit price; high sensitivity to value alignment
- Enterprise agreements with volume commitments and preferential terms
- May negotiate revenue sharing or success-based pricing
- Strategic investments in joint development may be on the table

## 7. Maturity Assessment Questionnaire

Use these questions during discovery to quickly assess a prospect's CML:

| # | Question | CML 1 | CML 2 | CML 3 | CML 4 | CML 5 |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Do you have a defined blockchain strategy? | No | Emerging | Yes | Yes, evolved | Enterprise-wide |
| 2 | Have you run a POC or pilot? | No | In progress / planned | Completed | Production | Multiple |
| 3 | Do you have dedicated blockchain staff? | No | 1-2 part-time | Team forming | Dedicated team | Center of excellence |
| 4 | Is there an approved budget for blockchain? | No | POC budget only | Project budget | Operational budget | Strategic budget |
| 5 | Are you live in production? | No | No | Planned | Yes, 1 use case | Yes, multiple |
| 6 | Which stakeholders are engaged? | Innovation only | Innovation + CTO | Cross-functional | All functions | Enterprise-wide + board |
| 7 | Have you evaluated vendors before? | No | Currently evaluating | Selected / finalizing | Vendor in place | Strategic partner |
| 8 | What's your decision timeline? | Undefined | 6-12 months | 3-6 months | Ongoing | Continuous |

**Scoring:** Majority of answers in one column indicates that CML level.

## 8. CML-Based Engagement Strategy

### Summary Decision Matrix

| Dimension | CML 1 | CML 2 | CML 3 | CML 4 | CML 5 |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Primary goal** | Educate | Prove | Win | Serve | Partner |
| **Engagement model** | Thought leadership | POC/technical eval | Full RFP response | Account management | Strategic partnership |
| **Content type** | White papers, workshops | POC proposals, demos | Full proposals, architecture | Optimization plans, expansion | Joint roadmap, co-creation |
| **Pricing approach** | Free / minimal | POC packages | Full commercial proposal | Operational contracts | Enterprise agreements |
| **Sales cycle** | 12-18 months | 6-12 months | 3-6 months | Ongoing | Multi-year |
| **Key persona** | Innovation lead | Digital Assets Head + CTO | Full buying committee | Operations + business leads | C-suite + strategy |
| **Success metric** | Build awareness | POC success | Contract win | Retention + expansion | Strategic revenue |
| **Investment level** | Low (nurture) | Moderate (compete) | High (win) | Medium (maintain) | High (strategic) |

### Common CML Transitions

**CML 1 → CML 2** (Trigger: Business case approved)
- Help them build the business case. This is the most valuable thing you can do at CML 1.
- Offer discovery workshops that produce a go/no-go recommendation.

**CML 2 → CML 3** (Trigger: POC success + production budget)
- POC must demonstrate clear business value, not just technical feasibility.
- Help them present POC results to the investment committee.

**CML 3 → CML 4** (Trigger: Production go-live)
- Ensure a smooth go-live experience. First impressions in production define the relationship.
- Proactive support, monitoring, and quick resolution of issues.

**CML 4 → CML 5** (Trigger: Additional use cases approved)
- Share insights from other production clients about expansion use cases.
- Offer expansion packages with streamlined evaluation (no full RFP for existing clients).
