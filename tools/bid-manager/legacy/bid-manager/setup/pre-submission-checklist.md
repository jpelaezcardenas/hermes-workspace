# Pre-Submission Self-Review Checklist

Use this checklist twice:
- **Section-level review** after drafting any individual section
- **Document-level review** after assembling the full proposal

This is a working checklist, not a teaching document.
If an item fails, fix it before handing the draft forward.

---

## Section-Level Checklist
Run this after drafting each section.

### 1) First-scan answer visibility
- [ ] Can a tired evaluator understand the answer from the heading, opening lines, and first visual pass?
- [ ] Are the core claim, fit, and proof cues visible without reading every paragraph?
- **See:** `reading-psychology.md`, `structure-patterns.md`

### 2) First paragraph answers directly
- [ ] Does the first paragraph answer the requirement explicitly rather than circling toward it?
- [ ] If the evaluator reads only paragraph one, do they get the substance, not just setup?
- **See:** `persuasion-framework.md`, `writing-quality-standards.md`

### 3) Claim-to-evidence distance
- [ ] Is every material claim followed by evidence, mechanism, or example within two sentences?
- [ ] Are there any unsupported adjectives, promises, or superiority claims left hanging?
- **See:** `persuasion-framework.md`, `writing-quality-standards.md`

### 4) Capability status is explicit
- [ ] Does the section clearly label whether each important capability is **native**, **configurable**, **partner-dependent**, or **roadmap**?
- [ ] Are boundaries stated plainly wherever full coverage does not exist?
- **See:** `persuasion-framework.md`, `defect-taxonomy.md`

### 5) All five evaluator types can survive it
- [ ] Would an executive, procurement reader, technical evaluator, delivery/risk reviewer, and legal/compliance reader each find what they need here?
- [ ] If one persona jumped straight into this section cold, would they be lost, unconvinced, or forced to infer too much?
- **See:** `reading-psychology.md`, `writing-quality-standards.md`, `structure-patterns.md`

### 6) Heading earns its place
- [ ] Is the heading a retrieval cue that tells the evaluator what answer lives here?
- [ ] Or is it wallpaper that sounds polished but says nothing useful?
- **See:** `structure-patterns.md`, `reading-psychology.md`

### 7) Differentiators are not being recycled lazily
- [ ] If a differentiator appears again, does it add new proof, context, risk reduction, or client relevance?
- [ ] Have I removed repeated value claims that merely echo earlier wording?
- **See:** `writing-quality-standards.md`, `persuasion-framework.md`

### 8) Paragraph architecture holds
- [ ] Do most paragraphs follow a clear arc: **topic -> evidence -> implication -> transition**?
- [ ] Are there paragraphs that drift, stack facts, or end without telling the evaluator why the point matters?
- **See:** `writing-quality-standards.md`, `persuasion-framework.md`

### 9) Format choice is doing real work
- [ ] Is this content in the best format: prose, bullets, table, callout, process diagram, or comparison view?
- [ ] Would a different format reduce evaluator effort or make scoring easier?
- **See:** `writing-quality-standards.md`, `structure-patterns.md`

### 10) Passive voice stays under control
- [ ] Is passive voice limited and used only where the actor is genuinely irrelevant?
- [ ] Does the section still sound accountable, specific, and operational?
- **See:** `writing-quality-standards.md`

### 11) Defect-taxonomy blockers are absent
- [ ] Have I checked for blocker-level defects: missing answer, thin answer, buried response, overclaim, vague capability language, unsupported assertion, internal leakage, or unsafe qualification?
- [ ] If a defect is present, is it fixed rather than cosmetically softened?
- **See:** `defect-taxonomy.md`

### 12) Ethos, logos, and pathos are all present
- [ ] **Ethos:** Does the section feel controlled, credible, and honest?
- [ ] **Logos:** Is the reasoning clear and evidence-led?
- [ ] **Pathos:** Does the evaluator feel the practical benefit, reduced risk, or confidence payoff?
- **See:** `persuasion-framework.md`, `reading-psychology.md`

### 13) Scoring implication is easy to infer
- [ ] Does the section make it easy for the evaluator to reward us without translating our answer into scoring language themselves?
- [ ] Is the client benefit or risk reduction stated clearly enough to support a higher mark?
- **See:** `persuasion-framework.md`, `reading-psychology.md`

### 14) Honesty survives pressure
- [ ] If this section were challenged line by line, would every statement hold up without retreat, reinterpretation, or excuse-making?
- [ ] Have I been precise about dependencies, assumptions, and limits?
- **See:** `defect-taxonomy.md`, `persuasion-framework.md`

## Document-Level Checklist
Run this after the full proposal is assembled.

### 1) The table of contents tells a story
- [ ] Can someone read the TOC alone and see a coherent case from problem understanding to fit, proof, delivery, control, and value?
- [ ] Does the section order reflect evaluator logic rather than author convenience?
- **See:** `structure-patterns.md`, `reading-psychology.md`

### 2) Primacy is used well
- [ ] Does the executive summary lead with the strongest, most defensible material?
- [ ] Are the best proof points placed early enough to shape later reading in our favor?
- **See:** `reading-psychology.md`, `persuasion-framework.md`

### 3) Win themes are reinforced, not copy-pasted
- [ ] Do the core win themes recur across the document with variation, development, and new proof?
- [ ] Have I removed verbatim repetition that makes the draft feel assembled from fragments?
- **See:** `persuasion-framework.md`, `writing-quality-standards.md`

### 4) Voice is consistent across sections
- [ ] Does the proposal sound like one disciplined team rather than six contributors stitched together?
- [ ] Are tone, claim strength, sentence rhythm, and qualification style consistent throughout?
- **See:** `writing-quality-standards.md`

### 5) Pacing varies intelligently
- [ ] Is there enough variation in density: prose, bullets, tables, diagrams, summary boxes, and transitions?
- [ ] Does the document avoid long stretches of uninterrupted heavy prose?
- **See:** `structure-patterns.md`, `reading-psychology.md`

### 6) Procurement can score efficiently
- [ ] Can a procurement or commercial evaluator find requirement answers, commitments, caveats, and evidence fast?
- [ ] Have we reduced hunting, interpretation, and cross-referencing work?
- **See:** `reading-psychology.md`, `structure-patterns.md`

### 7) Compliance matrix is complete and honest
- [ ] Does the compliance matrix fully cover the requirement set with no silent omissions?
- [ ] Are partial fits, assumptions, exclusions, and roadmap items stated honestly?
- **See:** `defect-taxonomy.md`, `persuasion-framework.md`

### 8) Section sequencing supports evaluator workflow
- [ ] Does each major section answer the next logical evaluator question?
- [ ] Are proof, controls, dependencies, and implementation details placed where readers expect them?
- **See:** `structure-patterns.md`, `reading-psychology.md`

### 9) Cross-section contradictions are gone
- [ ] Are timelines, scope statements, capability claims, terminology, and dependency descriptions consistent across the full document?
- [ ] Have I checked that one section does not quietly weaken another?
- **See:** `defect-taxonomy.md`, `writing-quality-standards.md`

### 10) The document is memorable after a gap
- [ ] Is there a clear "why us" that a committee member could still recall two hours later?
- [ ] Are the top three to five reasons to select us both distinct and easy to repeat?
- **See:** `reading-psychology.md`, `persuasion-framework.md`

### 11) Proof is distributed where evaluators need it
- [ ] Are case studies, examples, mechanisms, and delivery proof located near the claims they support, not dumped in one isolated proof section?
- [ ] Does the document earn trust progressively?
- **See:** `persuasion-framework.md`, `writing-quality-standards.md`

### 12) IP, security, and confidentiality defects are absent
- [ ] Have I removed internal file names, internal-only terms, draft comments, metadata leaks, code fragments, and confidential references that do not belong in submission material?
- [ ] Does every exported artifact look client-safe?
- **See:** `defect-taxonomy.md`

### 13) Boundary honesty holds at document scale
- [ ] Are native features, configuration options, partner dependencies, assumptions, and roadmap items described consistently across the document?
- [ ] Would a skeptical reader conclude we are candid rather than slippery?
- **See:** `defect-taxonomy.md`, `persuasion-framework.md`

### 14) Final recall and comparison test
- [ ] If an evaluator compares us against two competitors immediately after reading, do our strengths survive the comparison?
- [ ] If they skim again before scoring, are the strongest answers still easy to retrieve?
- **See:** `reading-psychology.md`, `structure-patterns.md`, `persuasion-framework.md`

## Mandatory Output Checks (ALL proposals, NO exceptions)

These items are non-negotiable. Every proposal must pass all three before delivery.

### Cover Page
- [ ] Cover page present as first page
- [ ] Client/prospect name populated (not placeholder)
- [ ] Proposal title populated (not placeholder)
- [ ] Date of submission populated (not placeholder)
- [ ] SettleMint company name and logo present
- [ ] Contact details populated (name, title, email, phone)
- [ ] No bracket placeholders remaining on cover page

### Table of Contents
- [ ] Table of contents present immediately after cover page
- [ ] TOC reflects all document sections (H1-H4)
- [ ] TOC is a Word field (right-click → Update Field works)

### DALP Screenshots
- [ ] Relevant DALP screenshots included (minimum 3 per proposal)
- [ ] Screenshots selected from `../shared/brand/dalp-screenshots/CATALOG.md`
- [ ] Each screenshot has a descriptive caption
- [ ] Screenshots placed after the text they illustrate
- [ ] Screenshots are relevant to the specific proposal topic

## Release Rule
Do not finalize a section or full proposal while any of the following remain true:
- the answer is hard to find
- claims outrun proof
- capability status is ambiguous
- blocker defects remain open
- the evaluator has to do interpretive labor we should have done for them

If in doubt, make the answer clearer, the proof closer, and the limits more explicit.
