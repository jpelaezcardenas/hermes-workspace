# Real Estate

## Problem Statement
Real-estate tokenization is rarely blocked by the token itself. It breaks on identity, ownership controls, approvals, registry alignment, and the operational burden of moving between property records, investor eligibility checks, and lifecycle events. Without those controls, teams end up with a demo instead of production infrastructure.

## DALP Solution
DALP supports real-estate assets as a governed asset class with controlled issuance, verified investor identity, transfer restrictions, custody and settlement integration, and audit-ready lifecycle management. It is suited to institutional and sovereign programs where ownership records, permissions, and operational evidence matter as much as the token design.

## Key Differentiators
- Real estate is a supported DALP asset class, not an ad hoc smart-contract experiment.
- DALP combines identity, compliance, custody coordination, and lifecycle operations on one control plane.
- The platform is credible for registry-linked and public-sector programs because it was built for regulated operating environments.
- DALP can integrate into external systems and registries rather than forcing the property process to live only on-chain.

### Problem Slide
- Property ownership flows involve registries, legal controls, eligibility checks, and operational approvals that most token stacks ignore
- Manual onboarding and transfer validation create friction for investors, operators, and public authorities
- Ownership evidence, servicing, and audit trails are often split across disconnected systems
- Real-estate initiatives fail when they stop at token issuance instead of designing the operating model around control and evidence

### Solution Slide
- **Pre-built real-estate asset class** with governed issuance and lifecycle orchestration
- **OnchainID-backed identity and compliance controls** enforce who can receive, hold, and transfer the asset
- **Custody and settlement integration points** support institutional operating boundaries and approval workflows
- **Audit-ready control plane** keeps asset state, permissions, and lifecycle events visible across the program

### Architecture Slide
- DALP models the real-estate instrument and issuance terms through its asset creation workflows
- Identity registry, claims, and transfer policies govern participant onboarding and transaction permissions
- Integration layers connect DALP to external registries, enterprise systems, or national infrastructure where required
- Settlement, servicing, and reporting stay on the same platform record instead of being reconstructed after the fact

### Benefits Slide
- Reduce manual transfer validation and ownership reconciliation through ex-ante policy enforcement
- Improve auditability for property-backed programs with one record of issuance, permissions, and lifecycle events
- Shorten launch time by reusing a supported asset class instead of building custom contracts and controls from scratch
- Support sovereign- or institutional-scale programs without changing the operating model when scope expands
